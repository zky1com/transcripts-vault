# Donald Trump - US policy should prioritize AI leadership over restrictive state-level regulation

## Position
Trump argues that US AI progress requires one federal standard instead of fragmented state-by-state rules.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript says the US should have a single federal AI standard, not 50 separate state regimes.
- He says the most restrictive state could effectively govern everyone if federal preemption is absent.
- He frames multi-state litigation and conflicting standards as barriers to competitive speed.

## Logic Chain
1. AI deployment needs regulatory predictability.
2. Fifty distinct state regimes create compliance drag and legal risk.
3. Therefore federal preemption is presented as necessary for US AI leadership.
