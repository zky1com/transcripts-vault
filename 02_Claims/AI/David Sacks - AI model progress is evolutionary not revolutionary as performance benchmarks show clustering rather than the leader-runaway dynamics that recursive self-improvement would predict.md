# David Sacks - AI model progress is evolutionary not revolutionary as performance benchmarks show clustering rather than the leader-runaway dynamics that recursive self-improvement would predict

## Position
The AGI-imminent narrative predicted that leading AI models would recursively improve, pulling irreversibly ahead of competitors. Instead, benchmark performance across the top labs is clustering — each model release shows incremental progress, not discontinuous jumps. GPT-5 underperformed its hyped 'Death Star' expectations, confirming that AI progress follows normal tech development trajectories, not exponential self-improvement loops.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "If we were on a path towards rapid takeoff, then what you would see is that the leading AI models would be increasing the distance between the top one or two models... And instead what we're seeing is a clustering of model performance."
- Transcript: "Chat GPT5 where a lot of people were expecting GPT5 to be this huge breakthrough. Sam Alman was sort of teasing this concept by posting photos of the Death Star... the reviews ended up being very mixed."
- Transcript: "It just fell short of these lofty expectations... it's not like the leading players just all of a sudden going to achieve AGI just very quickly."

## Logic Chain
1. Recursive self-improvement theory predicts that the leading model continuously accelerates, leaving all others behind.
2. Empirical benchmark data shows top models performing within similar ranges, not diverging.
3. GPT-5 was positioned as a transformative 'Death Star' breakthrough but received mixed performance reviews.
4. Incremental progress rather than discontinuous jumps is characteristic of normal technology development.
5. Therefore AI progress is a standard technology race, not a recursive self-improvement loop.
