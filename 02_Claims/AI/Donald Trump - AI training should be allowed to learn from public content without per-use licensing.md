# Donald Trump - AI training should be allowed to learn from public content without per-use licensing

## Position
Trump argues AI systems should be able to learn from written material without negotiating individual payment contracts for every source.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript says a successful AI program cannot require payment each time a model has read material.
- He distinguishes learning from reading versus copying or plagiarizing entire works.
- He ties this stance to competitiveness, saying rivals like China are not using heavy per-item licensing constraints.

## Logic Chain
1. Model training aggregates large volumes of prior knowledge.
2. Per-use licensing for each text source is operationally impractical at scale.
3. Thus he argues policy should permit broad learning use while still disallowing direct plagiarism.
