# David Sacks - A public code-red can be good management when it forces focus on a real business problem

## Position
David Sacks says Sam Altman's public code-red was a defensible management move because it prioritized business focus over PR comfort.

## Evidence Cited
- He gives Sam credit for "breaking the glass" and pulling the alarm.
- He says CEOs often prefer "happy talk" and avoiding bad PR.
- He says a CEO who accepts a bad PR story to focus troops on a real problem deserves credit.

## Logic Chain
1. Sacks contrasts PR management with operational focus.
2. He explicitly endorses the latter in this case.
3. Therefore the claim captures his view that a public code-red can be sound leadership.
