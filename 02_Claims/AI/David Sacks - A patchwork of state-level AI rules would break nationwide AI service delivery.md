# David Sacks - A patchwork of state-level AI rules would break nationwide AI service delivery

## Position
State-by-state AI regulation can create conflicting compliance obligations that make it impractical for national AI providers to serve users consistently across the U.S.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript discusses concern that different state rules could make it practically impossible for providers to service cross-state customers coherently.
- The panel references federal preemption pressure and criticism that state-rights framing does not fit distributed AI infrastructure.
- The policy argument is framed around preserving a unified national AI market rather than fragmented legal regimes.

## Logic Chain
1. AI services are delivered across state boundaries through shared infrastructure and model stacks.
2. Conflicting state requirements force either lowest-common-denominator service or withdrawal from some markets.
3. Thus regulatory fragmentation reduces service quality and slows deployment at national scale.
