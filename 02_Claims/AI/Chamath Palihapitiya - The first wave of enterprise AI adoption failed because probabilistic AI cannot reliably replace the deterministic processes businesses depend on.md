# Chamath Palihapitiya - The first wave of enterprise AI adoption failed because probabilistic AI cannot reliably replace the deterministic processes businesses depend on

## Position
The first wave of enterprise AI adoption was driven by board-level pressure rather than genuine bottom-up demand. Most implementations failed because LLMs produce probabilistic outputs that cannot meet the reliability standards required for production business processes. Sales and marketing AI failed at high rates; back-office process automation succeeded because those tasks tolerate less precision.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The first wave was just a lot of boards who read the words AI somewhere in an article and then went to a board meeting and turned to the CEO and said, 'What's your AI strategy?'"
- Transcript: "I think the biggest reason why you're seeing so many failure modes in sales and marketing is there's a big difference between probabilistic software and deterministic software."
- Transcript: "MIT found that 95% of GenAI pilots are failing to make it to production because of employee resistance, poor quality output."

## Logic Chain
1. Boards mandated AI adoption without rigorous evaluation of use-case fit.
2. Most early deployments targeted sales/marketing — high-variability domains with no tolerance for probabilistic errors.
3. Probabilistic LLM outputs cannot meet the accuracy standards required in deterministic business workflows.
4. The 95% failure rate reflects misalignment between AI capabilities and use-case requirements, not AI failure in general.
5. Correct use cases (back-office exception handling) succeed at higher rates.
