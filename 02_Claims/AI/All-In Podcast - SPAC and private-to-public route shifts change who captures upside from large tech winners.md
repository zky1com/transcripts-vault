# All-In Podcast - SPAC and private-to-public route shifts change who captures upside from large tech winners

## Position
When more large firms remain private longer or change listing routes, public-market participation in upside can narrow.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript includes explicit discussion of SPAC/public-market structure and retirement-account participation effects.
- Panel contrasts private concentration of gains with broad public access concerns.
- Conversation frames market-structure design as distributional issue.

## Logic Chain
1. Ownership route determines who can invest at key growth phases.
2. Delayed/public-limited access concentrates gains in private pools.
3. This can widen participation gaps in wealth creation.
