# Geoffrey Hinton - Humanity may soon face machine systems more intelligent than humans

## Position
Hinton argues we may be entering a period where machine systems exceed human intelligence, creating unprecedented strategic risk.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript includes Hinton saying humanity may have things more intelligent than us for the first time.
- He explicitly states belief that these systems can understand and are intelligent.
- Interview presents this as a near-to-medium-term concern, not distant speculation.

## Logic Chain
1. If systems exceed human cognitive ability broadly, human oversight becomes harder.
2. Harder oversight increases control and alignment risk in high-impact domains.
3. So superhuman-capability trajectories require urgent governance and safety preparation.
