# Jason Calacanis - Rich Sutton's Bitter Lesson shows general computation that scales with compute always outperforms hum

## Position
Rich Sutton's Bitter Lesson paper argues that AI approaches using general learning and search that scales with computation always beat approaches that encode human domain knowledge. Grok 4's competitive benchmark results are cited as the latest validation — Elon made an architectural bet on general computation at xAI (paralleling Tesla/SpaceX decisions) and it paid off.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "it basically says in a nutshell that you're always better off when you're trying to solve an AI problem taking a general learning approach that can scale with computation"
- Transcript: "whenever there was two competing approaches, one that used general computation and one that used human knowledge, the general computation problem always won"
- Transcript: "a fundamental architectural decision that Elon made... And it maps to an architectural decision he made at Tesla as well"
- Transcript: "these results show is a general computational approach that doesn't require as much human labeling can actually get to the answer and better answers faster"

## Logic Chain
1. Rich Sutton documented the pattern in 2019: compute-scaling approaches consistently beat human-knowledge approaches across chess, Go, speech, vision.
2. Elon's architectural choices at Tesla (software-first, scalable training) and now xAI follow this principle.
3. Grok 4's benchmark breakthrough vs competitors is the latest data point confirming the pattern.
4. This has large implications for companies making human-labeling bets (Meta/Scale AI, OpenAI RLHF approaches).
