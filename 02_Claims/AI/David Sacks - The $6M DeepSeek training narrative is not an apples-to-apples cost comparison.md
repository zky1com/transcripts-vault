# David Sacks - The $6M DeepSeek training narrative is not an apples-to-apples cost comparison

## Position
Claims that DeepSeek achieved frontier capability for $6M alone are framed as incomplete because they ignore full-stack compute and prior R&D.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript explicitly says $6M is discussed as final training run, not total program cost.
- The panel contrasts fully-loaded U.S. cost narratives with DeepSeek run-cost reporting.
- Conversation references large compute-cluster estimates beyond a single run.

## Logic Chain
1. Final-run cost excludes historical research and infrastructure.
2. Comparing final-run to fully-loaded totals misstates relative efficiency.
3. So the headline $6M claim should be interpreted cautiously.
