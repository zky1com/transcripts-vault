# David Friedberg - Hyperscaler AI chip depreciation extensions are justified by near-100% GPU utilization rates

## Position
Freeberg counters Michael Burry's claim that hyperscalers like Google and Meta are inflating earnings by extending GPU depreciation schedules from 3 to 6 years. He argues the extensions are technically valid because 7-to-8-year-old TPUs and GPUs are running at 100% utilization, and because data centers shifted from storage hardware (short lifecycles) to AI processors (longer lifecycles), legitimately justifying longer useful-life assumptions.

## Logic Chain
1. Legacy depreciation schedules reflected storage hardware with 2-4 year useful lives.
2. AI processors (TPUs, GPUs) have replaced storage hardware as the primary data center spend.
3. Google's internal data shows 7-8 year old chips at 100% utilization, proving longer useful lives.
4. Extending depreciation to match actual useful life is required under GAAP, not optional manipulation.
5. Burry's short thesis misunderstands the technical and accounting basis for the schedule change.
