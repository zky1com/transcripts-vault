# Chamath Palihapitiya - 95 percent of enterprise AI pilots fail to reach production due to resistance and poor ROI

## Position
An MIT study of 300 AI implementations across 52 companies found that 95% of generative AI pilots fail to reach production — driven by employee resistance, poor output quality, and resource misallocation, with 70% of GenAI budgets going to sales and marketing which has the worst ROI.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "MIT found that 95% of GenAI pilots are failing to make it to production because of employee resistance, poor quality output, and resource misallocation."
- Transcript: "70% of GenAI budgets go toward sales and marketing, which have poor ROI."
- Transcript: Cited as evidence of an "AI bubble" in enterprise adoption — hype outpacing real deployment.

## Logic Chain
1. Enterprise AI investment is heavily concentrated in high-hype, low-ROI applications (sales/marketing).
2. Most pilots fail before production due to organizational and technical challenges.
3. Therefore the current wave of AI enterprise spending is misallocated and will produce disappointment before eventual course-correction toward high-ROI use cases.
