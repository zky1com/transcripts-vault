# Travis Kalanick - AI agents will obsolete traditional consumer app-store distribution by replacing discrete app interfaces with conversational task execution

## Position
The dominant paradigm of downloading discrete apps to accomplish specific tasks is being replaced by AI agents that understand intent and execute across services without requiring users to navigate individual applications. This makes app-store distribution moats — built over decades — structurally irrelevant, threatening the existence of consumer software businesses that rely on direct app engagement.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Every consumer software CEO that has an app in the app store is tripping. They're tripping right now. I meet guys with real stuff and sometimes I'm doing like almost like therapy sessions with them."
- Transcript: "The paradigm shift is so profound that the idea that you would visit a web page goes away and you're just in a chat dial. You have an agent that's just taking care of your flights for you."
- Transcript: "You tell something, yo, I want to go to New York... Can you just go find something I'm probably going to like and give me a couple options?"

## Logic Chain
1. Current consumer software business model requires users to actively open specific apps to complete tasks.
2. AI agents can accept natural-language intent and execute tasks by interfacing with services directly.
3. When an agent can book a flight without the user opening a travel app, the travel app's user engagement collapses.
4. Consumer software businesses built on active engagement lose their core distribution advantage.
