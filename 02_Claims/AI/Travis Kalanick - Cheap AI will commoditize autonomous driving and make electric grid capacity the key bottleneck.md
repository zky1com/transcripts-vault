# Travis Kalanick - Cheap AI will commoditize autonomous driving and make electric grid capacity the key bottleneck

## Position
Travis Kalanick argues that as AI cost collapses, autonomy technology will be commoditized across multiple manufacturers simultaneously. At that point the binding constraint for large-scale AV fleet deployment will not be software or hardware, but electricity: converting California's vehicle miles entirely to EV ride-sharing would require roughly doubling the state's energy capacity, a 5-to-10-year infrastructure challenge.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "cheap AI makes cheap autonomy okay so if as cheap AI gets out there and proliferates... we should expect autonomy gets easier and easier."
- Transcript: "Tesla and FSD their new models are like I think in a three-month period he they went up like 10x in terms of performance."
- Transcript: "the Dark Horse that nobody's talking about which is it's called electricity... if all of the miles in California went EV ride sharing you would need to double the energy capacity."
- Transcript: "getting 20% more 10% more is going to be a gargantuan 5 to 10 year exercise."
- Transcript: Kalanick mentions living in LA with "power outages all the freaking time" citing current grid fragility as evidence.

## Logic Chain
1. Declining AI training costs reduce the software development barrier for autonomy.
2. Multiple manufacturers converging simultaneously (Waymo, Tesla, BYD) means AV becomes commoditized hardware.
3. All-EV AV fleets require massive charging infrastructure concentrated in urban areas.
4. California grid capacity doubling is a multi-year permitting and construction problem independent of technology.
5. Therefore electricity infrastructure, not AI or hardware, becomes the rate-limiting constraint for AV scale.
