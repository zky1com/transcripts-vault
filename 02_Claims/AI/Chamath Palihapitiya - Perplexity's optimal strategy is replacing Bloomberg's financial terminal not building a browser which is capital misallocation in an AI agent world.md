# Chamath Palihapitiya - Perplexity's optimal strategy is replacing Bloomberg's financial terminal not building a browser which is capital misallocation in an AI agent world

## Position
Bloomberg Terminal costs ~$25,000/year per user for financial data that Chamath characterizes as 'atrocious' and 'very limited.' Perplexity has demonstrated competency in financial data enrichment beyond pure LLM work. A focused vertical strategy targeting Bloomberg's ~100B market is attainable. Building a generic browser in 2025 is the wrong capital allocation because AI agents obsolete the browser paradigm entirely.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "I think their path to building a legacy business is to replace Bloomberg. As somebody who's paid $25,000 to Bloomberg for many years, the terminal is atrocious... anybody that could build a better product would take over a hundred billion dollar enterprise because I think it's there for the taking."
- Transcript: "A browser is like the dumbest thing to build in 2025 because in a world of agents, what is a browser? It's a glorified markup reader."
- Transcript: "In 2025, there's something that you just speak to... you type in get me a flight and it already knows what to do."

## Logic Chain
1. Bloomberg Terminal is a $100B+ enterprise with poor product quality, high pricing, and captive customers.
2. Perplexity has demonstrated capability in financial data beyond standard LLM functionality.
3. Vertical domination of financial data intelligence is an achievable and large-payoff opportunity.
4. Browsers process HTML/CSS for human visual consumption — irrelevant when AI agents interact with data directly.
5. Capital allocated to browser-building is capital not building defensible vertical data infrastructure.
