# David Sacks - AI development shows clustered model performance not recursive self-improvement takeoff

## Position
If AI were on a path toward rapid recursive self-improvement, leading models would be pulling away from competitors — but instead we see clustering of model performance and increasing specialization, which contradicts the "one model becomes all-knowing" takeoff scenario.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "If we were on a path towards rapid takeoff, the leading AI models would be increasing their distance from the rest. Instead we're seeing a clustering of model performance."
- Transcript: "The specialization really belies this idea of one model becoming all knowing."
- Transcript: Multiple competitive models performing at similar capability levels cited as evidence.

## Logic Chain
1. Recursive self-improvement would produce exponential divergence — one model dramatically outpacing all others.
2. Empirically, model performance benchmarks are converging/clustering rather than diverging.
3. Specialization across model families further contradicts the unified AGI takeoff hypothesis.
4. Therefore the current AI development trajectory is a normal technology race, not a recursive self-improvement event.
