# David Sacks - DeepSeek narrowed the China-US reasoning-model gap to roughly 3-6 months

## Position
DeepSeek's R1 release indicates China has moved closer to U.S. frontier reasoning models, compressing the perceived lag to roughly 3-6 months rather than 6-12 months.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript states that before R1, many in the field would have said China was 6-12 months behind, and after R1 that estimate shifted to around 3-6 months.
- The discussion explicitly compares OpenAI's earlier 01 release to R1 arriving about four months later.
- The panel frames the update as meaningful progress in the U.S.-China model race, not a trivial model copy event.

## Logic Chain
1. If a comparable reasoning model appears within a few months of the U.S. release, the effective frontier lag is smaller than prior assumptions.
2. R1 timing and quality are used as direct evidence that China's top labs are closer on frontier reasoning capability.
3. Therefore the competitive gap is better characterized as months, not nearly a year.
