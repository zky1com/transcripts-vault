# John Cassidy - Adam Smith criticized monopoly mercantile capitalism and defended competitive markets

## Position
John Cassidy argues that Adam Smith is often misread as a blanket defender of business, whereas Smith’s central critique targeted monopolistic mercantile systems and protection.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Cassidy describes Smith as critical of East India Company-style monopoly structures.
- Transcript: he contrasts that with Smith’s support for competition and free trade.

## Logic Chain
1. Smith’s target was market distortion, not all commerce.
2. Monopoly critique is foundational to his framework.
3. The claim captures Cassidy’s reinterpretation of Smith in capitalism debates.
