# Howard Lutnick - Country-specific tariffs were chosen because they are more precise than a universal tariff

## Position
Howard Lutnick says the administration chose country-specific tariffs over a universal tariff because a targeted structure was more nuanced and strategically tougher.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the president had to decide between a simple universal tariff and a country-by-country model.
- He calls the universal approach easier to execute but says they settled on the country-specific model.
- He describes the targeted version as more nuanced, clever, and difficult.

## Logic Chain
1. Lutnick presents the design choice as a tradeoff between simplicity and leverage.
2. He says the administration preferred the structure that created more negotiating precision.
3. That supports the claim that country-specific tariffs were chosen for strategic precision.
