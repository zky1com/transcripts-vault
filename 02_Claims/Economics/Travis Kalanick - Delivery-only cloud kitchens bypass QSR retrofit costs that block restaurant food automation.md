# Travis Kalanick - Delivery-only cloud kitchens bypass QSR retrofit costs that block restaurant food automation

## Position
Travis Kalanick explains that QSR chains attempting to automate in-store operations face prohibitive retrofit costs: existing kitchens are designed for human workflows, requiring demolition and multi-month closures to install automation machinery. Cloud Kitchens sidesteps this entirely by operating delivery-only facilities purpose-built for machines, eliminating the retrofit problem and enabling food automation at scale.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "when you go and put a machine like this in it changes the whole thing... you got to like do you've got to like if you're to replace the front line in Chipotle you got to demo it."
- Transcript: "the economics start to not work and by the way I still have to have humans in that brick and mortar."
- Transcript: "we're in that delivery only model so these are it's true infrastructure for making food behind the scenes for delivery so you don't have these issues."
- Transcript: "our infrastructure these kitchens are designed for these kinds of machines to be in them and vice versa."
- Transcript: Freeberg confirms the same pattern from his own early automated food company: "we were in the process of building out our first automated store when I actually took a medical leave of absence."

## Logic Chain
1. Existing QSR brick-and-mortar kitchens are optimized for human workers at every square inch.
2. Installing automation requires demolition, capital expenditure, and extended store downtime.
3. These costs make QSR in-place automation economically unviable for most operators.
4. Cloud Kitchens operates delivery-only facilities co-designed with automation machinery from the start.
5. The delivery-only model removes retrofit constraints and enables profitable food automation.
