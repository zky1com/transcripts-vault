# John Cassidy - Trump-style economic nationalism has political strength but faces internal economic contradictions

## Position
John Cassidy argues that nationalist managed-capitalism programs can be electorally potent, but broad high-tariff and hard labor-restriction agendas are difficult to sustain consistently in business-heavy coalitions.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Cassidy describes the political appeal of fortress-style economic messaging.
- Transcript: he also points to practical backtracking pressures around tariffs and labor-dependent sectors.

## Logic Chain
1. Political salience and economic coherence are separate tests.
2. Coalition constraints can force policy reversals.
3. The claim captures Cassidy’s distinction between rhetorical and operational durability.
