# David Friedberg - Social Security should become a visible defined-contribution system so people actually own and track the capital built from their labor

## Position
David Friedberg argues that Social Security should become a visible defined-contribution system so people actually own and track the capital built from their labor.

## Evidence Cited
- He says current Social Security effectively contains an IOU from the government rather than real invested assets.
- He says people should be able to see their money in named companies the same way a 401(k) owner can.
- He says moving from defined benefits to defined contributions is necessary alongside broader spending restraint.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg criticizes the current system for obscuring ownership and relying on government promises.
2. He presents visible capital accounts as a way to reconnect labor income with actual asset building.
3. That supports the claim that Social Security should become a visible defined-contribution system.
