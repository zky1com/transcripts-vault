# David Friedberg - Socialist political movements inevitably emerge in democracies when debt-trapped voters with negative capital have no capitalist path to recovery

## Position
When a majority of the voting population finds itself with negative capital and no viable path forward within the existing economic system, they rationally turn to socialist candidates who promise government redistribution — a pattern that has repeated across dozens of democracies and has never succeeded.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Where do you turn in that moment? You don't go turn to corporations... You turn to the voting booth and you hear a guy like Zoran show up and say, 'There can be a better path forward.'"
- Transcript: "This becomes a tipping point when the majority of the voter base ends up in that situation where they're that deeply in need where they have negative capital."
- Transcript: "In historical perspective, we have seen it time and time again across dozens of countries that have embraced socialism to get out of the debt cycle... It has never ever worked."

## Logic Chain
1. 32 million debt-trapped Americans have negative capital and no realistic economic future under the current system.
2. They concentrate in cities where political organization is easiest.
3. They rationally vote for candidates promising government relief, as this is their only remaining lever.
4. This process has repeated in dozens of democracies and has never produced the promised outcome.
