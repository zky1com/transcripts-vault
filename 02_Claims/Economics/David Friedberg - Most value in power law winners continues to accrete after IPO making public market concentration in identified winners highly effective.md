# David Friedberg - Most value in power law winners continues to accrete after IPO making public market concentration in identified winners highly effective

## Position
The conventional assumption that venture investors capture most of the value and public market investors get scraps is contradicted by data. Companies like Palantir (16B market cap at IPO → $436B in 5 years), Airbnb ($47B → $77B+), Uber ($75B → $190B), Spotify ($27B → $147B+) generated the majority of their total lifetime value as public companies. Identifying a power law winner matters more than catching it early.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Palanteer went public after 17 years and their market cap was 16 billion. Since they've been public in 5 years, they're now worth $436 billion. So they've created another $420 billion of equity market value in just the last 5 years."
- Transcript: "Uber went public 75 billion after 9 years as a private company and now they're worth 190 billion. So they've added 120 billion of value in just 6 years since being public."
- Transcript: "Facebook... eight years as a private company, 100 billion market cap at IPO added another two plus trillion dollars in the years since."

## Logic Chain
1. Power law compounders continue to compound after their IPO because the underlying business dynamics don't change.
2. Palantir: 97% of total lifetime value created post-IPO.
3. Facebook: ~95% of total lifetime value created post-IPO.
4. Therefore the public market offers nearly the same upside as private venture, with far better liquidity.
5. The implication: identifying power law winners is more important than getting in early.
