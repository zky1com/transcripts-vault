# David Sacks - A softer jobs report does not automatically mean recession because investment and rate cuts still support growth

## Position
David Sacks argues that a softer jobs report does not automatically mean recession because investment and rate cuts still support growth.

## Evidence Cited
- He says recession predictions are still weak relative to stronger market-based expectations.
- He points to large planned investment, the AI build-out, lower energy costs, and tax incentives as bullish factors.
- He says rate cuts should provide additional support even if the labor data softens.

## Logic Chain
1. A weak jobs print is only one input into the macro outlook.
2. Sacks says multiple forward-looking growth supports are still in place.
3. That is why he argues that softer jobs data does not automatically imply recession.
