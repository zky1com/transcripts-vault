# Ivy Zelman - Home-price performance divergence is primarily inventory-driven across local housing markets

## Position
Ivy Zelman argues that the wide gap in regional home-price performance is mostly explained by local inventory conditions rather than a single national demand trend.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Zelman contrasts low-inventory markets with strong pricing against higher-inventory markets with weaker pricing.
- Transcript: she ties buyer bargaining power directly to available supply and option set expansion.

## Logic Chain
1. Inventory determines market tightness.
2. Tightness governs pricing power and concessions.
3. The claim reflects Zelman’s supply-first framework for regional housing outcomes.
