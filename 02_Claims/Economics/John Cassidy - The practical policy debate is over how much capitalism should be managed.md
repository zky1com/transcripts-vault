# John Cassidy - The practical policy debate is over how much capitalism should be managed

## Position
John Cassidy contends that in current politics the core dispute is not whether to abolish markets, but how far to manage capitalism through regulation, fiscal policy, and institutional design.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Cassidy repeatedly frames the issue as the degree of management, not total replacement.
- Transcript: he places both left and right proposals within competing managed-capitalism models.

## Logic Chain
1. Extreme poles attract attention but understate policy reality.
2. Most workable agendas still use markets with differing controls.
3. The claim captures Cassidy’s central analytic framing for present-day policy.
