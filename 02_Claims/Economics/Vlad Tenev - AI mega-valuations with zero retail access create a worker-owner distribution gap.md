# Vlad Tenev - AI mega-valuations with zero retail access create a worker-owner distribution gap

## Position
Tenev argues that AI companies like OpenAI are accumulating hundred-billion-dollar valuations while remaining entirely privately held — meaning ordinary people have no economic exposure to the technology that could displace their jobs. He frames tokenizing private AI company equity as not just a financial product but a mechanism for distributing AI's economic gains to the people most affected by its disruption.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "these AI companies in particular are getting into valuations of hundreds and hundreds of billions with zero retail ownership. And that technology could completely disrupt how normal people live their lives"
- Transcript: "imagine the scenario if you know 20 to 30% of someone's net worth is in AI companies. Now, suddenly they're not fighting against this thing. They want it to succeed"
- Transcript: "entrepreneurship as a way to let more people participate in the boom"

## Logic Chain
1. Major AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) are private with no retail ownership pathway.
2. Their valuations are in the hundreds of billions — comparable to public companies where retail investors do participate.
3. If AI disrupts employment, workers who own no AI equity bear the costs with no offsetting gains.
4. Making AI company equity accessible via tokenization converts potential adversaries of AI into beneficiaries.
