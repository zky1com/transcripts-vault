# Howard Lutnick - Tariffs are meant to stop foreign producers from using trade surpluses to buy America

## Position
Howard Lutnick says tariffs are meant to counter a long-running pattern in which trading partners use surpluses to accumulate ownership claims on the United States.

## Evidence Cited
- He contrasts America's former net ownership position with the current claim that foreigners now own far more US assets than Americans own abroad.
- He argues that producer countries recycle dollar surpluses into American stocks, bonds, companies, and property.
- He says rebalancing trade is therefore also about rebalancing ownership.

## Logic Chain
1. Lutnick treats trade deficits as a capital-ownership problem rather than only a goods-pricing issue.
2. He says tariffs are a tool to interrupt that loop.
3. That supports the claim that tariffs are meant to stop foreign producers from buying more of America with trade surpluses.
