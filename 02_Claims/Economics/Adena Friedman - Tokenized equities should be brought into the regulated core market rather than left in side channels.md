# Adena Friedman - Tokenized equities should be brought into the regulated core market rather than left in side channels

## Position
Adena Friedman argues that tokenization should be integrated into regulated public markets so it improves the existing system instead of developing as a separate side sleeve.

## Evidence Cited
- She says equities should be tokenized and traded in the markets, not in a side sleeve.
- She connects tokenization to modernizing post-trade plumbing and reducing friction.
- She says regulatory convergence is what makes serious participation possible.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedman is not treating tokenization as a parallel rebel system.
2. She wants the benefits of tokenization inside the same investor-protection structure as existing markets.
3. That supports the claim that tokenized equities belong in the regulated core market.
