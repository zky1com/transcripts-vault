# Chamath Palihapitiya - Post-tariff stock market drop is mean reversion not a crash signal

## Position
The equity selloff following Trump's tariff announcement represents a healthy mean reversion from historically inflated forward multiples, not a crash — the market remains well above levels from 1-3 years prior.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The stock market is still way above where it was last year, 2 years ago, 3 years ago."
- Transcript: "What has happened is that the forward multiples have compressed. So that's number one. That's a fact."
- Transcript: "What we've seen for many years with trade imbalances, trade deficits, and close to zero interest rates... we have allowed the stock market to inflate past historical averages. What we've actually seen happen in the last week is what most people would call mean reversion."

## Logic Chain
1. Years of near-zero interest rates and trade deficit conditions inflated stock multiples above historical averages.
2. The post-tariff selloff compressed forward multiples back toward the mean.
3. Absolute price levels remain elevated vs. 1-3 years ago.
4. Therefore the selloff is normalization, not a signal of structural economic breakdown.
