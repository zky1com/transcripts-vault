# Cathie Wood - Government shutdown episodes are framed as periods where equities can remain resilient

## Position
The episode notes that past shutdown periods have often coincided with stable-to-positive equity behavior rather than immediate market collapse.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript opens by noting the US government is effectively shut for many functions.
- Cathie remarks that equity markets have often gone up during shutdown windows.
- The segment treats shutdown headlines as less mechanically bearish than media framing suggests.

## Logic Chain
1. Not all political-operational disruptions translate directly into earnings shocks.
2. Market reactions depend on expectations and liquidity, not headline intensity alone.
3. So shutdown periods can still produce resilient equity performance.
