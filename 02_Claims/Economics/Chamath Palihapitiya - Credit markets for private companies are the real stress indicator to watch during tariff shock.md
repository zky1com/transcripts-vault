# Chamath Palihapitiya - Credit markets for private companies are the real stress indicator to watch during tariff shock

## Position
While public equity and bond markets dominate headlines, the structural stress from tariffs will manifest most acutely and with least liquidity in credit markets for private companies — which is where investors need to focus their attention.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "What we do know though where the structural complexity of the market — and this is where Larry I agree with you — is acute and important to observe is in the credit markets for private companies and that is where you have to pay a lot of attention."
- Transcript: Chamath distinguishes between two markets (stock market and bond market) and within the bond market between public treasuries and private credit.

## Logic Chain
1. Public equity markets reflect sentiment and multiple compression.
2. Public bond markets can be distorted by single large actors or short-term technical flows.
3. Private credit markets have less liquidity and more fundamental exposure to real economic conditions.
4. Therefore stress in private credit signals true economic dislocation, not just market noise.
