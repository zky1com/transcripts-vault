# David Sacks - DOGE largest efficiency potential lies in forensic IT-spend auditing not headcount cuts

## Position
Chamath argues DOGE operates as a three-layer system where headcount reductions and facility consolidation are significant but ultimately dwarfed by a third layer: engineers accessing government systems of record to forensically trace where IT contracts and service spend actually flow. He believes that once this forensic audit is complete, total savings could exceed $2 trillion.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "doge is a three layer onion so layer one is the people we have now given a pretty generous offer to folks."
- Transcript: "the second as you guys just said the second layer of the onion is going to be the infrastructure all the buildings all the physical plants."
- Transcript: "both of them will pale in comparison to the third layer of this onion which is the it and the services and the spend."
- Transcript: "at the center and nucleus of every single one of these Doge teams is an engineer... they can get into these systems of record and start to trace where the money is going."
- Transcript: "I suspect to be honest it could be more than two trillion dollars when it's all said and done."

## Logic Chain
1. DOGE Layer 1 (people buyouts) yields near-term savings but is limited by workforce size.
2. DOGE Layer 2 (real-estate consolidation) adds more but facilities are a one-time harvest.
3. DOGE Layer 3 requires engineers to forensically audit IT systems and service contracts.
4. Government IT and services spending is opaque and not regularly subjected to private-sector efficiency review.
5. Chamath estimates IT forensics will dwarf other layers and could yield over $2 trillion total.
