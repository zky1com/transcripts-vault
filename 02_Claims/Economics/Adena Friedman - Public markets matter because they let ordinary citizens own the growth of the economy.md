# Adena Friedman - Public markets matter because they let ordinary citizens own the growth of the economy

## Position
Adena Friedman says public markets are economically important because they give broad populations the chance to own productive companies instead of keeping the gains only inside private capital circles.

## Evidence Cited
- She says public markets give every citizen a chance to become an owner in the economy.
- She contrasts that with companies staying private for too long.
- She highlights long-term index returns as proof that public ownership has mattered.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedman frames public listing as a distribution mechanism for economic upside.
2. She worries that excessive private-market duration excludes ordinary investors from that upside.
3. That supports the claim that public markets matter because they broaden ownership.
