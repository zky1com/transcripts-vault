# Adena Friedman - The burden of being public has become too different from being private and needs reform

## Position
Adena Friedman says going public has become too heavy a transition and argues that disclosure, proxy, and litigation reforms are needed to make public-company life more workable.

## Evidence Cited
- She says becoming public has turned into crossing the Rubicon for CEOs.
- She says the burden should not be so different from private-company life.
- She explicitly calls for disclosure, proxy, and litigation reform.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedman does not argue against disclosure in principle.
2. She argues the current burden exceeds what is necessary for sound investment decisions.
3. That supports the claim that the public-company burden needs reform.
