# David Friedberg - The US debt spiral becomes far more dangerous once higher Treasury yields force massive refinancing at much more expensive rates

## Position
David Friedberg argues that the US debt spiral becomes much more dangerous once higher Treasury yields force refinancing at materially more expensive rates.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the average federal borrowing cost was around 3.3 percent while the 30-year yield had moved to about 4.9 percent.
- He says refinancing the existing federal debt at those levels would add roughly $700 billion of annual interest cost.
- He says that additional debt service would consume a meaningful share of the federal budget and GDP.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg focuses on refinancing math rather than on the headline debt number alone.
2. If rollover rates reset much higher, debt service compounds and crowds out other spending.
3. That supports the claim that higher yields make the debt spiral much more dangerous.
