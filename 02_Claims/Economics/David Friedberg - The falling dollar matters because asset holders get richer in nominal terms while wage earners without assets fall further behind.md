# David Friedberg - The falling dollar matters because asset holders get richer in nominal terms while wage earners without assets fall further behind

## Position
David Friedberg argues that the falling dollar matters because asset holders get richer in nominal terms while wage earners without assets fall further behind.

## Evidence Cited
- He says as the dollar devalues and everything inflates, asset prices rise and asset owners become wealthier.
- He says most Americans do not own meaningful assets and therefore do not benefit from that repricing.
- He says this dynamic is what fuels populism, socialism, and social division.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg treats monetary debasement as a distributional event, not just a macro chart move.
2. Because asset ownership is uneven, inflationary asset repricing widens the gap between owners and wage earners.
3. That supports the claim that the falling dollar enriches asset holders while leaving non-owners behind.
