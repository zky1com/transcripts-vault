# Cathie Wood - Tariff revenue can narrow deficits but cannot by itself deliver fiscal normalization

## Position
Cathie says tariffs may improve the deficit trajectory, but broader growth and spending dynamics remain decisive for achieving fiscal targets.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript estimates tariff inflows are improving deficit arithmetic at a several-hundred-billion annualized pace.
- The same discussion still frames the deficit as far above the 3% of GDP target.
- Commentary emphasizes additional levers beyond tariffs, including spending discipline and growth effects.

## Logic Chain
1. Incremental revenue helps but does not close a large structural fiscal gap alone.
2. Deficit sustainability depends on both revenue and expenditure paths plus growth.
3. Therefore tariffs are a partial, not sufficient, fiscal solution.
