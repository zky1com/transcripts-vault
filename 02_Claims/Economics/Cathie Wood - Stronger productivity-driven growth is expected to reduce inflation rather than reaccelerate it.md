# Cathie Wood - Stronger productivity-driven growth is expected to reduce inflation rather than reaccelerate it

## Position
The episode argues growth fueled by productivity tends to be disinflationary, contradicting the common growth-equals-inflation narrative.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript states ARK's core view that accelerating real growth can coincide with lower inflation.
- Cathie references multi-decade historical periods where productivity gains suppressed inflation pressures.
- The discussion presents sticky recent inflation as challengeable by a coming productivity impulse.

## Logic Chain
1. Growth from demand surges can be inflationary, but growth from productivity expansion can be disinflationary.
2. If output per worker rises materially, unit costs decline.
3. Hence stronger real growth can occur with lower inflation.
