# Cathie Wood - Tesla's milestone-based CEO compensation is the right model for aligning executive incentives with shareholder outcomes

## Position
Elon Musk's milestone-based compensation package at Tesla — where he receives nothing unless specific performance targets are hit — is not just justified but is the model more CEOs should adopt, as it perfectly aligns executive motivation with long-term shareholder value creation.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "I wish more CEOs would do this. Elon's not going to be paid unless he reaches these milestones either."
- Transcript: "It's kind of an incentive to shoot for the stars, but do it in a very first principles way. Everything's physics-based and everything's milestone-based. And he's very disciplined."
- Transcript: ARK's 5-year Tesla price target is $2,600 (vs ~$330 at recording) and their 10-year model puts Tesla at $8.5 trillion market cap.
- Transcript: ARK notes humanoid robot value is largely absent from their model: "If he delivers on humanoid robots the way he thinks he is, we don't have enough in there."
- Transcript: On Delaware courts overriding shareholder-approved Musk compensation: "What business do they have overriding the shareholders of Tesla when it comes to a pay package?"

## Logic Chain
1. Traditional CEO compensation is often decoupled from company performance, creating misaligned incentives.
2. Milestone-based packages pay zero unless extraordinary outcomes are achieved.
3. This creates maximum motivation for the CEO to execute at the highest level.
4. Therefore milestone-based compensation is superior for alignment and should be more widely adopted.
