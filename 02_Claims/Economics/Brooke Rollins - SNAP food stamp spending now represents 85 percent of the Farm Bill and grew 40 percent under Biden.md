# Brooke Rollins - SNAP food stamp spending now represents 85 percent of the Farm Bill and grew 40 percent under Biden

## Position
What is nominally called the "Farm Bill" now allocates only 15% of its cost to actual farming programs while 85% goes to SNAP food stamps — and SNAP grew 40% under Biden deliberately to entrench the program and make it politically difficult to roll back.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The farmer part of the farm bill is 15%. The food stamp part is 85%."
- Transcript: "SNAP grew 40% under Biden. They did that on purpose — they knew it would be hard to roll that back."
- Transcript: Total Farm Bill cost is implicitly large given SNAP at $123B represents only 85% of it.

## Logic Chain
1. The Farm Bill's naming creates a political shield for entitlement expansion — cuts are characterized as "hurting farmers."
2. Democrats deliberately expanded SNAP 40% under Biden knowing political entrenchment would follow.
3. The 85/15 split means agricultural policy is actually food entitlement policy in disguise.
4. Therefore reforming the Farm Bill is largely a fight about entitlement spending, not agricultural economics.
