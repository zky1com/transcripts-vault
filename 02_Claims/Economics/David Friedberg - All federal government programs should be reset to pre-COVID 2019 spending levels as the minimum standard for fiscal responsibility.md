# David Friedberg - All federal government programs should be reset to pre-COVID 2019 spending levels as the minimum standard for fiscal responsibility

## Position
COVID-era emergency spending created a permanent step-change in government program costs that was never unwound. SNAP food assistance doubled from $60B to $120B/year. Across all programs, costs remain far above 2019 levels. Friedberg argues that resetting to 2019 baselines is the minimum responsible action — not a cut from today's levels, but a return to pre-emergency spending that was already sustainable.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "My guiding principle number one would be that any program that we intend to continue to persist have its budget level cut to pre-COVID to 2019 levels. If we did that we would be in a much better fiscal situation."
- Transcript: "The SNAP program... has absolutely exploded in size from 60 billion a year in 2019 to 120 billion a year today. In this budget proposal, they're actually cutting it back by about 30 billion. So, to 90 billion. So, it's still 50% higher than it was pre-COVID."
- Transcript: "All existing programs got to go back to pre-COVID levels."

## Logic Chain
1. 2019 spending levels supported a functioning government before the COVID emergency.
2. COVID triggered emergency spending increases that were never legislatively reversed.
3. These increases persist as baselines today, far above pre-emergency levels.
4. Returning programs to 2019 levels is not a radical cut — it is restoring the pre-emergency normal.
5. Any bill that doesn't achieve this minimum standard cannot be called fiscal reform.
