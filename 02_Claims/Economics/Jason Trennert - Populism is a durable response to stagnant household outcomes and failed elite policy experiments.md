# Jason Trennert - Populism is a durable response to stagnant household outcomes and failed elite policy experiments

## Position
Jason Trennert argues that modern populism reflects prolonged frustration with policies that benefited elites while leaving many households with weak real economic progress.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Trennert ties populism to dissatisfaction with long-running social and economic policy experiments.
- Transcript: he frames trade, immigration, and cost-of-living pressures as central drivers of political backlash.

## Logic Chain
1. Long-run distributional dissatisfaction builds political demand for disruption.
2. Disruption-oriented politics alters macro policy priorities.
3. The claim captures Trennert’s structural account of populist persistence.
