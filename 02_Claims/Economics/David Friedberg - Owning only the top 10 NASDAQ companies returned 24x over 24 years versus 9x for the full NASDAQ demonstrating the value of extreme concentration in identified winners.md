# David Friedberg - Owning only the top 10 NASDAQ companies returned 24x over 24 years versus 9x for the full NASDAQ demonstrating the value of extreme concentration in identified winners

## Position
Public market data confirms the power law thesis: from roughly 1999 to 2023, an investor who held only the top 10 NASDAQ companies would have earned a 24x multiple, compared to 9x for the full NASDAQ index. The concentrated bet on identified winners massively outperformed market-cap-weighted index exposure, suggesting that returns in public markets are also heavily concentrated.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "If you buy just the top 10 companies in the NASDAQ and you just held it, you would have made a 24x multiple over a 24-year period versus if you had just bought the NASDAQ. So just owning the best companies in the NASDAQ is by far the best way to drive multiple of return."
- Transcript: "You made a 9x multiple just by owning the top 10 companies in the NASDAQ."
- Transcript: "The job of investing is to find the power law winners. It is not to buy the index. If you buy the index, you are losing to the market."

## Logic Chain
1. Top 10 NASDAQ companies returned 24x vs 9x for the full index over 24 years.
2. The ~160% outperformance came purely from identifying the top-tier compounders vs buying the whole market.
3. This is consistent with the power law thesis: a small number of winners generate most returns.
4. Practical implication: the value of an investor is in identifying winners, not managing diversification.
