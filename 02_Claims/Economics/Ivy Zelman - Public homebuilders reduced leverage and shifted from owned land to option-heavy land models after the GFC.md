# Ivy Zelman - Public homebuilders reduced leverage and shifted from owned land to option-heavy land models after the GFC

## Position
Ivy Zelman says public homebuilders are structurally different from pre-crisis peers, with far lower leverage and much greater use of land options instead of carrying large owned land banks.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Zelman contrasts prior high debt-to-capital and large land ownership with current low leverage.
- Transcript: she explains how option structures defer land takedown until vertical construction timing is clearer.

## Logic Chain
1. Lower balance-sheet leverage reduces insolvency risk.
2. Option-heavy land strategy reduces capital intensity and inventory risk.
3. The claim captures Zelman’s post-GFC risk-model shift thesis.
