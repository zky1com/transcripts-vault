# Tom Emmer - Republicans need to balance the budget before they can credibly tackle long-term entitlement reform

## Position
Tom Emmer argues that Republicans need to balance the budget before they can credibly tackle long-term entitlement reform.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the first step is to stop annual deficits of roughly 1.5 to 2.2 trillion dollars.
- He says only after performing on core promises can Republicans build credibility with the public for harder reforms.
- He says promised Social Security benefits must be honored while future reforms are debated.

## Logic Chain
1. Voters will not trust major entitlement reforms from a party that has not first shown fiscal discipline.
2. Emmer says balancing the budget and restoring credibility must come first.
3. That is why he frames budget balance as the prerequisite for credible long-term reform.
