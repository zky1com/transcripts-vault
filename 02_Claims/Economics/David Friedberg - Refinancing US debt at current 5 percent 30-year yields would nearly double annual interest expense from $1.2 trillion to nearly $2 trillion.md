# David Friedberg - Refinancing US debt at current 5 percent 30-year yields would nearly double annual interest expense from $1.2 trillion to nearly $2 trillion

## Position
The US government's existing $36 trillion in debt carries an average interest rate of 3.3%, costing $1.2 trillion annually. As this debt matures and needs to be refinanced at the current 5% market rate, interest expense will climb toward $2 trillion per year — and this number compounds upward because the US continues to run deficits, growing the total debt load each year.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We have 36 trillion of debt. The average interest rate we're paying on that debt today is 3.3%... we have a run rate interest expense of $1.2 trillion a year."
- Transcript: "If this spikes up to 5% from 3.3, we're talking about nearly $2 trillion a year in interest expense."
- Transcript: "That number is only going to get bigger as we borrow more money each year and the loan balance goes up... because we are still running a deficit."

## Logic Chain
1. US has $36T in debt at average 3.3% cost = $1.2T annual interest expense.
2. Current 30-year Treasury yields are 5% — the market rate for new US borrowing.
3. As existing bonds mature, they must be refinanced at ~5% instead of lower historical rates.
4. At 5% average rate on $36T, interest expense would be ~$1.8-2T annually.
5. Continued deficit spending grows the total debt load, making each year's interest burden larger than the last.
