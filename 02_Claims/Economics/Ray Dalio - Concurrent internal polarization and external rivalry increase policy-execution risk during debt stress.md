# 
Ray Dalio - Concurrent internal polarization and external rivalry increase policy-execution risk during debt stress

## Position
The interview argues that domestic political fragmentation and geopolitical competition together reduce coordination capacity when debt adjustment demands difficult tradeoffs.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript describes simultaneous internal conflict and international conflict pressures.
- Dalio says cooperation in solving hard fiscal problems is likely to weaken as stress rises.
- He links this environment to higher execution risk for corrective policy pathways.

## Logic Chain
1. Debt stabilization requires sustained multi-actor coordination.
2. Polarization and external conflict reduce willingness to absorb shared short-term pain.
3. So governance fragmentation raises probability of delayed or inconsistent adjustment.
