# Cathie Wood - Five converging innovation platforms will accelerate real GDP growth from 3% to over 7% annually

## Position
ARK believes five simultaneously evolving innovation platforms — robotics, energy storage, AI, blockchain, and multi-omic sequencing — are converging in ways that will more than double real GDP growth from its 125-year average of ~3% to 7%+ per year, potentially conservative.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We believe that the productivity uplift here is going to be so strong during the next 5 to 10 years... that real GDP growth will accelerate from that 3% where it has been for the last 125 years towards 7% plus. And we think that could be conservative."
- Transcript: "For the first time in 125 years this time there are five platforms not three major platforms and they involve 15 different technologies."
- Transcript: Historical analog: late 1800s/early 1900s three-platform era took real GDP growth from 0.6% to 3% — a 5-fold increase. Current era should deliver at minimum a 2x increase.
- Transcript: Convergence example — autonomous mobility converges robotics, energy storage, and AI: "One S-curve feeding another S-curve feeding another S-curve."

## Logic Chain
1. In the late 1800s, three major platforms (telephone, electricity, combustion engine) lifted GDP from 0.6% to 3% sustained growth for 125 years.
2. Today, five platforms (not three) with 15 underlying technologies are converging simultaneously.
3. Technology cross-pollination multiplies the productivity uplift beyond each platform's individual contribution.
4. Therefore a more-than-doubling of the current 3% growth rate to 7%+ is ARK's base expectation.
