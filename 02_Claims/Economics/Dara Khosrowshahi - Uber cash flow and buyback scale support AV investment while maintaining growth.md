# Dara Khosrowshahi - Uber cash flow and buyback scale support AV investment while maintaining growth

## Position
Uber's business model has become highly cash generative: $8.5B+ in cash flow over the preceding 12 months, with top-line revenue growing 18% and operating earnings growing 35%, allowing management to announce a $20B stock buyback while simultaneously committing capital to AV partnerships and fleet investments.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "in the past 12 months, we've had over 8.5 billion of cash flow. the business is growing... top line 18% bottom line 35%"
- Transcript: "we announced a $20 billion buyback"
- Transcript: "It's not either or. We can walk and chew gum at the same time... we've got enough capital to be super aggressive there appropriately and at the same time buy back our stock"

## Logic Chain
1. Uber's unit economics have scaled to produce $8.5B+ annual cash flow.
2. Revenue growing 18% YoY means the cash flow base continues expanding.
3. Bottom-line growing at 35% (faster than top line) indicates improving margins.
4. This cash generation enables simultaneous AV investment and capital return — resolving the false trade-off narrative from earlier Uber years when the company was burning cash.
