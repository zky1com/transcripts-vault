# Chamath Palihapitiya - The BLS jobs data is too unreliable to guide policy because revisions are too large

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that the BLS jobs data is too unreliable to guide policy because revisions are too large.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the same jobs report can support multiple conflicting narratives because the underlying data is too brittle.
- He points to revisions large enough to swing the truth by hundreds of thousands of jobs.
- He argues policymakers cannot react intelligently when the initial readings keep changing so dramatically.

## Logic Chain
1. Policy decisions require measurements that are stable enough to trust.
2. Chamath says the jobs numbers are revised so heavily that the first prints are not decision-grade data.
3. That supports his claim that the current BLS jobs data is too unreliable to guide policy.
