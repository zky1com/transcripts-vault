# David Sacks - U.S.-allied states may seek non-U.S. AI stacks if U.S. policy constraints block practical access

## Position
If U.S. policy limits practical access paths, partner countries may adopt alternative AI ecosystems to meet immediate needs.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript references concern that allies in strategic competition need workable U.S.-aligned options.
- Panel warns that insufficient access can push partners toward China-linked systems.
- Discussion frames this as geopolitical as well as technical risk.

## Logic Chain
1. Partners prioritize available capability for domestic goals.
2. Unavailable aligned options increase substitution toward alternatives.
3. That substitution can weaken long-term U.S. ecosystem influence.
