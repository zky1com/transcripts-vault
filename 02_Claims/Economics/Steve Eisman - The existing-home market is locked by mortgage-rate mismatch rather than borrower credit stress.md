# Steve Eisman - The existing-home market is locked by mortgage-rate mismatch rather than borrower credit stress

## Position
Steve Eisman says existing-home turnover is suppressed because owners locked in low mortgage rates and are unwilling to swap into much higher-rate loans.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: he says many owners refinanced around 3% during COVID while new buyers face roughly 7% rates.
- Transcript: he argues equivalent-payment math would require unrealistic house-price cuts, so owners do not sell.

## Logic Chain
1. Eisman emphasizes payment shock as the dominant friction.
2. He links low mobility to homeowner incentive structure under rate divergence.
3. The claim captures rate-lock-induced market freeze.
