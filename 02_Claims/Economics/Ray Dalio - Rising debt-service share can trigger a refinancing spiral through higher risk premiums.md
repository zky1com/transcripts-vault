# 
Ray Dalio - Rising debt-service share can trigger a refinancing spiral through higher risk premiums

## Position
As debt service absorbs larger budget share, investors demand higher yields, which can intensify borrowing pressure in a self-reinforcing cycle.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript describes a debt spiral where borrowing to service debt worsens credit perception.
- Dalio says higher perceived risk pushes rates up, which worsens conditions for heavily indebted issuers.
- He identifies this mechanism as a core warning phase in debt-cycle deterioration.

## Logic Chain
1. Higher leverage raises sensitivity to borrowing costs.
2. Risk repricing increases future service burden and rollover stress.
3. So debt-service growth can become a nonlinear destabilizer through yield feedback loops.
