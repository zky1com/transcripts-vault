# David Friedberg - Power law distributions in free markets mean a small number of compounding winners generate the vast majority of total value creation

## Position
In free markets, value accretes through compounding: better products accumulate market share faster, making their lead increasingly insurmountable over time. This creates a power law distribution where a small number of winners capture the vast majority of total market value — mirroring the fat-tail distribution seen in venture capital returns.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "In free markets, value creation creates a power law distribution which means that a few of the many account for the vast majority of the capital appreciation."
- Transcript: "If you build a better engine of technology, a better business engine, you will compound and that means that over time you will accumulate more and more of the market in an outsized way. As you accumulate more, you actually move faster... eventually it becomes a runaway flywheel. No one can catch up."
- Transcript: "45% of [a major venture fund's] capital went into flat or down rounds and they had a negative net return on that allocation."

## Logic Chain
1. Companies with superior products compound market share because customers prefer them.
2. As market share increases, the company gains data, brand, and economies of scale advantages.
3. These advantages accelerate further market share gains — the flywheel effect.
4. The result is a power law: a few companies absorb nearly all value, most capture nearly none.
5. This pattern holds from individual companies to venture fund portfolios.
