# Cathie Wood - The Fed is balancing sticky tariff inflation against a weakening labor backdrop

## Position
Cathie describes policy tension between inflation readings that remain elevated and labor indicators that are increasingly weak.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript says the Fed cut rates by 25 bps while inflation appears stuck around roughly 2.5% to 3%.
- Tariff pass-through is cited as one reason inflation has not fallen faster.
- At the same time, employment data is characterized as deteriorating.

## Logic Chain
1. Persistent inflation pressures constrain aggressive easing.
2. Labor weakness raises pressure to avoid overtight policy.
3. This creates a difficult policy tradeoff for the Fed.
