# David Friedberg - Replacing Jerome Powell would not solve the US fiscal crisis because the binding constraint is long-end Treasury yields not the Fed Funds rate

## Position
The Fed can only control the short end of the yield curve. The 30-year Treasury yield is set by market participants who assess long-run US solvency. No Fed Chair change can force the market to accept a lower 30-year yield. The real fiscal problem — a $36 trillion debt that needs to be refinanced at 5% rates — requires Congressional spending discipline, not a Fed personnel change.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "I'm not sure like the firing of Jerome Pal necessarily solves the US fiscal challenge which is rising interest rates on the long end of the Treasury curve."
- Transcript: "The 30-year Treasury yield over time... is the highest it's been since going all the way back to 2007 as of today."
- Transcript: "Making adjustments to the short end of the Treasury curve... will stimulate the economy... So the theory is that if we can drive rates down on the short end... But there comes a point where you're only going to be able to move the market so much."

## Logic Chain
1. The Fed controls only the overnight rate (short end of the curve).
2. Long-term Treasury yields are determined by bond market participants based on fiscal sustainability assessment.
3. The 30-year yield at 5% reflects market concern about US government solvency over a 30-year horizon.
4. No Fed Chair can force bond markets to accept lower long-term yields unless fiscal conditions improve.
5. Therefore the real constraint is Congressional fiscal policy, not the Fed Funds rate.
