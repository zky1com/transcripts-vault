# Howard Lutnick - Government should be judged by outcomes rather than by how hard officials say they worked

## Position
Howard Lutnick says government should be judged by outcomes, not by process stories about how hard officials worked.

## Evidence Cited
- He says if he worked hard and failed, that is still failure.
- He says if he barely worked and still got the right outcome, that is still success.
- He uses that framework to explain how he approached the Commerce Department.

## Logic Chain
1. Lutnick explicitly rejects effort as the standard for public success.
2. He centers measurement on whether the intended result was achieved.
3. That supports the claim that he judges government primarily by outcomes.
