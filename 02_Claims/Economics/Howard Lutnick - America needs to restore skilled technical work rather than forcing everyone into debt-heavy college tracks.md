# Howard Lutnick - America needs to restore skilled technical work rather than forcing everyone into debt-heavy college tracks

## Position
Howard Lutnick argues that America needs to rebuild high-value technical and manufacturing work instead of pushing everyone into expensive college pathways.

## Evidence Cited
- He criticizes the loss of shop-class style pathways and says too many people were told they had to take on debt for liberal-arts degrees.
- He says a stronger industrial economy creates better paid work for technicians, trades, and manufacturing roles.
- He frames that as part of restoring the American dream for people who work with their hands.

## Logic Chain
1. Lutnick links social mobility to dignified, well-paid technical work.
2. He argues the country weakened itself by narrowing status and opportunity around college debt tracks.
3. That supports the claim that America needs to restore skilled technical work.
