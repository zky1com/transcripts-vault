# David Friedberg - The Big Beautiful Bill fails to address the US fiscal emergency as the annual deficit remain

## Position
Despite being positioned as a fiscal responsibility measure, the Big Beautiful Bill produces no meaningful reduction in the annual deficit. Interest expense on US debt is already approaching $2T/year on a $37T debt load at an average 3.3% rate — and as that debt rolls over at current 5% market rates, interest expense will compound upward regardless of the bill's minor spending adjustments.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "If you're an American, you should feel shame that your elected officials are proposing that this is the bill that gets passed... The bill ultimately yields no real change in the annual deficit. The annual deficit could climb to $2.5 trillion being added to the federal debt load every single year going forward."
- Transcript: "The United States has called $37 trillion of debt. At 5%, we're paying close to $2 trillion a year just in interest on our debt."
- Transcript: "We are in a fiscal crisis and we're not willing to admit it."

## Logic Chain
1. The Big Beautiful Bill's spending cuts (~$1.5T over 10 years = $150B/year) are insufficient to offset $400B/year in tax cut revenue losses.
2. Net result is approximately zero reduction in annual deficit.
3. A $37T debt at current 5% long-term yield carries ~$1.85T annual interest.
4. As existing lower-rate bonds mature and are refinanced at 5%, interest expense grows automatically.
5. The bill does not address the structural mechanism driving deficit growth.
