# Ivy Zelman - Existing-home turnover is constrained by mortgage lock-in from ultra-low-rate vintages

## Position
Ivy Zelman says the existing-home market remains stuck because many homeowners are locked into sub-market mortgage rates and resist trading into much higher financing costs.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Zelman highlights broad lock-in below current mortgage rates as a major turnover constraint.
- Transcript: she links this lock-in dynamic to depressed existing-home transaction activity.

## Logic Chain
1. Rate lock-in raises the effective cost of moving.
2. Higher moving friction suppresses turnover and available listings.
3. The claim captures Zelman’s explanation for persistent resale-market stagnation.
