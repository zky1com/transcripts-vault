# 
Ray Dalio - Fiscal adjustment made early requires less severe cuts than delayed adjustment

## Position
The interview argues that earlier deficit correction materially reduces the scale of later austerity required to restore debt stability.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript explicitly states faster spending cuts mean less total cutting is required.
- Dalio warns delay compounds debt and interest burden through arithmetic accumulation.
- He advocates a planned path rather than late-stage emergency actions.

## Logic Chain
1. Deficit persistence compounds principal and interest obligations.
2. Compounded burdens increase eventual adjustment magnitude nonlinearly.
3. So early staged correction is less destructive than delayed forced correction.
