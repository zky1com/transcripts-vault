# 
Ray Dalio - Central-bank monetization stabilizes financing short-term but transfers stress into currency purchasing power

## Position
When central banks absorb debt supply, immediate funding stress is reduced, but inflation and currency dilution risks rise over time.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript states governments can print and monetize debt when private demand is insufficient.
- Dalio links monetization episodes to weaker money value and inflation pressure.
- The interview discusses pandemic-era policy waves as examples of this mechanism.

## Logic Chain
1. Monetization replaces market funding gaps with balance-sheet expansion.
2. Expanded money supply can erode real value of nominal claims.
3. Therefore monetization trades near-term solvency pressure for medium-term purchasing-power risk.
