# David Sacks - The federal student loan program should be eliminated and replaced with private capital markets that underwrite loans based on degree and institution earning potential

## Position
The federal student loan program provides capital with zero underwriting — any student can borrow unlimited amounts for any degree at any school regardless of expected earnings. This destroys the market signal that would otherwise discipline universities and students toward economically viable degrees. Private lending with underwriting standards would restore competitive pressure on tuition and degree quality.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The student loan program, the federal student loan program needs to go away because when it goes away... the capital markets will flood to fill that hole. Banks and lenders will show up and they will say, 'We'll underwrite the loans.' But they'll underwrite it by saying good colleges get loans and bad colleges don't. Good degrees get the loans, bad degrees don't."
- Transcript: "Universities have been using the spiraling tuitions to fund massive amounts of bureaucracy... for DEI and woke and all sorts of stuff like that."
- Transcript: "There's no underwriting in student loans today."

## Logic Chain
1. Federal student loans require no underwriting — the government guarantees repayment regardless of degree value.
2. Without repayment risk, universities face no market pressure to control tuition or ensure employment outcomes.
3. Private capital markets with underwriting would price risk based on school quality and degree earning potential.
4. High-cost, low-employment-outcome programs would face capital constraints, forcing price discipline.
5. Eliminating federal backing restores market-driven allocation of educational capital.
