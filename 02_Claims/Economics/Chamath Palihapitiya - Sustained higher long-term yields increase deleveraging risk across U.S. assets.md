# Chamath Palihapitiya - Sustained higher long-term yields increase deleveraging risk across U.S. assets

## Position
If long-term U.S. yields remain structurally higher, leveraged positions across the economy become more fragile, increasing deleveraging risk and downside pressure on asset prices.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript repeatedly frames bond-market stress and higher rates as a central macro risk.
- The discussion highlights rising interest burden and concern that policy choices are adding debt while rates stay elevated.
- Panelists connect higher yields to potential forced selling and reduced risk appetite in financial markets.

## Logic Chain
1. Higher long-end rates raise refinancing costs and collateral pressure for leveraged holders.
2. When financing costs rise faster than cash flows, leverage tolerance falls and de-risking accelerates.
3. Therefore persistent high yields can propagate into broader asset repricing.
