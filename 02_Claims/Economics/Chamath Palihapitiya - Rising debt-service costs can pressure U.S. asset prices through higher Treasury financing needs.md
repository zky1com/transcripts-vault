# Chamath Palihapitiya - Rising debt-service costs can pressure U.S. asset prices through higher Treasury financing needs

## Position
As U.S. interest expense rises, larger Treasury financing needs can tighten financial conditions and weigh on asset prices via higher rates and crowding effects.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript describes materially higher annual interest burden as rates move up relative to prior low-rate baselines.
- Discussion links additional Treasury issuance and bond-market absorption needs to broader asset-price pressure.
- Panel frames debt-service dynamics as recursive: higher rates increase interest costs, which can require more financing.

## Logic Chain
1. Higher debt-service costs increase expected Treasury funding requirements.
2. Larger funding needs can raise term premium and keep rates elevated if demand does not fully absorb supply.
3. Higher discount rates and tighter liquidity then pressure risk assets and valuations.
