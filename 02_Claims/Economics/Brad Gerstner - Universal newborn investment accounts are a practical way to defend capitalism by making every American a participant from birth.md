# Brad Gerstner - Universal newborn investment accounts are a practical way to defend capitalism by making every American a participant from birth

## Position
Brad Gerstner argues that universal newborn investment accounts are a practical way to defend capitalism by making every American a participant from birth.

## Evidence Cited
- He says less than half of people under 40 have a positive view of capitalism.
- He says every child born in the United States will now begin life with a $1,000 investment account in the S&P 500.
- He says the purpose is to give everyone an ownership stake in America's upside and transfer trillions of dollars of wealth to people who otherwise would have had zero.

## Logic Chain
1. Gerstner links anti-capitalist sentiment to exclusion from asset ownership.
2. He argues that broadening ownership from birth creates a concrete stake in capitalist upside.
3. That supports the claim that universal newborn investment accounts are a practical defense of capitalism.
