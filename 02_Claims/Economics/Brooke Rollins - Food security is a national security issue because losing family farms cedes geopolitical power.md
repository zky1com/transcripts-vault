# Brooke Rollins - Food security is a national security issue because losing family farms cedes geopolitical power

## Position
The loss of tens of thousands of family farms in the last decade, combined with China's acquisition of American farmland, makes food security a direct national security threat — losing the ability to feed yourself means losing power on the world stage.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We have already lost tens of thousands of family farms in the last decade."
- Transcript: "The minute you become unable to feed yourself is the minute you lose all power on the world stage."
- Transcript: "China's been buying up a lot of our farmland in America."
- Transcript: Rollins is Secretary of Agriculture, presenting this as the core framing for USDA policy priorities.

## Logic Chain
1. Food production capacity is a prerequisite for national self-sufficiency.
2. Foreign ownership of American agricultural land creates dependency and intelligence/security risks.
3. The consistent loss of family farms reduces domestic production redundancy and resilience.
4. Therefore farm policy is inseparable from national security policy.
