# Chamath Palihapitiya - Credit default swap spread widening is the primary early warning indicator of systemic credit crises as demonstrated in the 2008 financial crisis

## Position
CDS markets price the probability of default events and act as the canary in the coal mine for systemic credit stress. When CDS spreads widen materially, it signals that sophisticated market participants see structural risk in the corporate credit stack — as happened before the 2008 financial crisis.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The CDS actually represents the structural risk in the United States private economy, in the corporate economy. When you see these spreads blowing out, this is actually a very important warning sign."
- Transcript: "This is what was the canary in the coal mine for the great financial crisis. This was the stuff that showed us that there was a big default event happening in the mortgage side of the market."

## Logic Chain
1. CDS contracts price the market's assessment of default probability for specific debt instruments.
2. As default risk rises, spreads widen to compensate sellers of protection.
3. Spread widening preceded the 2008 mortgage crisis by months, giving advance warning.
4. The same dynamic applies to corporate credit: widening spreads signal structural vulnerability before defaults materialize.
