# 
Ray Dalio - Technology rivalry between major powers is becoming a national-security imperative rather than a pure profit race

## Position
The interview frames frontier technology competition as strategic state capacity competition where losing leadership has military and geopolitical consequences.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript states AI/technology rivalry is a war no major country can afford to lose.
- Dalio links technology leadership to broader power positioning beyond commercial returns.
- Discussion ties this rivalry to future spending and policy prioritization choices.

## Logic Chain
1. General-purpose technologies affect economic and defense capability simultaneously.
2. Strategic competitors allocate resources to avoid asymmetric dependence.
3. Therefore technology competition increasingly behaves like security policy, not only market competition.
