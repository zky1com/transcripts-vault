# Rick Caruso - Los Angeles spends $900,000 per homeless person removed from streets representing catastrophic misallocation of homelessness program funding

## Position
Los Angeles's homelessness spending has produced spending metrics that are economically indefensible: $900,000 per homeless person removed from the streets. This implies either that the programs are extraordinarily expensive to operate, that they fail at high rates requiring repeated interventions per person, or that funds are being diverted to overhead rather than direct services — making it impossible to justify continued funding at current cost levels.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We are spending in the city of Los Angeles $900,000 per person that they're removing from the streets."
- Transcript: "There's billions and billions of dollars wasted. Redirect that to organizations that have incredible track records of being successful in Los Angeles of housing people, giving them the services for mental health and drug addiction."

## Logic Chain
1. $900,000 per person removed from streets is the reported cost of LA's homelessness programs.
2. This cost is multiples higher than the cost of simply housing a person permanently.
3. High per-person costs suggest inefficient program design, high administrative overhead, or low success rates.
4. Evidence-based organizations achieving similar outcomes do so at a fraction of the cost.
5. The gap between cost and outcome indicates systemic misallocation, not an underfunding problem.
