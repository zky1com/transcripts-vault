# Chamath Palihapitiya - Letting data centers pay the full cost of their own power demand is a necessary first step but not enough to solve the wider electricity crunch

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that forcing data centers to pay the full cost of their own power demand is a necessary first step, but not enough to solve the wider electricity crunch.

## Evidence Cited
- He says hyperscalers paying for their own power and water impacts is a good first-order move.
- He also says the broader issue is that utilities underbuilt for years while demand from the whole economy kept rising.
- He argues that households still need help with energy costs and new generation has to be built faster.

## Logic Chain
1. Palihapitiya distinguishes between local fairness and system-wide adequacy.
2. Even if data centers stop shifting costs, the grid can still be too constrained overall.
3. That supports the claim that full-cost pricing for data centers is necessary but not sufficient.
