# David Friedberg - Government-backed college loans inflated education costs and created a generation of 32 million Americans trapped in debt with no economic path forward

## Position
Government-subsidized student loan programs with no underwriting or cost controls allowed universities to charge unlimited tuition, trapping 32 million Americans in negative-capital debt cycles they cannot escape.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We gave unrestricted access to capital, which inflated the cost of education. People could go to school like Zoron and major in African studies and graduate with $200, $300,000 of debt and then never get a job."
- Transcript: "This is the truth for 32 million young Americans. They find themselves living in places where they cannot afford to pay their bills every month."
- Transcript: "They all have what is called negative capital. They have debt and they will never be able to get out of that cycle."

## Logic Chain
1. Government loan programs provided unrestricted capital with no underwriting on quality or earning potential of degrees.
2. Universities responded to unlimited demand by raising tuition indefinitely.
3. Graduates with worthless degrees and six-figure debt now have negative capital with no realistic exit path.
