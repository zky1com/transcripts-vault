# Vlad Tenev - A $130T intergenerational wealth transfer can expand retail platform participation

## Position
Tenev frames the coming intergenerational wealth transfer as the defining opportunity for Robinhood: $130T+ changing hands from older generations to Gen X, Millennials, and Gen Z — who are Robinhood's primary customer base. With $250B+ already on the platform, he argues Robinhood is positioned to be the primary financial institution capturing this transfer.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "there's going to be over 130 trillion that changes hands from silent generation and baby boomers to younger people"
- Transcript: "I think Robin Hood's actually very well positioned to be one of the, if not the number one primary institution that benefits from that transfer"
- Transcript: "We've got over a quarter trillion assets on the platform already, which seems like a big number, but it's actually just a drop in the bucket compared to what's going to happen"

## Logic Chain
1. The silent generation and baby boomers hold the majority of US household wealth (~$130T estimate).
2. This wealth will transfer to younger generations over the coming decades as boomers age.
3. Younger generations have disproportionately adopted Robinhood as their primary financial platform.
4. Robinhood's $250B+ AUC is a fraction of what it could receive if it captures even a small share of $130T.
