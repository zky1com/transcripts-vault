# John Cassidy - Postwar managed capitalism combined demand support financial controls and social insurance effectively

## Position
John Cassidy argues the postwar model worked by combining macro demand management, tighter financial regulation, and broader social safety nets, producing a more stable capitalism for several decades.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: Cassidy identifies low-unemployment demand policy, financial constraints, and welfare supports as linked pillars.
- Transcript: he describes that configuration as a successful historical variant of managed capitalism.

## Logic Chain
1. Stability came from policy architecture, not spontaneous market self-correction.
2. Coordinated institutions reduced boom-bust extremes.
3. The claim summarizes Cassidy’s favorable reading of the postwar settlement.
