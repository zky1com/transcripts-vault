# 
Ray Dalio - Debt-cycle stress historically correlates with currency devaluation and periodic regime reset

## Position
The interview argues that long debt cycles tend to end with devaluation/restructuring phases that reset nominal claims and start a new cycle.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript references historical debt and currency outcomes across many countries and eras.
- Dalio states most long-lived debt/currency regimes eventually devalue rather than persist unchanged.
- He presents these outcomes as recurring cycle mechanics rather than isolated anomalies.

## Logic Chain
1. Persistent debt overhangs exceed what real growth and taxation can absorb.
2. Policy systems eventually rebalance through nominal adjustment or restructuring.
3. Hence debt-cycle endpoints often include devaluation-driven reset dynamics.
