# Chamath Palihapitiya - Sovereign and institutional asset allocation may rotate if U.S. rate volatility remains structurally high

## Position
Persistent U.S. rate volatility can incentivize global allocators to reduce concentration and rebalance exposures.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript includes discussion of potential selling/deleveraging pressure tied to U.S. rates.
- Panel frames high volatility as a cross-asset risk management issue.
- Conversation links policy credibility to international allocation behavior.

## Logic Chain
1. Allocators respond to sustained volatility and policy uncertainty.
2. Risk-adjusted returns drive diversification decisions.
3. Sustained volatility can therefore trigger allocation rotation.
