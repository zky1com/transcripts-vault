# Howard Lutnick - If companies need American market access or state help the American public should capture part of the upside

## Position
Howard Lutnick argues that when companies rely on American market access or presidential leverage to unlock major advantages, the American public should share in the upside.

## Evidence Cited
- He says President Trump asks what the country gets back when firms need him to open doors or change policy for them.
- He describes arrangements where the government takes a share or tariff when it creates that value.
- He links that upside capture to reducing deficits and funding national priorities.

## Logic Chain
1. Lutnick rejects the idea that public power should be deployed for free on behalf of large firms.
2. He says government-created gains should return some value to taxpayers.
3. That supports the claim that the public should capture part of the upside when state leverage is decisive.
