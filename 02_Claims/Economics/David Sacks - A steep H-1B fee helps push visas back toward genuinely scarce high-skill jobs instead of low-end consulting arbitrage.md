# David Sacks - A steep H-1B fee helps push visas back toward genuinely scarce high-skill jobs instead of low-end consulting arbitrage

## Position
David Sacks argues that a steep H-1B fee helps push visas back toward genuinely scarce high-skill jobs instead of low-end consulting arbitrage.

## Evidence Cited
- He says there are roughly five times as many H-1B applications as slots, which has turned the system into a lottery.
- He says about half the visas go to IT consulting firms with average salaries around $65,000.
- He says the fee creates scarcity that favors higher-skilled and higher-paid jobs where there is a real shortage of Americans.

## Logic Chain
1. Sacks treats the current lottery as a mechanism that misallocates visas toward low-value labor arbitrage.
2. He argues that raising the price changes who applies and which jobs are worth sponsoring.
3. That supports the claim that a high fee pushes H-1Bs back toward genuinely scarce high-skill roles.
