# Chamath Palihapitiya - Corporate debt covenants tied to revenue create hidden systemic default risk that tariff-driven revenue shocks can trigger at scale

## Position
A large volume of US corporate debt includes covenants tied to revenue and EBITDA metrics. When tariffs reduce company revenues, these covenant triggers can force a cascade of defaults that is not visible in standard credit or equity metrics until it is too late to hedge.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "There is a tremendous amount of corporate debt that supports these businesses today... the tariff picture actually impacts revenues. The problem with that is that there's a lot of companies that have debt covenants tied to revenue and EBITDA."
- Transcript: "This is what I spoke about at the beginning of January, which is the one risk that is uncontrollable is what happens to corporate debt and could we see a wave of defaults."

## Logic Chain
1. Many corporate debt instruments contain revenue and EBITDA covenant triggers.
2. Tariffs compress corporate revenues by raising input costs and reducing volumes.
3. If revenue falls below covenant thresholds, technical defaults are triggered automatically.
4. A wave of simultaneous covenant violations would constitute a systemic credit event.
