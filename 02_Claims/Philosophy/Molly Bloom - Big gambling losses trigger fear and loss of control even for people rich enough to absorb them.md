# Molly Bloom - Big gambling losses trigger fear and loss of control even for people rich enough to absorb them

## Position
Molly Bloom says the wild behavior she saw after large gambling losses was driven less by pure money math than by fear and the feeling of losing control.

## Evidence Cited
- She says many players could afford the losses on paper but still reacted in extreme ways.
- She says what sat underneath those reactions was fear.
- She describes losing as a trigger for feeling out of control.

## Logic Chain
1. Bloom is separating balance-sheet capacity from emotional response.
2. She argues that rich players can still panic because the deeper issue is control and identity.
3. That supports the claim that fear explains the behavior better than wealth does.
