# Naval - The only two resource allocation mechanisms are market price signals and human authority — authorit...

## Position

When deciding how to distribute economic value, there are exactly two approaches: use an objective function (market prices, determined by voluntary exchange) or use a subjective function (people in authority decide who deserves what). The problem with subjective allocation is that it requires concentrating power in whoever makes the decisions, and humans are not altruistic with that power â€” it flows to friends, family, and allies. This is not a design flaw in particular implementations of communism; it is an inevitable consequence of subjective allocation. Every communist system has followed this pattern.

## Logic Chain

1. Resource allocation requires a decision rule
2. Decision rules are either objective (prices) or subjective (authority)
3. Subjective allocation creates a power concentration â€” someone must be in charge of deciding
4. Humans with power over allocation systematically favor their in-group
5. Therefore: any allocation system that rejects market prices must answer who decides, and the answer will be whoever acquires sufficient coercive power
