## Position

The conventional undergraduate explanation of wave-particle duality â€” that a photon is "born as a particle, lives as a wave, and then dies again as a particle" â€” is not just incomplete; it is nonsense. The criterion (Deutsch's): a good explanation must be hard to vary, mechanistic, and must not require an entity to have self-knowledge it cannot possess. A photon doesn't "know" whether an observer is present, doesn't "know" what experiment it is participating in. The explanation depends on the photon making contextual decisions it has no capacity to make. This is precisely the kind of bad explanation Deutsch identifies: it tells a story that sounds plausible but has no underlying mechanism that could produce the described behavior. It is easy to vary (swap in any vague story about "observation") and makes no precise, risky predictions about what the photon "decides" and when.

## Logic Chain

1. Standard explanation: photon is a particle when emitted, a wave while traveling, a particle again when detected
2. This requires the photon to "know" whether an observer is present (to collapse to a particle) vs absent (to propagate as a wave)
3. But a photon has no mechanism by which it can possess or act on such contextual knowledge
4. Therefore: the explanation lacks a mechanism â€” it is narratively fluent but mechanistically empty
5. A good explanation (Deutsch) must have a mechanism that explains *why* each parameter is what it is â€” this one has none
