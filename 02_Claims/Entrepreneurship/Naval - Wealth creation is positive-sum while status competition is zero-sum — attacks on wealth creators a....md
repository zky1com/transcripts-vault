# Naval - Wealth creation is positive-sum while status competition is zero-sum — attacks on wealth creators a...

## Position

Naval identifies two fundamental games in life: the money game (wealth creation) and the status game (social hierarchy). Wealth is positive-sum: everyone can have a house; more houses being built makes it easier and cheaper to build more houses. Status is zero-sum: for number three to move to number two, number two must move out. When journalists or politicians attack wealthy people or the technology industry, they are playing the status game â€” claiming to represent "the people" and therefore ranking higher in the moral hierarchy. The mechanism is to put wealthy people down to elevate oneself. This is why status games make you "an angry combative person" â€” you are always fighting to elevate yourself by putting others down. Naval argues you should mostly avoid status games precisely because they require harming others to win.

## Evidence Cited

- "wealth is not a zero-sum game everybody in the world can have a house because you have a house doesn't take away from my ability to have a house"
- "status on the other hand is a zero-sum game... for a number three to move to number two number two has to move out of that slot"
- "when journalists attack rich people or they attack the technology industry they're really bidding for status they're saying no the people are more important"
- "most of the times when you're trying to create wealth you're actually getting attacked by someone else and they're trying to look like a goody two-shoes but really what they're doing is they're trying to up their own status at your expense"

## Logic Chain

1. Wealth creation is positive-sum: building things creates value that didn't exist before; one person's wealth doesn't reduce another's potential wealth
2. Status competition is zero-sum: for one person to rise, another must fall
3. Most moral attacks on wealth creators are structurally status plays â€” the attacker benefits in the status game by positioning themselves above the target
4. Understanding this explains why "virtue signaling by attacking the whole enterprise" of wealth creation is so common
5. Conclusion: avoid status games because they require zero-sum thinking and make you combative; focus on positive-sum wealth creation instead
