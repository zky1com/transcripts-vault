# Naval - Mathematics is a creative act and therefore fallible — even necessary truth is only accessible through fallible human minds

## Position

Mathematics is commonly treated as uniquely certain — as the one domain where conclusions are definitively true rather than provisionally corroborated. This is a conflation of two distinct things: the subject matter of mathematics (necessary truth) and our current knowledge of that subject matter (our mathematical theories and proofs). The subject matter may be necessary and exact, but the process of accessing it — mathematical reasoning by human mathematicians with physical brains — is fallible in the same way as any other reasoning process. A mathematician can make an error in a proof, miss a faulty axiom, or reason incorrectly. Because mathematics is a creative act (new theorems must be invented, not just read off from reality), it inherits all the fallibility of human creativity. This does not mean mathematical conclusions are unreliable in practice; it means they are not infallible in principle, and should be treated as corrigible rather than final.

## Evidence Cited

- "even mathematics is capable of error because mathematics is a creative act — we're never quite done — there could have been a mistake in your axiom somewhere"
- "a mathematician is just as fallible as anyone else"
- "because a mathematician has a brain which is a physical object and all physical objects are subject to making errors — a mathematician is just as fallible as anyone else — then what they end up proving could be an error"

## Logic Chain

1. Mathematics subject matter = necessary truth (what is necessarily true in all possible worlds)
2. But accessing that subject matter requires: inventing axioms, constructing proofs, reasoning step-by-step
3. These activities are performed by human minds — physical objects subject to error
4. Therefore: our mathematical theories may contain errors even when targeting necessary truth
5. Mathematics is a creative act: new mathematics must be invented, not observed — it is not read off from an external source
6. Creative acts are fallible by nature → mathematical knowledge is fallible
7. Compare: particle physics subject matter = fundamental particles; but our standard model is fallible because accessing it requires human theorizing and experiment interpretation
