# Naval - Eddington's eclipse experiment eliminated Newton but did not prove general relativity is the final word on gravity

## Position

The 1919 Eddington eclipse experiment is commonly described as "proving general relativity." This description is wrong and misleads people about how science works. What actually happened was a crucial experiment: a test designed to discriminate between two competing theories (Newtonian gravity and general relativity) by measuring how much light bends around the sun during an eclipse. Newton predicted one value; Einstein predicted a different value. The observed value matched Einstein and contradicted Newton. This result eliminated Newton's theory and retained GR — not because GR was proven true, but because it was consistent with the observation while Newton's theory was not. GR remains provisional: it is the best theory we have, consistent with all available tests, but there are good reasons to think it must eventually be superseded (its inconsistency with quantum mechanics being the most obvious).

## Evidence Cited

- "the correct way of describing what happened is not that we showed that general relativity was correct in some final sense — but rather we refuted Newton's theory of gravitation"
- "Newton's theory was ruled out because it was inconsistent with the test — while general relativity was consistent with the test"
- "this doesn't mean that general relativity is the final word in science — it means that it's the best theory we have for now"
- "we never have the final word — and that's a good thing"

## Logic Chain

1. Two competing theories: Newtonian gravity (light bending = X) vs GR (light bending = Y, where Y ≠ X)
2. Crucial experiment: measure actual light bending during 1919 eclipse
3. Result: matches Y (GR), contradicts X (Newton)
4. Conclusion: Newton's theory refuted; GR retained as best available theory
5. This is not proof that GR is true — it is proof that Newton's theory is false (in this domain)
6. GR may be refuted by future experiments or superseded by a theory that explains both GR and QM
7. The experiment is valuable precisely as a refutation, not as a confirmation
