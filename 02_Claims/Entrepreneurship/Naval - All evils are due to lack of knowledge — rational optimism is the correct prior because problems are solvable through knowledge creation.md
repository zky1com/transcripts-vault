# Naval - All evils are due to lack of knowledge — rational optimism is the correct prior because problems are solvable through knowledge creation

## Position

Naval takes the Deutschian position that all evils — suffering, poverty, disease, conflict — are due to insufficient knowledge. This is not a feel-good slogan but a philosophical claim: nothing is inherently beyond solution except what the laws of physics themselves prevent. Given that knowledge creation is an open-ended process, every current evil is in principle solvable by the knowledge that has not yet been created. Therefore rational optimism — the belief that problems can be solved — is not naive but epistemically correct given the historical record and the structure of knowledge. The data supports it: human material conditions have improved dramatically across every measurable dimension over the past two centuries. History supports it: every problem declared unsolvable (Malthusian food limits, infectious disease, illiteracy) was eventually solved. Rational optimism is the way out because pessimism — the belief that problems cannot be solved — is self-fulfilling: it prevents the creative work that would solve them.

## Logic Chain

1. All evils = problems that cause suffering or constrain human flourishing
2. Claim: these all arise from insufficient knowledge — if we knew enough, we could solve them
3. Knowledge creation is open-ended — there is no finite ceiling on what can be known
4. Therefore: every current evil is in principle solvable by future knowledge
5. Historical record: problems declared unsolvable consistently were solved by knowledge advances
6. Pessimism is self-defeating: believing problems are unsolvable reduces investment in solving them → becomes self-fulfilling
7. Rational optimism is both epistemically correct (matches evidence) and instrumentally correct (enables the creative work that solves problems)
