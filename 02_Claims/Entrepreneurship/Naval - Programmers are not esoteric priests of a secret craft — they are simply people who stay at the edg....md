# Naval - Programmers are not esoteric priests of a secret craft — they are simply people who stay at the edg...

## Position

Programming is not a fixed ritual â€” it is a disposition toward mastery. Today's programmers use languages and frameworks; tomorrow's will use natural language interfaces. The skill is not knowledge of any particular tool, but the willingness to stay at the frontier and apply intense, dedicated effort to building software. A natural-language programmer who deeply understands what they are building is still a programmer in the meaningful sense. The definition of "programmer" evolves with the tools, not with any specific syntax or paradigm.

## Evidence Cited

- "programmers are not these magical elves who have learned this secret esoteric ritual which nobody else can learn and understand"
- "programmers are simply the people who are so dedicated to building software that they're willing to stay at the edge of the craft and learn and use every tool no matter how sophisticated or complex it is"
- "tomorrow's program will be doing natural language programming but they will be every bit as dedicated skilled intense and applying effort as in the past"

## Logic Chain

1. The surface of programming (syntax, paradigm, toolchain) has changed completely across each generation of software
2. What persists across generations is the underlying disposition: dedication to building software at the edge of available capability
3. Natural language AI tools change the surface, not the disposition
4. Therefore: tomorrow's programmer is not a non-programmer using AI â€” they are a programmer whose primary language is natural language
5. Obsolescence risk applies to people who stop learning, not to the category of "programmer"
