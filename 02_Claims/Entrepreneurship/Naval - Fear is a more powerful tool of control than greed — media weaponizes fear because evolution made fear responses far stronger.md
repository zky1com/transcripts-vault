# Naval - Fear is a more powerful tool of control than greed — media weaponizes fear because evolution made fear responses far stronger

## Position

Society rails against greed but is far too complacent about those who weaponize fear. In evolutionary terms, fear had much larger consequences than greed: missing a predator or a hostile tribe attack was fatal; failing to get an extra deer carcass was not. This asymmetry means humans are wired to respond far more strongly to fear stimuli than to greed stimuli — and this is precisely why media and political actors prefer fear as their tool. Greedy actors in a capitalist environment are mostly constrained to voluntary transactions; their worst case is they extract some surplus value. Fearful actors can drive people into conflict, anger, and collective action against threats (real or manufactured). Media that generates fear gets rewarded with engagement; the person inducing fear often gets to present as a public servant ("I'm warning you for your own good") while simultaneously capturing full control of attention. Therefore: be far more suspicious of people trying to scare you than people trying to sell you something.

- "always be very wary of people who are trying to scare you usually they're up to no good"
- "in evolution the rewards to being greedy were fairly low... fear on the other hand had huge consequences"

## Logic Chain

1. Evolutionary history: fear of predators/hostile tribes = fatal; missed greed opportunity = minor setback
2. Therefore the fear response system is much more powerful and sensitive than the greed response
3. This makes fear a more effective control mechanism — fear overrides rational evaluation more completely than greed
4. Greedy actors in capitalism still depend on voluntary exchange; the worst they can do is extract surplus from willing participants
5. Fear actors can manufacture threats, drive anger, and mobilize collective conflict
6. Media discovers that fear content generates more engagement than other content → feedback loop toward fear-based framing
7. Fear-inducing actors present as altruists ("warning you"), gaining virtue signal AND control simultaneously
8. Implication: anger at greedy people is often misdirected; the true danger is those who induce fear
