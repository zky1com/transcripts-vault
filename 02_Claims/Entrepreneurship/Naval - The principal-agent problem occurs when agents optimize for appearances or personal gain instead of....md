# Naval - The principal-agent problem occurs when agents optimize for appearances or personal gain instead of...

## Position

Principals (owners) want what maximizes the business. Agents (employees) want what looks good to the principal, makes them friends, or maximizes their personal pay. These are structurally different objectives. Hired-gun CEOs of widely-held public companies are the clearest example: no single large owner, so the CEO stuffs the board with allies and issues themselves low-priced stock options. Agents always find ways to hack incentive systems.

## Evidence Cited

- Hired CEO example: stuffing board with allies, stock buybacks tied to compensation
- Charlie Munger: "If you can be working on incentives, don't work on anything else"
- Julius Caesar/Napoleon quote: "If you want it done, go. If not, send."

## Logic Chain

1. Principal's incentive: maximize business value long-term
2. Agent's incentive: maximize personal reward (status, money, relationships) in the near term
3. Without aligned ownership, agents will rationally optimize for their own objectives
4. Incentive systems are complex; agents will always find workarounds (Goodhart's Law)
5. The solution is alignment through ownership, not just monitoring
