# Orlando Bravo - Modern software private equity is driven more by growth and terminal value than by leverage engineering

## Position
Orlando Bravo argues that software private equity today depends far more on growing the business and selling a better asset later than on old-school leverage-driven returns.

## Evidence Cited
- He says old stereotypes about private equity fit earlier decades more than software investing today.
- He explains that modern software deals are mostly equity and require terminal value appreciation.
- He says buyers now need to build and grow the business because that is where the return comes from.

## Logic Chain
1. Bravo contrasts older debt-heavy buyouts with current software deal math.
2. He explains that expensive software assets force firms to earn returns through operating improvement and growth.
3. That supports the claim that growth matters more than leverage engineering in this segment.
