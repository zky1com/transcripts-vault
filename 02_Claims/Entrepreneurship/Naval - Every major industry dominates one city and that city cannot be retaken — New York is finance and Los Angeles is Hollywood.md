# Naval - Every major industry dominates one city and that city cannot be retaken — New York is finance and Los Angeles is Hollywood

## Position

Major industries cluster in dominant cities and those cities become self-reinforcing ecosystems that cannot easily be displaced by newcomers. New York's entire support infrastructure — services, culture, real estate, social networks — optimizes for finance. An industry trying to establish itself there would always be secondary: the city's culture, talent pipeline, and infrastructure cater to finance and would not serve tech's needs as well as they serve finance's. The same logic applies to Hollywood in LA. A tech worker or founder trying to compete in either city would be a second-class citizen in an ecosystem built for someone else. This means that the early choice of location is critical and very durable: once an industry-city pairing is established, it locks in and cannot be easily retaken by a competing industry — the path dependency at the city level is real even if it isn't what caused the original clustering.

## Evidence Cited

- "every major industry in the United States dominates a particular town"
- "New York is taken — you cannot move the tech industry to New York — it just disappears — there's too many other things to do, too many distractions, and you'll always be number two"
- "Los Angeles is dominated by Hollywood — it is a cultural capital of the United States — it is taken as well"
- "the whole town caters towards Hollywood so it wouldn't cater towards the needs of the Nerds in Tech"

## Logic Chain

1. Major industries cluster: finance in New York, media in LA, tech in SF
2. Once clustered: city's infrastructure, culture, talent, services all optimize for that industry
3. Secondary industries in that city get second-class treatment: less infrastructure support, talent poached by dominant industry, culture doesn't fit
4. Trying to move tech to New York: tech becomes number two; finance culture crowds out tech culture; talent pool is finance-trained
5. Same for LA/Hollywood: tech workers don't fit the media ecosystem; entertainment culture dominates
6. Therefore: industry-city lock-in is strong once established; you cannot insert a different dominant industry into an already-captured city
7. Implication: new industries need new cities (or uncaptured cities) — the first-mover advantage in city capture is enormous
