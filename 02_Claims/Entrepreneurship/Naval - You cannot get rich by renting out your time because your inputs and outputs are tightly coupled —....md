## Position

The employee is fundamentally replaceable: if a job can be taught in a school, someone is being trained to replace you right now. Doctors who get truly rich do so by opening a private practice (building a brand and system), not by seeing more patients per hour. 1. Salary income: every unit of output requires a unit of input (time); income scales linearly at best
2. Employers pay the minimum necessary to retain you, not the value you create
3. Most skilled jobs can be taught; if teachable, someone is being trained to replace you right now
4. Nonlinear wealth requires disconnecting inputs from outputs â€” the software engineer creates once and the product earns forever
5. The only way to own the upside of that disconnection is equity: a piece of the business, not just a salary from it
