# Vitalik Buterin - Bitcoin is the most important crypto but is limited by non-programmability and lack of privacy

## Position

Bitcoin deserves enormous credit as the first and most important cryptocurrency. It nailed the "protect your money, Austrian economics-style" use case and has the most credible claim to being a store of value. However, it falls short in two fundamental ways that cannot be fixed without severe trade-offs to its core properties: it is not programmable (no smart contracts) and it is not private (all transactions visible on-chain). These limitations are not incidental bugs but consequences of design choices optimized for simplicity, decentralization, and security. Adding programmability and privacy to Bitcoin would require changes that would compromise what makes Bitcoin valuable. Therefore, Bitcoin and Ethereum coexist as complements rather than competitors: Bitcoin handles the gold-like store-of-value use case; Ethereum handles the programmable application layer.

## Logic Chain

1. Bitcoin's design optimizes for: simplicity, security, decentralization, credible scarcity
2. These optimizations require: no Turing-complete execution environment; transparent UTXO model
3. Not programmable: cannot build complex financial applications, DAOs, etc. on top of Bitcoin
4. Not private: all transactions visible → not suitable for privacy-requiring use cases
5. Fixing either limitation would require compromising the simplicity/security/decentralization trade-offs
6. Therefore: Bitcoin's limitations are inherent to its design, not bugs to be fixed
7. Conclusion: Bitcoin and programmable+privacy chains (Ethereum, ZCash etc.) are complementary, not competing
