# Naval - Agents who think like principals get promoted disproportionately because they solve the hardest pro...

## Position

Employees who think like owners solve the principal-agent problem from the inside, which is rare and extremely valuable. Founders recognize this and promote such agents dramatically out of proportion to their title or experience, skipping multiple levels. As an employee, thinking like an owner is both training for eventually becoming one and the fastest path to advancement within an organization.

## Evidence Cited

- Naval's observation that founders skip people multiple levels when they find agents who think like principals
- Heuristic: "What would the founder do?" as the agent's daily operating principle
- Naval's personal approach: small teams where everyone is economically aligned + filtered for mission commitment

## Logic Chain

1. The principal-agent problem is the single biggest problem in management
2. An employee who thinks like an owner eliminates the problem from within
3. This is so rare and valuable that founders disproportionately reward it with authority and equity
4. The employee gains: training for ownership + aligned compensation + fast advancement
5. Over time, the agent who thinks like a principal becomes a principal
