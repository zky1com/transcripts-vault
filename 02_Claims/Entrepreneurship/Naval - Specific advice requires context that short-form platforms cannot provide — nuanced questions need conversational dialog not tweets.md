# Naval - Specific advice requires context that short-form platforms cannot provide — nuanced questions need conversational dialog not tweets

## Position

Naval identifies a structural mismatch between the advice people seek and the platforms they seek it on. Highly specific questions — "should I quit my job to start a company?" "should I take this investor's term sheet?" — require deep contextual understanding to answer well. Twitter at 280 characters provides no context. The person asking doesn't have room to explain their situation, and Naval doesn't have room to qualify his response. The result is generic advice misapplied to specific situations. This is why Naval preferred voice platforms (Periscope, Clubhouse, Air Chat): they enable the back-and-forth needed to establish context. "When people would ask me a question, then I could ask a follow-up question back." This iterative dialog is the mechanism through which context-appropriate advice becomes possible. High-level principles work on Twitter; specific advice requires a different medium.

## Evidence Cited

- "One of the problems I have with the how to get rich content is people ask me highly specific questions on Twitter in 140 or 280 characters and I just don't have enough context to respond."
- "That's why I liked Air Chat. That's why I liked Clubhouse. That's why I liked spoken format...When people would ask me a question, then I could ask a follow-up question back to them and they could ask me another question and we could dig through and try to get to the meat of what they were asking."

## Logic Chain

1. Specific advice depends on contextual details that vary person-to-person
2. Short-form platforms (Twitter) provide no room for the advisor to receive context or the person to supply it
3. Voice/dialog platforms enable iterative clarification — multiple rounds of question and answer establish context
4. Once context is established, specific advice becomes possible: "if I were in your shoes, I would do the following"
5. Therefore: match the medium to the advice type — general principles on short-form, specific advice in dialog
