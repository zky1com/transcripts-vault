# Naval - Bayesianism is our evolutionary default because non-explanatory animals are Bayesian by necessity

## Position

Humans naturally think in Bayesian patterns — accumulating observations, projecting past patterns into the future — not because it is the epistemically correct approach but because it is the evolutionarily default one. Every animal without a neocortex capable of forming good explanations must operate this way: sun rose yesterday → sun will rise tomorrow; that thing was hot → it will be hot again. This pattern-based induction is wired into older brain structures. The neocortex added a qualitatively different capability: the ability to explain observations in terms of unseen causes, to construct models of mechanisms rather than just patterns. But this capacity is effortful and non-instinctual. People default to Bayesianism because their evolutionary heritage runs much deeper than their ability to form explanations. Recognizing this helps explain why Bayesian epistemology feels natural and correct even though it has fundamental problems as a theory of knowledge.

## Evidence Cited

- "I think the reason we fall into Bayesianism so easily is probably related to why we fall into pessimism so easily — we're evolutionarily hardwired for Bayesianism"
- "every other animal on the planet that can't form good explanations is a Bayesian — they're just looking at repeated events: the sun rose yesterday, the sun will rise tomorrow"
- "we have this neocortex that can form good explanations — that can explain the scene in terms of the unseen — and that gives us a higher level of reasoning — but that higher level of reasoning is not instinctual to us — it requires effort, it requires deep thinking — but we default to Bayesianism because that is how a lot of the natural world around us seems to work at a purely biological level"

## Logic Chain

1. Animals without explanatory capacity: can only observe patterns and project them forward → this is Bayesian updating
2. This has been evolutionarily successful for hundreds of millions of years → deeply embedded in biology
3. Human neocortex adds capacity to form explanations (model unseen causes) → higher-level reasoning
4. But: ancient pattern-recognition circuitry is much more deeply embedded and requires no effort
5. Explanatory reasoning requires effort, is non-instinctual, and is a recent evolutionary addition
6. Therefore: humans default to Bayesianism under cognitive load or when not actively applying explanatory reasoning
7. This explains why Bayesian epistemology "feels right" — it maps onto deep cognitive defaults — even though it has fundamental epistemic problems
