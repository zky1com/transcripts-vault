# Naval - The early team is the DNA of the company because their values and standards replicate through every...

## Position

The first 10-40 people a founder directly manages are not just early employees â€” they are the genetic code of the company. Their values, judgment, work ethic, ego levels, and standards will propagate through every hire they make in the future, compounding across the company's life. Whoever built great early teams (Jobs, Musk) built companies that are still admired generations later. Whoever compromised early set a lower quality floor that spread throughout the organization and proved very difficult to raise.

## Evidence Cited

- "the early people are the DNA of the company"
- Required attributes for early people: "self-managing, low ego, hardworking, highly competent builders, technical, maybe one or two sellers"
- "you can't watch everything, you can't micromanage everything" â€” so early people must carry the standard themselves
- Connection to "team you build is the company you build" (Venode Kla)

## Logic Chain

1. Early employees become future managers and hiring decision-makers
2. Each person's own standards, taste, and values determine who they hire next
3. These standards compound exponentially as each early hire eventually manages multiple people
4. Therefore: the quality floor of the entire future company is set by the quality floor of the first directly managed team
5. This is why no amount of later intervention can fully overcome compromises made in the first 10-20 hires
