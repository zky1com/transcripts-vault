# Naval - Better bored than busy — the over-scheduled life fills time with the many and crowds out the timeless few

## Position

The world is full of low-value opportunities that demand time and attention. A scheduled life responds to all of them with "yes" or at least "maybe," which gradually fills every slot with commitments that are fine but not exceptional. The result is a life so busy that the rare high-value opportunity — the one worth "coming out of retirement" for — gets no attention because there is no attention left. The correct filter is radical: say no to everything by default, keep time genuinely empty, and reserve availability only for opportunities so good they pull you out of your existing comfortable state. Boredom is the correct state because it signals that you have protected availability. Busyness signals that you have squandered it. The final accounting on your deathbed will judge the timeless things, not the number of appointments kept.

## Evidence Cited

- "I say no to everything"
- "my mottos are: better bored than busy and the over-scheduled life isn't worth living"
- "the world is full of opportunity — you just have to be available — time, mental space to grab it — and when you grab it you're going to be sorry you grabbed it because now there's a thousand other things you can't grab — so you only want to grab the ones that really, really, really appeal to you"
- "when you look back on your life in that final moment you will wish that you'd spent more time on the timeless and less time on the temporary"

## Logic Chain

1. Most opportunities are low-to-moderate value; rare opportunities are very high value
2. Scheduled life: says yes to many low-value opportunities → all time slots filled
3. When high-value opportunity appears: no available time/attention → missed
4. Each commitment displaces many alternatives → selecting by default is very costly
5. Optimal filter: say no to everything by default; accept only when the pull is overwhelming ("brings you out of retirement")
6. Boredom = preserved availability = capacity to seize rare high-value opportunities
7. Deathbed accounting: you will not wish you had more meetings — you will wish you spent more time on the few timeless things
