# Naval - Individuals search for truth while groups search for consensus — the larger the group the less real...

## Position

There is a structural difference between how individuals and groups process information. Individuals can pursue truth at the cost of social friction â€” they can say unpopular things, maintain minority positions, and update based on argument alone. Groups cannot do this because a group that fails to achieve consensus disintegrates. A group's primary imperative is cohesion. This means groups systematically filter out information that would create conflict, which is often exactly the most important information. The implication is direct and uncomfortable: as groups grow larger, the quality of feedback they produce declines monotonically. Small, tight-knit groups can sometimes maintain real standards. Large organizations, award bodies, mass media, and fan communities produce feedback that is almost entirely social performance rather than signal. The practical consequence: do not optimize for group validation â€” optimize for market acceptance and physical performance metrics.

## Evidence Cited

- "You can't get good feedback from groups because groups are just trying to get along. Individuals search for truth. Groups search for consensus. A group that doesn't get along decoheres. It falls apart. And the larger the group, the less good feedback you're going to get from it."
