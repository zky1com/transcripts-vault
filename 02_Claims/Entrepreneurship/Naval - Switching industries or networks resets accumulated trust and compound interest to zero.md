# Naval - Switching industries or networks resets accumulated trust and compound interest to zero

## Position

Every time you switch industries or networks, you lose the accumulated trust, reputation, and relationships you've built. You start from zero â€” strangers don't know if they can trust you, and you don't know if you can trust them. Compound interest in relationships requires staying in the game long enough for the interest to compound. Hopping between industries feels like optionality but destroys the asset that actually makes people wealthy.

## Evidence Cited

- Woodworking industry example: if you build a network there and then jump to an adjacent industry, "every time you reset, every time you wander out of where you built your network, you're gonna be starting from scratch"
- "They're not gonna trust you, you're not gonna know who to trust"
- Warren Buffett example as counterpoint: he succeeded in part because the US stock market was stable â€” the platform didn't get destroyed, allowing decades of compounding

## Logic Chain

1. Trust and reputation compound over time within a stable network
2. Each industry or network switch loses that accumulated trust
3. New participants don't know you and have no reason to extend trust
4. This explains why "staying in your lane" is not timidity â€” it's protecting the compounding asset
5. Therefore, frequent industry-switching is a form of wealth destruction, not opportunity-seeking
