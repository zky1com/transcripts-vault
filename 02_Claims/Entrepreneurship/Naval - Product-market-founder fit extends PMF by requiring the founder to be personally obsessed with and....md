# Naval - Product-market-founder fit extends PMF by requiring the founder to be personally obsessed with and...

## Position

Marc Andreessen's product-market fit is necessary but insufficient. Naval adds a third dimension: product-market-founder fit â€” how well the founder is personally suited to the specific business. A founder with deep personal alignment to the problem, the market, and the product type will outperform a generically skilled founder pursuing the same opportunity. This should be the first filter before choosing what to build.

## Evidence Cited

- Marc Andreessen's product-market fit as the starting definition Naval extends
- Naval's framing: "what is something where there is a market emerging, a product I can build, and I have specific knowledge and I'm really into it"

## Logic Chain

1. Product-market fit tells you the product fits the market
2. Founder fit tells you whether this specific founder can execute in this specific context
3. A mismatched founder wastes their specific knowledge and intrinsic motivation advantages
4. Founder fit is the cheapest thing to optimize because it's determined before starting
5. Optimizing all three simultaneously maximizes the probability of breakthrough outcomes
