# David Deutsch - Destroying the means of error correction is the cardinal moral failure — political systems that eliminate it trap humanity in local minima

## Position

The deepest principle of morality is: do not destroy the means of error correction. Humans and human institutions always make mistakes — this is not a defect but a feature of fallible knowledge-creating beings. The only protection against mistakes is the ability to detect and correct them. Political systems that remove this ability — by eliminating elections, making revolution impossible, arming the ruling class against the population — trap society in whatever local minimum they happen to occupy. North Korea is the paradigm case. If too much of the world falls into this trap, humanity loses its most important discovery: the ability to make new discoveries. This is not merely a political observation but an epistemological one: error correction in politics is the same process as error correction in science, and both depend on the ability to criticize, challenge, and replace the existing regime. Anti-rational memes that restrict knowledge growth are as dangerous as physical catastrophes.

## Logic Chain

1. All humans and human institutions make mistakes — this is inevitable for fallible knowledge-creating beings
2. The only defense against mistakes is the ability to detect and correct them
3. Therefore preserving error-correction mechanisms is the foundational moral requirement
4. Political systems that eliminate error correction (no elections, revolution impossible, armed ruling class) permanently trap society in current error states
5. This is epistemologically equivalent to immunizing a theory against criticism — it cannot improve
6. If this pattern spreads to enough of the world, humanity loses the capability that makes all progress possible
7. Therefore political error correction is not just a nice-to-have but the precondition for all other goods
