# Naval - The double slit experiment proves the particle-wave distinction is a deficiency in classical catego...

## Position

The historical sequence of experiments reveals that the particle/wave binary is not a real division in nature but an artifact of classical physics categories that were never adequate. Newton treated light as corpuscular (particles); Young's double-slit experiment demonstrated interference patterns and seemed to prove light was a wave. Einstein's photoelectric effect proved photons were particles â€” they could knock individual electrons out of atoms the way billiard balls do, not the way ocean waves do. These two results appeared to contradict each other, so physicists tried to maintain both by assigning each to the appropriate context. But then quantum mechanics discovered that electrons â€” which have mass, charge, and spin, which are unambiguously particles in every classical sense â€” also produce interference patterns when sent through a double slit. The contradiction is not between light-as-particle and light-as-wave. It is between the category "particle" and the category "wave" themselves. Both categories fail to describe what is actually happening. The multiverse interpretation resolves this: a single electron in this branch interferes with versions of itself in adjacent branches, producing the pattern. The classical categories do not break down because our instruments are inadequate; they break down because they were never descriptions of fundamental reality.
