# Naval - ChatGPT succeeded over Google by being simpler — even the simplest product in the internet (a searc...

## Position

Google was considered the simplest possible internet product â€” just a box. But Naval argues it was not actually simple: users had to learn keyword syntax, could not ask natural questions, received a ranked list of links requiring further judgment (distinguishing ads from organic results, clicking through, reading), and then synthesizing the answer themselves. ChatGPT removed all of that: you talk to it like a human and receive a direct answer. The conversational interface eliminates the keyword syntax requirement, the link-scanning step, and the synthesizing step. This represents a genuine simplicity improvement, not just a cosmetic one â€” it reduces the cognitive steps between question and answer from five or more to one.

## Logic Chain

1. Google appeared simple (a single box) but required trained behavior: keyword syntax, scanning results, distinguishing ads, clicking through
2. Each of these steps is a cognitive burden that users learned to accept as normal
3. ChatGPT replaces all of those steps with one: ask a question in natural language, receive a direct answer
4. This is a genuine reduction in complexity, not just a styling change
5. Therefore: ChatGPT's early adoption success is explained by a fundamental simplicity advantage over the workflow Google required
