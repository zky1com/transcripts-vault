# Naval - The company transformation threshold is not a headcount number but the moment the founder stops dir...

## Position

The conventional wisdom that companies change at headcount milestones (20, 30, 40 employees) is wrong. The real threshold is the moment the founder is no longer personally involved in recruiting and directly managing everyone. Once middle layers of management form, the founder is disconnected from the company â€” the ability to directly drive a zero-to-one product team goes away. There is now a "fly-by-wire" element: a mechanical linkage through other humans who lack the founder's selectivity and judgment.

- "there's now a fly by wire element in between â€” some mechanical linkage going through another human, often at a distance"
- Outsourced recruiters lack the founder's level of selectivity

## Logic Chain

1. Company culture and quality are transmitted primarily through direct personal selection â€” who you choose to hire
2. When a third party makes hiring decisions, they necessarily apply lower selectivity because their judgment and standards differ
3. The first hired-without-founder-involvement person sets a new, lower bar
4. That bar then propagates as that person hires more people
5. Therefore the zero-to-one phase ends precisely when the founder's direct hiring involvement ends, regardless of headcount
