# Naval - Knowledge grows by error correction not accumulation of certainty — all knowledge is conjectural and survives by failing to be refuted

## Position

Popper's and Deutsch's replacement for justified true belief is fallibilism: all knowledge is conjectural — our best guess at the time, not a collection of verified certainties. A conjecture is not arbitrary just because it is a guess. It has survived systematic attempts to show it false; other conjectures have been eliminated because they could be shown to be false or had internal contradictions. The surviving conjecture is our current best knowledge — not because it has been proven true, but because it is the best explanation that has not yet been refuted. This framing has a crucial advantage: it makes progress possible forever. If all knowledge is potentially false, then finding errors and correcting them is always valid. The history of science is the history of error correction — Newton corrected Aristotle, Einstein corrected Newton — and this process can continue without end. If any knowledge were truly final, the process would stop there.

## Evidence Cited

- "all we have are guesses about reality conjectures...it's a guess that has stood up against trials against attempts to show that it's false and when people are unable to show that it's false via this method of refutation then we accept it as a piece of knowledge"
- "this allows us to thereby accept the fact that we're going to be able to make progress in the future because all of our knowledge is conjectural all of it is our best guess at the time and therefore there's this elasticity within the knowledge"

## Logic Chain

1. All knowledge = conjectural (best current guess), not justified true belief
2. A conjecture earns its status by surviving serious refutation attempts — not by being proven
3. Other conjectures are eliminated when they are shown to be false or contradictory
4. This process is permanent: no conjecture is immune from future refutation
5. Therefore progress is always possible — there is always more error to correct
6. Historical pattern: Newton improved on Aristotle, Einstein improved on Newton, quantum mechanics improved on Newton again — error correction all the way down
