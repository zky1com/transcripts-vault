# Molly Bloom - High-stakes private poker works because it sells access belonging and fantasy as much as it sells cards

## Position
Molly Bloom says the appeal of elite private poker was never just the game itself but the social access, belonging, escapism, and fantasy built around it.

## Evidence Cited
- She says she realized the business was not just about poker.
- She says she wanted players to feel like they had entered Monaco or a James Bond movie.
- She describes the room as a Trojan horse into power and culture.

## Logic Chain
1. Bloom is describing the product as a designed social experience, not a neutral card table.
2. She emphasizes status and atmosphere as part of the value proposition.
3. That supports the claim that access and fantasy were central to the business.
