# Naval - If any task can be outsourced for less than your hourly rate you should outsource it even before yo...

## Position

Any task that can be hired out for less than your aspirational hourly rate should be outsourced regardless of current wealth level. This includes cooking, errands, repairs. The mental and physical energy available for high-output work is limited; spending it on low-value tasks is exactly equivalent to throwing money away at the rate of your hourly value.

## Evidence Cited

- Naval preferred hiring an assistant even when he had no money
- Would rather give items to Salvation Army than spend time returning them
- Paul Graham's advice: YC founders should only work on product-market fit and health â€” that's all the time you have

## Logic Chain

1. High-output mental hours are scarce and non-renewable within a day
2. Every hour spent on a $15/hour task is an hour not spent on a $1,000/hour task
3. If a task can be delegated for under your rate, not delegating it is net-negative even if you currently lack money
4. The correct framework: outsourcing is not about affordability but about opportunity cost at your target rate
