# Naval - The ultimate purpose of money is freedom from obligation — not having to be in a specific place at...

## Position

Naval defines the terminal value of wealth not as consumption capacity or status, but as freedom from obligation. Specifically: not being required to be at a particular location, at a particular time, performing a particular activity against your will. This is a precise, minimal definition that focuses on optionality rather than acquisition. It explains why income without freedom (a high-paying job you hate) is less valuable than it appears, and why passive income, equity, and location-independent income streams are categorically superior to employment compensation.

## Evidence Cited

- "to me the ultimate purpose of money is so that you do not have to be in a specific place at a specific time doing anything you don't want to do"
- "money buys you freedom in the material world"
- "money lets you not do the things that you don't want to do"

## Logic Chain

1. Money has instrumental value: it can be traded for goods, time, and options
2. The highest-value thing it can purchase is freedom â€” the ability to choose how you spend your time
3. This freedom is maximally defined as absence of constraint: no required location, no required time, no required activity
4. Employment trades this freedom for income, which is often a poor trade at high income levels
5. Therefore: the goal of wealth creation is to reach the point where no external party can mandate your location or activity
