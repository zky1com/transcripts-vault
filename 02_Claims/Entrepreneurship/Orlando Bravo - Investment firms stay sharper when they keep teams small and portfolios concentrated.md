# Orlando Bravo - Investment firms stay sharper when they keep teams small and portfolios concentrated

## Position
Orlando Bravo says investment organizations perform better when they stay small enough to remain externally focused and concentrated enough to mentor talent and know each asset well.

## Evidence Cited
- He says big teams become internally focused and start having too many internal conversations.
- He says his firm keeps a small team and a concentrated portfolio by design.
- He ties that structure to better mentoring and better company attention.

## Logic Chain
1. Bravo sees organizational sprawl as a threat to judgment and execution.
2. He prefers fewer deals and fewer people so the firm stays close to companies and markets.
3. That supports the claim that small teams and concentration are strategic advantages.
