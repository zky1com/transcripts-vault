# Naval - Advice from someone who got rich quickly is just their winning lottery ticket numbers not transfera...

## Position

When someone who succeeded quickly tells you what they did, they're reciting the specific conditions that happened to work for them â€” many of which were non-repeatable luck, timing, or unique personal circumstance. This is like reading winning lottery numbers: they won with those numbers, but that doesn't mean those numbers will work again or for you. The advice isn't wrong, but it's context-specific. The pattern doesn't generalize; only the principles underlying the pattern (if you can identify them) might.

## Evidence Cited

- "you can't just take their exact circumstance and map it onto yours"

## Logic Chain

1. Fast wealth creation is often a combination of systematic skill and circumstantial luck (timing, market conditions, specific relationships)
2. The person who succeeded cannot easily distinguish which parts were skill vs. luck
3. They report the full combination as "what I did" â€” including the unrepeatable parts
4. A listener applying those exact steps inherits neither the luck nor the circumstance
5. Therefore: stories of fast success are data points (anecdotes) not generalizable lessons unless the underlying mechanism is explicitly identified
