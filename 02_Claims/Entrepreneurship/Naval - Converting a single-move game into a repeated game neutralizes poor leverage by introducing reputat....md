# Naval - Converting a single-move game into a repeated game neutralizes poor leverage by introducing reputat...

## Position

When you have poor negotiating leverage or are in a low-information transaction (like hiring a contractor), the best remedy is converting the relationship from a single transaction to a repeated game. In single-move games, cheating is rational because there are no future consequences. In repeated games, reputation becomes a real cost â€” the cheating party loses all future transactions with you, your network, and anyone who reads their public reviews. Practical tactics: split a project into phases, bring in future referrals, operate within a community, or leave a public review.

## Evidence Cited

- Kitchen renovation as a high-cost, low-information single-move game where both sides have historically cheated
- Tactics: split work into two projects; bring 3 friends as future referrals; operate within a reputation community; Yelp/Thumbtack review
- "what you're basically doing is trying to convert a single move expensive game... into a multi move game"

## Logic Chain

1. In a one-shot game, the expected value of cheating can exceed the expected value of honesty (no future punishment)
2. In a repeated game, cheating destroys all future value â€” making honesty the dominant strategy
3. Therefore: converting the transaction's structure to a repeated game changes the incentives, not the people
4. This works even with strangers â€” reputation systems (Yelp, Thumbtack) effectively create repeated-game dynamics without a prior relationship
