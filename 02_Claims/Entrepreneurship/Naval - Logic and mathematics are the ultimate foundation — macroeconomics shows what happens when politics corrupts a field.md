# Naval - Logic and mathematics are the ultimate foundation — macroeconomics shows what happens when politics corrupts a field

## Position

The most durable intellectual foundations are those that can be attacked or defended from any angle — where understanding is demonstrated by the ability to reconstruct an argument from first principles rather than by memorizing it. Logic and mathematics have this property. Macroeconomics does not: it is entangled with political agendas, produces no consensus, and cannot be falsified in practice. The existence of modern monetary theory — which essentially argues that deficits don't matter except for inflation — is Naval's evidence that macroeconomics has been fully corrupted into a branch of politics. A real science converges; a corrupt one proliferates contradictory theories each backed by a different political camp. Starting from logic and mathematics inoculates against this: it builds the habit of demanding that arguments be reconstructable and defendable from first principles, which political pseudo-science cannot survive.

## Evidence Cited

- "the ultimate foundation are mathematics and logic — if you understand logic and mathematics then you have the basis for understanding the scientific method"
- "macroeconomics is voodoo — complex science meets politics — you can't find two macroeconomists to agree on anything"
- "the fact that there are people who have macroeconomist in their title and are peddling modern monetary theory just tells you that macroeconomics as a so-called science has been corrupted — it's a branch of politics"
- "foundational things are principles, there are algorithms, they're deep-seated logical understanding where you can defend it or attack it from any angle"

## Logic Chain

1. Real knowledge = understanding that can be reconstructed and defended from any angle (not just memorized)
2. Logic and mathematics have this property — every step is derivable, every claim is verifiable
3. Once you have logic + mathematics, scientific method becomes comprehensible (it is an application of logical structure)
4. Once you have scientific method, you can evaluate any field's truth claims
5. Macroeconomics fails this test: no convergence, cannot be falsified, supports contradictory theories for political patrons
6. MMT example: claims deficits are fine "except for inflation" — the exception is the whole point; not dismissing it is not scientific reasoning
7. Starting from first principles (logic, math) builds the habit of demanding reconstructable arguments → immunizes against politically motivated pseudo-science
