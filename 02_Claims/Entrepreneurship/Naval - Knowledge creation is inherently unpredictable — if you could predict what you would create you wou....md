## Position

The future growth of knowledge is structurally impossible to predict, and this is not a limitation of our forecasting methods â€” it is a logical consequence of what knowledge creation is. A new idea is something that did not exist before. If you could fully specify it in advance, you would have already had it: prediction of a creative act is the act. Therefore any model that claims to predict what specific knowledge will be created is self-refuting. This principle has profound implications for pessimistic forecasting. Doomsday predictions are always projections of current trajectories without the creative solutions that will in fact intervene. Every generation has produced Malthusian catastrophists who turned out to be wrong, not because their arithmetic was faulty, but because they could not model the knowledge that would be created to solve the problems they extrapolated. The unpredictability of creativity is not a bug in our forecasting â€” it is the defining feature of knowledge as a phenomenon.
