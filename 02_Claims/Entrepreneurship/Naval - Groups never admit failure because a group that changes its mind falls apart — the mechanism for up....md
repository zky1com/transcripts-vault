# Naval - Groups never admit failure because a group that changes its mind falls apart — the mechanism for up...

## Position

Individuals can be forced to admit they were wrong. Groups cannot, because a group is held together by its shared beliefs â€” revising the core belief dissolves the basis of the group's identity. Therefore, the group's rational strategy is to maintain its beliefs indefinitely and declare victory regardless of outcomes. When reality becomes too divergent from the group's beliefs, the mechanism for updating is not consensus revision but schism: the group splits, with one fragment holding the old belief and a new fragment adopting a modification. The Catholic/Protestant split, crypto forks, and political party splits all follow this pattern.

## Logic Chain

1. A group's cohesion depends on shared beliefs â€” these beliefs are the membership criterion
2. Revising a core belief means some members accept the revision and others don't
3. The group cannot simultaneously hold two contradictory beliefs â€” it splits
4. Therefore: from the group's perspective, the stable move is to not revise the core belief
5. Change occurs through schism (fork/split), not through centralized belief revision
