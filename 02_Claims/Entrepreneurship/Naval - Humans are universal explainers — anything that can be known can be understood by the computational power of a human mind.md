# Naval - Humans are universal explainers — anything that can be known can be understood by the computational power of a human mind

## Position

Deutsch's concept of "universal explainer" makes a strong claim: there is no knowable phenomenon that falls outside the cognitive reach of a human mind. This is the Popperian-Deutschian response to the pessimistic view that future physics or alien intelligence might be comprehensible to more advanced minds but not to us. Universal explainers can, in principle, understand anything — not instantly or easily, but in the sense that no permanent cognitive ceiling blocks access to any knowable truth. This is not a claim that humans are the most intelligent beings possible; it is a claim about the qualitative power of the human reasoning apparatus. Because humans can form good explanations, and because good explanations can in principle reach any knowable truth, there is no ceiling on what humans can eventually understand given sufficient time and effort.

## Evidence Cited

- "humans are universal explainers — that anything that can be known and understood can be known and understood by human beings in the computation power of a human system — it's all knowable by humans"
- "good explanations have tremendous reach — they are acts of creativity"
- "we're at the beginning of an infinity of knowledge — as we understand things using good explanations and we create new theories that are constantly being destroyed and replaced by better ones — there's no endpoint in sight"

## Logic Chain

1. Human minds can form good explanations — models of unseen causes that generate testable predictions
2. Good explanations have unlimited reach: they can model any domain in terms of underlying causes
3. "Universal explainer" = a system capable of constructing good explanations about any knowable domain
4. Humans qualify: given sufficient time, any human can in principle understand any knowable truth
5. This is not a claim of cognitive perfection — it is a claim of qualitative sufficiency
6. Contrast: most animals are domain-specific learners (learn their niche but not others); humans are not domain-specific
7. Therefore: no permanently inaccessible truth exists for human minds — the ceiling is removed
