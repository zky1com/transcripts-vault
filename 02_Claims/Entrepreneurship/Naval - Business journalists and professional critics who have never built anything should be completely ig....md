# Naval - Business journalists and professional critics who have never built anything should be completely ig...

## Position

Business journalists and economists who critique private companies or speculate about markets are professional critics with no skin in the game and no building experience. Reading them actively makes you dumber. Their incentive is page views not accuracy. On private company judgment specifically, they have zero relevant expertise.

## Evidence Cited

- Reference to economists railing against Bitcoin and journalists attacking pre-IPO companies
- Taleb quote: "if you want to be a philosopher king, first become a king, then become a philosopher"
- Taleb's "Fooled by Randomness" as cautionary frame

## Logic Chain

1. Expertise in critique requires no expertise in the domain being critiqued
2. Journalists are incentivized for engagement (outrage/controversy) not accuracy
