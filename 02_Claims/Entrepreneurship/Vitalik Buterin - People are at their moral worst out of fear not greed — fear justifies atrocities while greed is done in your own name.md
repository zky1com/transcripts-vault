# Vitalik Buterin - People are at their moral worst out of fear not greed — fear justifies atrocities while greed is done in your own name

## Position

The conventional assumption is that people do terrible things because of greed — self-interest at others' expense. Vitalik argues the mechanism runs the other way: the worst collective behaviors are driven by fear, not greed. Greed is transactional — you do it in your own name, which limits its scope and makes it relatively transparent. Fear is collective — it is always articulated as concern for everyone else ("we must do this to protect the tribe/children/country"), which enables it to recruit wider participation, justify measures that individuals would never undertake for personal gain, and insulate leaders from accountability because they claim to be acting selflessly. This explains the Patriot Act, COVID lockdowns, community toxicity in crypto, and geopolitical aggression: these are all fear-driven rather than greed-driven phenomena.

## Evidence Cited

- "one of the things that I feel like I've learned over the last decade is that people are at their morally worst not out of greed but out of fear"
- "fear is always done in everybody else's name — so you end up with a whole bunch of self-styled white knights battling each other"
- "greed is always done in your own name and nobody wants to do it in their own name"
- "fear is used to justify all the horrible things — from the Patriot Act to the COVID lockdowns — fear drives it all"
- "even when the leader actually is greedy and megalomaniacal, fear is what they use to sell it"

## Logic Chain

1. Greed: self-interested action → done in actor's name → visible → limited in scale → constrained by accountability
2. Fear: collective danger framing → done in name of protecting others → recruits white knights → bypasses accountability
3. Fear-driven actors present themselves as selfless → harder to oppose ("you're pro-hacker if you oppose the fork")
4. Even greedy leaders use fear as their public justification → the mechanism is fear regardless of private motivation
5. This applies across domains: crypto communities, geopolitics, security legislation, pandemic policy
6. Result: collective behavior in crisis is almost always worse than individual behavior → fear amplifies beyond greed
