## Position

Navigating the idea maze requires frequent backtracking, side turns, and abandonment of earlier beliefs. The biggest impediment to this is ego/pride: the founder's investment in being right about their original vision makes it psychologically costly to change direction. Founders who stay locked into their initial thesis stop navigating and start defending â€” which is the opposite of what the maze requires. The correct mental model is that all work is experimental and most of it will be thrown away; the skill is distilling the insights from each failed experiment.
