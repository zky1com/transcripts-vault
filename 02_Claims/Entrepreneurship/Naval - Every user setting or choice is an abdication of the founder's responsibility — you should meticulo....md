# Naval - Every user setting or choice is an abdication of the founder's responsibility — you should meticulo...

## Position

Naval's sharpest product principle: choices given to users are not features â€” they are failures. When a product puts a setting in the preferences menu, it is the founder admitting they could not decide what the right answer was and delegated the decision to the user. Users, especially in the era of TikTok and ChatGPT, do not want cognitive load. They want the right defaults. The product job is to figure out what those defaults are and enforce them. Every extra click, button, or setting that doesn't directly serve the product's core purpose should be meticulously removed. Simplicity is not a style choice; it is a discipline of decision-making on behalf of the user.
