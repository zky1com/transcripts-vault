# Kapil Gupta - The solution to any problem lies inside the problem — understanding what anxiety is ends it more surely than any treatment

## Position

The standard approach to problems — seek a solution, apply a treatment, manage the symptom — is the wrong approach because it treats solution and problem as independent things. In reality, the solution to any problem is embedded within the problem itself: deeply understanding the problem is how it is resolved. Applied to anxiety: the problem is not "how do I get rid of anxiety?" but "what is anxiety, when does it arise, what part of me does it originate from?" The person who truly understands anxiety in this way finds it dissolves — not because they applied a technique, but because understanding is itself the resolution. This is the difference between a cure and a treatment. Treatments (therapy, meditation, medication, journaling) manage symptoms indefinitely. Understanding produces a permanent resolution: the problem does not return because it was understood at its root, not suppressed or worked around. The destination is real and reachable — the "journey forever" framing is a social lie invented by those selling treatments.

- "the way is actually to learn where anxiety comes from the people who have conquered anxiety are the ones who had a clear understanding of what it is and where it came from"
- "know the face of anxiety not run from it like a boogie man"
- "there is a destination it isn't about spending the next 90 years getting assaulted and then feeling better"
- "you want to get to the point where you don't have it anymore you aren't looking for treatments you're looking for a cure"

## Logic Chain

1. Standard problem-solving treats solution and problem as separate things — apply X to remove Y
2. But for internal problems, the solution is embedded inside the problem — understanding the problem is the resolution
3. Anxiety example: the person asking "how do I get rid of anxiety?" is asking the wrong question
4. The right question: "what is anxiety, when does it arise, what part of me does it come from?"
5. Deep understanding of the structure of anxiety dissolves it — not through technique but through clarity
6. This produces permanent resolution (cure) rather than ongoing management (treatment)
7. The "work on it forever" framing is useful only to those selling perpetual treatments, not to the person seeking freedom
8. Practical domains (making money) are understood as destination-oriented; internal domains should be too
