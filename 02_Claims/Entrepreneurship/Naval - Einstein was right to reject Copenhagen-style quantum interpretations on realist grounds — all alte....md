# Naval - Einstein was right to reject Copenhagen-style quantum interpretations on realist grounds — all alte...

## Position

Naval defends Einstein's famous realist dissent from quantum mechanics ("God does not play dice with the universe") on Deutschian grounds. When the founders of quantum theory developed Copenhagen-style interpretations, Einstein rejected them not because he was being stubborn or ignorant, but because none of them provided a realistic explanation of what was physically happening. They were stories that worked for calculation purposes but that required accepting fundamentally non-physical premises (the act of observation collapsing a wave function, for example). Einstein was right: none of those alternatives make any sense as physical explanations. The resolution came only in the 1950s when Hugh Everett devised the many-worlds (multiverse) interpretation â€” a realistic explanation where the wave function never collapses but continues evolving, and what we call "observation" is the splitting of a branch of the multiverse.
