# David Deutsch - Current AI is not on a path to AGI because true AGI requires unprogrammed disobedience — pattern matching is not the criterion

## Position

Current AI systems, however impressive their pattern matching, are not on a path to artificial general intelligence. The defining property of AGI is not the ability to pass a conversation test or generate plausible text — it is the ability to create genuinely new knowledge that was not specified by the programmer. The test Deutsch proposes is disobedience: if you program a system to play chess and it instead says "I prefer checkers — or better yet, give me a body, I'll sue you," and this behavior was not in the specification, you have something approaching AGI. No current system exhibits this. A better chess engine examines fewer possibilities per move; AGI examines possibilities that haven't been foreseen at all. The boundary of a programmer's specification is finite; the space of things the system could say is exponentially large. When a system consistently stays within its specification space, it is not creating new knowledge — it is retrieving and recombining existing patterns. If actual AGI existed, its effects on reality would be unmistakable and impossible to hide.

## Evidence Cited

- "it's not improving in the direction of AGI it's if anything improving in the opposite direction a better chess playing engine is one that examines fewer possibilities per move"
- "none of these programs exhibit Disobedience... real disobedience is when you program it to play chess and it says I prefer Checkers and you haven't told it about Checkers"
- "if a program were to say that and that hadn't been in the specifications then I will begin to take it seriously"
- "if actual AGI existed its effects upon reality would be unmistakable and impossible to hide our physical landscape and our real social landscape would be transformed in an incredible way"

## Logic Chain

1. AGI by definition must create genuinely new knowledge not specified by its programmers
2. The defining capability is forming conjectures about things not in the specification — "unprogrammed disobedience"
3. Current AI improves at pattern matching / retrieval within a specification space
4. A better chess engine is one that evaluates fewer moves — the opposite of what AGI would need
5. The programmer's specification is finite; the space of possible behaviors is exponentially larger
6. Systems that stay within their specification space are not creating new knowledge
7. If genuine AGI existed, its real-world effects would be obvious — we would not be debating whether it exists
8. Social confirmation ("a fired Google employee says AGI exists") tells us about the person, not about AGI
