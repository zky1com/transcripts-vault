# Naval - The best people only want to work with the best people so anyone on your team you would flinch at h...

## Position

The best people are only motivated to stay when surrounded by peers of equal or higher quality. Any person below that bar is a cognitive load and a signal to top performers that they should be elsewhere. Naval's practical test: when recruiting a new person, tell them to walk into the team room, pick anyone at random, and interview them for 30 minutes â€” if they're not impressed, they shouldn't join. The critical insight: if the founder internally flinches at this test because of a specific team member, that's the person who needs to go. This is the bar that must be maintained to keep the team self-reinforcing.

## Logic Chain

1. Top performers evaluate their environment partly by the quality of peers around them
2. A single below-standard person signals to all top performers that standards have been compromised
3. Once standards slip, top performers update their beliefs about the company's quality floor
4. The flinch test externalizes the founder's internal knowledge of who the weak link is
5. Removing that person is necessary to maintain the self-reinforcing high-quality culture
