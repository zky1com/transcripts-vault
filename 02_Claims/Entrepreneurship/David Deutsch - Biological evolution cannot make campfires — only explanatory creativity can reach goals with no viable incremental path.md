# David Deutsch - Biological evolution cannot make campfires — only explanatory creativity can reach goals with no viable incremental path

## Position

Biological evolution is fundamentally limited because it has no foresight: it can only reach destinations accessible via a sequence of incremental steps each of which yields a viable organism. Human explanatory creativity faces no such constraint — it can conjecture and reach solutions that have no viable path of incremental improvements leading to them. The campfire is the paradigm case: no partial campfire confers any survival advantage, so there is no incremental evolutionary path to campfire-making. By contrast, the Bombardier beetle's boiling-water defense evolved because squirting cold water at enemies is already somewhat useful, then slightly warmer water, etc. Billions of species over billions of years, and not one ever made a campfire. Humans got there because explanatory creativity can jump directly to things that have no viable incremental path. This is Charles Darwin's insight — evolution creates knowledge before natural selection begins — and it marks the decisive difference between biological and human knowledge creation.

## Evidence Cited

- "of all the billions and billions of species that have ever existed none of them has ever made a campfire even though many of them would have been helped by having the genetic capacity to make campfires"
- "the reason it didn't happen in the biosphere is that there is no such thing as making a partially functional campfire"
- "humans have the explanatory creativity and once you have that you can get to the moon you can cause asteroids which are heading towards the Earth to turn round and go away"
- "biological evolution can't reach places that are not reachable by successive improvements that Each of which allows a viable organism to exist"

## Logic Chain

1. Biological evolution creates new knowledge through random variation + natural selection
2. Each intermediate step must yield a viable organism — there must be an incremental path to the solution
3. Campfires have no partial precursor — a "slightly functional campfire" does not help survival
4. Therefore no evolutionary path to campfire-making exists, and in ~4 billion years no species found one
5. Human explanatory creativity can form conjectures about things that don't yet exist and for which no incremental path exists
6. Therefore human knowledge creation is categorically more powerful than biological evolution
7. Corollary: anything physically possible is eventually achievable by humans through knowledge; biological evolution cannot make that claim
