# Naval - Science is not about extrapolating trends but explaining mechanisms — only after explanation can reliable prediction follow

## Position

The popular conception of science as trend-detection and prediction reverses the actual epistemic order. Science first produces an explanation — a model of the underlying mechanism that makes observed events intelligible — and only then can reliable predictions follow from that explanation. Without the explanation, predictions are trend extrapolations that will fail at regime changes. The boiling water case shows the sequence: the "science" of water heating is not "temperature rises 10° per minute therefore keep rising" — it is the particle kinetics story (kinetic energy, escape velocity, latent heat) that explains why water heats at that rate and why it must plateau at 100°C. The prediction flows from the explanation. This means: generating the correct explanation requires creativity (you have to invent the right model, not read it off from data), and predictions are only as reliable as the explanatory model they are derived from.

## Evidence Cited

- "science is not about predicting where the trend starts and where the trend goes"
- "that whole complicated story about how the particles are moving faster — this invocation of the term latent heat — it's not about trends and predictions, it's about explanation"
- "only once we have the explanation can we in fact make the prediction"

## Logic Chain

1. Naive model: science = collect data → find trend → predict future data
2. Failure: trends break at regime changes; no trend data can predict when/why regimes change
3. Correct model: science = invent explanatory mechanism → derive predictions from mechanism
4. Explanatory mechanism: particle kinetics (kinetic energy increases with temperature; at escape velocity, phase transition; latent heat absorbed; temperature plateaus)
5. This mechanism correctly predicts both the linear rise and the plateau — the trend model predicts neither discontinuity
6. Predictions derived from explanation are reliable because they encode the mechanism, not just past regularity
7. Generating the explanation requires creativity — it cannot be extracted algorithmically from data
