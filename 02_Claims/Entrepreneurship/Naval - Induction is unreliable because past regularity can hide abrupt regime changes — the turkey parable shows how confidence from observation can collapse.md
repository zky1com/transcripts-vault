# Naval - Induction is unreliable because past regularity can hide abrupt regime changes — the turkey parable shows how confidence from observation can collapse

## Position

The turkey parable makes the limits of induction vivid: a turkey is fed well every day, and from this repeated observation builds a strong inductive theory that it lives in a benevolent household. The theory is strengthened by each new day of feeding. On the morning of Thanksgiving, the theory is refuted catastrophically. The problem is not bad luck — it is a structural flaw in induction: you cannot distinguish "this regularity will continue" from "this regularity applies until the hidden regime-change condition is met." Past observations cannot reveal the existence of regime-change conditions that have not yet triggered. Induction looks reliable until it isn't, and it fails precisely at the moments when you most need it to be reliable. This is why knowledge must be conjectural (going beyond the data) and falsifiability-tested (designed to reveal what conditions would break it) rather than inductively derived.

## Evidence Cited

- "the fun and easy one is the turkey you could have a turkey that's being fed very well every single day and fattened up and it thinks that it belongs and lives in a benevolent household where the farmer comes and feeds it every day until thanksgiving arrives and then it's in for a very rude awakening"
- "that shows you the limits of induction precisely the theories have to be guessed"

## Logic Chain

1. Turkey: daily feeding → inductive theory "I live in a benevolent environment" → gets stronger every day
2. Thanksgiving: theory catastrophically refuted by a regime-change condition that was invisible to induction
3. Induction cannot distinguish "regularity that continues" from "regularity that continues until hidden condition triggers"
4. The hidden condition (Thanksgiving) was present all along but invisible to the inductive method
5. Therefore: induction fails at exactly the moments it matters most — regime changes and structural breaks
6. Correct epistemology: produce theories (guesses) that include mechanisms for detecting hidden conditions; test by trying to falsify
