# Naval - To a caveman very few things are resources — what counts as a resource depends entirely on available knowledge

## Position

A resource is not an intrinsic property of matter; it is a relational property: matter becomes a resource when humans know how to extract, transform, and apply it. A caveman living on the same planet as modern civilization had access to almost no resources: a few edible plants and animals. The oil beneath his feet, the iron ore in nearby hills, the uranium in the ground — all worthless because he lacked the knowledge to use them. The same atoms, the same planet, radically different resource status. This demonstrates that resource availability is a function of knowledge, not of physical abundance. When people say "we are running out of resources," they are really saying "we have reached the limits of our current knowledge about how to use what exists." This framing makes resource pessimism a knowledge-pessimism claim, which can be refuted by pointing to the historical record of knowledge expansion continuously unlocking new resources.

## Evidence Cited

- "to a caveman very few things are resources — just a few edible plants and a few edible animals and that's it"
- "a resource is just something that through knowledge you can convert from one thing to another"
- "uranium has gone from being completely worthless to being an incredible resource"
- "domestication, harvesting crops, metallurgy, chemistry, physics, developing engines and rockets — all of these are things that are taking things that we thought were worthless and turning them to resources"

## Logic Chain

1. Definition: resource = matter that knowledge can convert from one form to usable/valuable form
2. Caveman: same planet, same atoms → very few resources (lacks knowledge of most transformations)
3. Modern civilization: vastly more knowledge → same atoms → vastly more resources
4. Historical examples: coal (useless → industrial revolution); iron (useless → metallurgy); uranium (useless → nuclear energy)
5. Therefore: resource availability = f(knowledge), not f(physical abundance)
6. "Running out of resources" = "reached limits of current knowledge" → refutable by knowledge expansion
7. The boundary can also be moved by redefining scope: town → country → planet → solar system → galaxy
