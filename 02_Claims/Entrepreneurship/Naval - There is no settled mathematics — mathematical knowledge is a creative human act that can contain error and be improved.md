# Naval - There is no settled mathematics — mathematical knowledge is a creative human act that can contain error and be improved

## Position

Mathematics is commonly treated as the one domain where knowledge is final — where what has been proved is certainly true. This is wrong. Mathematical knowledge is produced by human mathematicians using creative reasoning; it is not read off from an external source of necessary truth. Like any human creative activity, it can contain errors (in proofs, in axioms, in reasoning chains). Mathematical history includes cases where long-accepted proofs were later found to be flawed. More fundamentally, Gödel's incompleteness theorem shows that no formal system can be both complete and consistent — there will always be true statements the system cannot prove. This means mathematics is always open-ended: there are always more truths to reach, always the possibility of finding errors in existing work, always new creative steps to take. The idea of "settled mathematics" is a misconception — it confuses the subject matter (necessary truth) with our fallible knowledge of it.

## Logic Chain

1. Mathematics subject matter = necessary truth (what is necessarily the case)
2. Our knowledge of it = theorems and proofs constructed by fallible human mathematicians
3. Human mathematicians can make errors in proofs → existing theorems may contain hidden errors
4. Gödel: no consistent formal system is complete → there are truths the system cannot prove from its own axioms
5. Mathematics is therefore always open: new truths remain to be proved, existing proofs may contain errors
6. Mathematics is a creative act: new theorems must be invented/discovered, not passively received
7. Creative acts are fallible → "settled mathematics" is a contradiction in terms
