# Naval - Learning necessarily involves throwing away most work so founders must make teams comfortable with...

## Position

As companies scale, employees hired by other employees come with an expectation that their work should not be discarded â€” they're optimized to impress their manager rather than serve the end outcome. This creates resistance to the experimental mode that made the company successful: the principal-agent problem applied to experimentation. The correct frame is that a startup is in a search/discovery process where throwing away failing ideas is the mechanism of learning, not a sign of failure. Founders must establish a culture where repeated small failure is expected and extracting insights from each failure is the measure of success, not the amount of work preserved.
