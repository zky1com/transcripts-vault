# Naval - School is primarily socialization compliance training and babysitting with a small amount of educat...

## Position

Compulsory schooling is sold as education but the actual curriculum of a school day consists mostly of: socialization (learning to function in peer groups), compliance training (following rules and authority), and babysitting (supervised time that allows parents to work). Actual knowledge transfer is a small fraction of the school day, delivered at the speed of the slowest student, covering mostly outdated subjects. The evidence for this is that homeschooled students consistently outperform peers on academic measures despite receiving far fewer hours of formal instruction â€” suggesting the school day's remaining time is not educationally productive.

## Logic Chain

1. If school were primarily about education, reducing school hours while maintaining quality instruction would produce the same outcomes
2. Homeschooled students receive fewer total hours but achieve comparable or better outcomes
3. This implies the additional hours in school are not educationally productive
4. Those hours serve other social functions: socialization, compliance training, and supervised childcare
5. Therefore: the school system's primary outputs are social, not educational, which explains both its near-universal adoption and its poor marginal educational productivity
