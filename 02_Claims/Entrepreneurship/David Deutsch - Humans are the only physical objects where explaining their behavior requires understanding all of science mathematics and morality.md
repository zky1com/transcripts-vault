# David Deutsch - Humans are the only physical objects where explaining their behavior requires understanding all of science mathematics and morality

## Position

Humans are exceptional not merely in degree but in kind. To explain the behavior of any other physical object — a quasar, a rock, a beetle — you need only a tiny sliver of physics. But to explain human behavior you need everything: general relativity (to understand why Einstein was taken to Sweden and given gold), mathematics (to understand a Fields Medal), morality, philosophy, and the whole of science. An alien observer trying to explain Earth would need to understand the entire accumulated body of human knowledge just to make sense of any single significant human event. This is not true of any other known physical system. It means humans are, in a specific and deep sense, the most consequential objects in the universe — the things that eventually determine what transformations occur within it.

- "to understand humans sufficiently well you must understand everything sufficiently well and humans are the only remaining physical systems that we know of in the universe of which that is true"
- "everything else is really inconsequential in that sense"

## Logic Chain

1. To explain a physical event involving humans (Einstein winning the Nobel Prize), an observer needs general relativity, the concept of scientific achievement, the history of science, etc.
2. This chain of required knowledge has no natural stopping point — it expands to encompass the entire structure of human knowledge
3. For every other physical object — quasars, planets, animals — only a small domain of physics is needed to predict or explain behavior
4. Therefore humans are categorically different: they are universal in the sense that their behavior requires universal knowledge to explain
5. This makes humans the most consequential physical systems we know of — they are the entities that transform everything else
