# Naval - Democracy is a mechanism for removing bad rulers without violence not for selecting perfect ones

## Position

The classical question about democracy — who should rule? — is the wrong question. Plato's version sets up an impossible challenge: how do we identify and install the philosopher-king? Popper's reframing makes democracy tractable: the goal is not to select good rulers but to be able to remove bad ones without violence. This shifts the criterion from an impossible epistemic task (identifying the best ruler in advance) to a practical mechanism design task (building institutions that allow peaceful transitions of power). Different democracies can now be ranked: the better democracy is the one that removes unpopular or harmful rulers most quickly, cheaply, and peacefully — regardless of how "good" those rulers were when selected. This parallels science: science is not about finding the true theory but about efficiently eliminating false ones. Both are error-correction mechanisms, not truth-discovery machines.

## Evidence Cited

- "Popper questioned this whole idea — he went even deeper and said democracy has got nothing to do with who should rule — democracy is the system which allows you to remove policies and rulers most efficiently without violence"
- "to the extent that we're actually able to get rid of the people that we don't like from the democratic system quickly, efficiently, easily, without violence — that's the measure of a good democratic system"
- "rather than trying to figure out which system is going to give us the best rulers — that's the same mistake as saying what method of science is going to give us the true theory"

## Logic Chain

1. Classic question: who should rule? → requires identifying best ruler in advance → impossible epistemic task
2. Plato's answer: philosopher-kings → assumes someone can reliably identify wisdom → no reliable method exists
3. Popper's reframe: democracy's purpose is removing bad rulers without violence → mechanical criterion, not epistemic one
4. Evaluation criterion: how efficiently can bad rulers/policies be removed? → measurable, comparable across systems
5. Parallel to science: science goal = remove false theories efficiently; democracy goal = remove bad rulers efficiently
6. Both are error-correction mechanisms, not truth-selection mechanisms
