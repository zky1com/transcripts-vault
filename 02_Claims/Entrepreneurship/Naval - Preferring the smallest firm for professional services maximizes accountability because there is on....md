# Naval - Preferring the smallest firm for professional services maximizes accountability because there is on...

## Position

In large professional firms (law, banking, accounting), the principal sells the relationship but the actual work is done by an agent who cares far less. The bigger the firm, the greater the separation between who you hired and who actually works on your case. Naval prefers boutiques and ideally one-person firms: maximum accountability, nowhere to point fingers, one throat to choke.

## Evidence Cited

- Naval's personal heuristic: ideal law firm = 1 lawyer, ideal banker = solo banker
- Large firms: principal closes the sale, then hands off to junior agents
- Trade-off acknowledged: smaller firms have fewer resources â€” you bet big on one person

## Logic Chain

1. In large firms, rainmakers (principals) sell engagements but agents execute them
2. Agents care less; service quality degrades at the execution level
3. Small firms collapse the principal-agent gap: the person who sold is the person who executes
4. One person = maximum accountability, maximum incentive alignment
5. Trade-off: smaller resource pool and concentration risk, but better outcomes for complex, judgment-intensive work
