# Molly Bloom - Trust and reliable settlement are what keep private poker games alive after big losses

## Position
Molly Bloom says private poker games survive only when the operator protects trust, settles losses cleanly, and prevents one unpaid debt from breaking the whole ecosystem.

## Evidence Cited
- She says if one player stiffed the game, the whole game could break permanently.
- She explains that she often had to act as the bank and keep the table stable.
- She says trust problems in New York created an opening for her version of the game.

## Logic Chain
1. Bloom frames trust as the real operating infrastructure of private poker.
2. Settlement reliability matters because the financial and emotional stress after losses is extreme.
3. That supports the claim that trust and collection discipline keep the game alive.
