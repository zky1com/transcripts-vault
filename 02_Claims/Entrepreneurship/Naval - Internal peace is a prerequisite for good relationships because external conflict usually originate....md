# Naval - Internal peace is a prerequisite for good relationships because external conflict usually originate...

## Position

Naval argues that loving relationships fall out naturally from a calm internal state: once you are internally peaceful and financially stable, you will pick fewer fights, be more loving without conditions, and project warmth rather than need. Most interpersonal conflict is not caused by the other person â€” it is caused by the actor's own unresolved inner turmoil. Financial stress accounts for a large fraction of divorces; eliminating it removes that trigger. The remaining relational quality improvement comes from inner work.

## Evidence Cited

- "a lot of external battles happen because your internal state is not good"
- "when you're naturally internally peaceful you're gonna pick less fights you're gonna be more loving without expecting anything in return and that will take care of things on the external relationship front"
- "a lot of divorces actually happen over money... having money removes that part of it"
- "loving household and relationships actually automatically falls out of the other things if you have a calm mind and if you've already made money"

## Logic Chain

1. Interpersonal conflict has two proximate causes: financial stress and internal reactivity
2. Wealth eliminates the financial stress trigger
3. Internal peace (achieved through deliberate cultivation) eliminates the reactivity trigger
4. When both are removed, loving behavior emerges as the default state
5. Therefore: working on internal state and financial state are the primary levers for relationship quality
