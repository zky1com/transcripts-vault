# Naval - There can be no final theory of gravity — all we have is better and better approximations to reality

## Position

General relativity is our best current theory of gravity. It is better than Newtonian gravity in that it corrects the errors Newton's theory made (e.g., it correctly predicts the perihelion precession of Mercury and gravitational lensing). But this does not make general relativity final. For a theory to be final, it would need to be provably complete — and no theory can prove its own completeness. General relativity is inconsistent with quantum mechanics, suggesting it is an approximation of some deeper theory that unifies both. The Popperian conclusion is not pessimistic but optimistic: science proceeds by producing better approximations, each of which captures more of reality than its predecessor, with no endpoint. "Better" is a meaningful comparative judgment even without an achievable endpoint of "best."

## Logic Chain

1. Newton's gravity: good approximation; passed every test until 1919; but false in important ways (perihelion precession, gravitational lensing)
2. General relativity: corrects Newton's errors; passes every test to date
3. But GR is inconsistent with quantum mechanics → GR is an approximation of some deeper theory
4. Any "final" theory would require proof of completeness → no theory can prove its own completeness (Godel, and more practically, because completeness would require knowing all possible phenomena in advance)
5. Therefore: GR is better than Newton, but not final
6. The pattern iterates: Newton → GR → quantum gravity (TBD) → ...
7. Science = indefinitely improving approximations; "final theory" is not a meaningful scientific goal
