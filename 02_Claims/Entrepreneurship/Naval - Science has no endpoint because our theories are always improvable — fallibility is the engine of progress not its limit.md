# Naval - Science has no endpoint because our theories are always improvable — fallibility is the engine of progress not its limit

## Position

The fear that science might one day come to an end — that all phenomena might eventually be explained, leaving nothing left to discover — is based on a confused picture of what science is. If science produced final truths, it would eventually exhaust its subject matter. But science produces provisional explanations that can always be improved. Because scientists are fallible human beings, their theories are never perfect; because their theories are never perfect, there is always room to improve them; because there is always room to improve, science can never end. Fallibility, commonly treated as a regrettable limitation to be minimized, is actually the structural feature that makes indefinite progress possible. "We are at the beginning of infinity" means: the frontier of knowledge will always exist because every answer generates new questions and every explanation admits of improvement.

## Evidence Cited

- "we never have the final word — and that's a good thing — that's a really positive, optimistic thing — because it means we can keep on improving, we can keep on making progress and we keep on discovering new things"
- "there is no end of science"
- "we are at the beginning of infinity — and we will always be at the beginning of infinity — precisely because we can improve our ideas — because we're fallible human beings — so none of our theories are perfect"

## Logic Chain

1. If theories could be final: science would eventually exhaust all phenomena → endpoint is conceivable
2. But theories are produced by fallible humans → always contain potential errors
3. Errors can be corrected → every theory can in principle be improved
4. Improvement = progress → progress cannot end while improvement is always possible
5. Therefore: science has no endpoint — the frontier advances indefinitely
6. Fallibility is not the enemy of progress; it is the precondition for it — without imperfection, there is nothing to improve
7. "Beginning of infinity" = the frontier always exists; we are never near the end of possible knowledge
