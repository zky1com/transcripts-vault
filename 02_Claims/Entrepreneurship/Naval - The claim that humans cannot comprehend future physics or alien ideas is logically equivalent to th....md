# Naval - The claim that humans cannot comprehend future physics or alien ideas is logically equivalent to th...

## Position

Scientifically-minded people who claim that humans will be unable to understand the next generation of physics, or that alien civilizations will have ideas incomprehensible to us, are making a claim logically identical in structure to religious belief. The theistic claim is that God exists in a domain beyond human comprehension â€” supernatural, infinite, unknowable. The "incomprehensible aliens" claim says the same thing about a different entity: some minds exist with ideas that lie beyond the reach of human understanding. But if aliens are physical beings governed by the same laws of physics as we are, the claim that their ideas are categorically beyond us has no basis. The idea that physical beings within the same universe can develop ideas that are structurally incomprehensible to other physical beings within the same universe is incoherent. It posits a kind of cognitive transcendence that has no physical justification. It is, as Naval puts it, "nothing but the appeal to the supernatural."
