# Naval - Physical laws alone cannot explain human civilization — Manhattan requires invoking minds knowledge and choices not just physics

## Position

Reductionism holds that all phenomena can in principle be explained by the fundamental laws of physics. Naval (following Deutsch) argues this is false in practice and misleading in principle when applied to human-made structures. An astrophysicist can explain the shape of a galaxy through gravity and thermodynamics alone. But the laws of physics alone cannot explain why Manhattan looks the way it does. To explain Manhattan, you must invoke people — their scientific, philosophical, and political knowledge; their choices; the institutions they built; the economic incentives they faced. Omitting people from the explanation is not a simplification — it is a failure to identify the actual causal mechanism. Reductionism is useful for understanding natural physical systems, but it is wrong to apply it to systems where knowledge and choices are the primary causal drivers. The future evolution of the planet and beyond will increasingly depend on what people know and decide — which means understanding it requires understanding knowledge, not just physics.

## Evidence Cited

- "the laws of physics alone will not be able to explain the appearance of manhattan you have to invoke things other than merely the fundamental laws of physics you need to invoke the existence of people and their capacity to explain the world scientifically philosophically politically"
- "if we want to understand how the universe from this point onwards...is going to evolve over time we're going to have to talk about the knowledge that people create and the choices that they're going to make"

## Logic Chain

1. Reductionism: all phenomena explained by fundamental physical laws
2. Galaxy shape: explainable by gravity and thermodynamics alone — no minds required
3. Manhattan: not explainable by physics alone — the actual causal drivers are human knowledge and choices
4. Reductionism fails for human-created systems because it omits the causally primary mechanisms
5. Future trajectory of planet/solar system/galaxy: increasingly driven by human knowledge and decisions
6. Therefore: understanding the future requires understanding knowledge creation — not reducible to physics
