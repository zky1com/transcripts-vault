# Naval - Silicon Valley emerged because mobile creative workers chose the best unclaimed geography — not because of path dependency

## Position

The conventional explanation for Silicon Valley's dominance — universities, government semiconductor spending, historical accidents — is mostly wrong. These are reinforcing factors that matter only after the initial clustering. The primary cause is a selection mechanism: the tech workforce is uniquely mobile (small teams, digital tools, remote-work-capable), highly creative, and highly paid. People with these characteristics voted with their feet for the best available geography not already dominated by another industry. The SF Bay Area had the right combination: exceptional natural beauty, Mediterranean climate, second-densest US city concentration, access to high-quality food and environment, and crucially — no pre-existing dominant industry that would make tech workers second-class citizens. This is why you cannot move tech to New York (finance dominates) or LA (Hollywood dominates): the cultural and infrastructural environment already serves a different elite class.

## Evidence Cited

- "I don't think that Silicon Valley is Silicon Valley because a set of magic events happened at a particular place — rather I think it's driven by geography"
- "the tech people are the only ones who've been able to do remote work — they can also work in smaller teams — so they are more mobile — and these are very creative, very intelligent people"
- "I think it's natural for them to choose the San Francisco Bay Area — it's absolutely beautiful if you actually look at the physicality of it"
- "these people picked the best piece of land that was available to them"

## Logic Chain

1. Tech workforce is uniquely mobile: small teams, digital, remote-capable → can locate anywhere
2. Mobile creative workers who can work from anywhere will choose the best environment available
3. "Best available" requires: not already captured by a dominant industry (which would make tech workers second-class)
4. New York captured by finance, LA by Hollywood → tech would be secondary in both
5. SF Bay Area: beautiful, Mediterranean climate, dense, uncaptured by any dominant industry when tech was forming
6. These workers chose Bay Area by revealed preference → clustering → universities/VCs/infrastructure followed
7. Universities (Stanford, Berkeley) and path dependency amplified, not caused, the clustering
