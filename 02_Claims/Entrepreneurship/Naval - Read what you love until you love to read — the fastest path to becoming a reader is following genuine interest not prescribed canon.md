# Naval - Read what you love until you love to read — the fastest path to becoming a reader is following genuine interest not prescribed canon

## Position

The bottleneck to self-education is not access to books — the internet has made all knowledge accessible — but the desire to learn. The correct strategy for building reading as a habit is to start exactly where you are and read what genuinely interests you, regardless of whether it is considered serious or foundational. Fiction to science fiction to nonfiction to science — the natural path forward. Trying to force the canon first produces the experience of reading as chore, which kills the habit. The goal is to make reading so enjoyable that it becomes automatic, and then over time boredom with simpler material will naturally push you toward more difficult and foundational works. Naval applies this to himself: he became a serious reader by following curiosity, not by disciplining himself through books he didn't want to read.

- "take your natural path and just read the things that interest you until you kind of understand them and then you'll naturally move to the next thing and the next thing and the next thing"

## Logic Chain

1. The bottleneck is desire, not access — books are freely available
2. Forced reading (prescribed canon first) creates chore-association → kills reading habit
3. Interest-driven reading creates enjoyment-association → builds reading habit
4. Once the habit is established, boredom with simple material naturally drives escalation to more demanding works
5. The path: fiction → sci-fi → nonfiction → science/philosophy — each transition driven by natural curiosity, not prescription
6. Result: a habitual reader who seeks out foundational material independently
