# Travis Kalanick - Food automation only works economically when the system replaces the whole assembly workflow instead of adding expensive machines beside human labor

## Position
Travis Kalanick argues that food automation only works economically when it replaces the whole assembly workflow instead of adding expensive machines alongside human labor.

## Evidence Cited
- He says many failed automated food systems still had people feeding ingredients in and packaging the output.
- He says his system lets staff load ingredients and then leave while the restaurant runs for hours without people there.
- He says labor in their delivery kitchens can drop from roughly 30 to 35 percent of revenue to about 7 to 10 percent.

## Logic Chain
1. Kalanick contrasts partial automation with end-to-end workflow replacement.
2. He uses labor share reduction as the proof that full-stack automation changes the unit economics.
3. That supports the claim that automation only works when it replaces the full assembly workflow.
