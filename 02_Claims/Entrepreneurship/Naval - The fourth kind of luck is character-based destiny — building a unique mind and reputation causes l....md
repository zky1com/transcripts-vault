# Naval - The fourth kind of luck is character-based destiny — building a unique mind and reputation causes l...

## Position

Naval (drawing on a Marc Andreessen blog post) distinguishes four kinds of luck. (1) Blind luck: fortune, fate, completely out of your control. (2) Hustle luck: stirring the pot, creating lots of energy and motion, luck that comes from persistence. (3) Prepared-mind luck: being so skilled in a domain that you notice lucky breaks others miss (chance favors the prepared mind). (4) Character-based luck: building a unique character, mindset, and brand such that luck seeks you out specifically. The deep-sea diver example: if you are the best and most daring underwater diver, when someone discovers a sunken treasure they cannot access, they will specifically seek you out to retrieve it. Their blind luck becomes your destiny. Naval's insight: the fourth kind of luck is so deterministic that it stops being luck and becomes destiny. "Your character is your destiny" is the formulation he endorses.
