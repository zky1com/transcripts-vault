# Naval - Compromise between two genuine theories produces a third position nobody believed — this is the poverty of compromise

## Position

When two parties hold genuine competing theories (X and Y), a compromise produces policy Z — something that is literally between X and Y and that neither party thought was correct. When Z fails, nothing is learned: party A returns to X, party B returns to Y, and no one is surprised that Z didn't work because no one thought it would work. This is the poverty of compromise: it generates no real information and makes no genuine progress. The error-correction value of a failed experiment requires that someone actually believed the hypothesis being tested. A compromise hypothesis that nobody believed produces a failed experiment that nobody learns from. The right approach is the scientific one: implement X or Y, observe the result, and use that result to update beliefs about X and Y. This requires the courage to implement a theory rather than averaging between theories.

## Evidence Cited

- "person A has idea X and person B has idea Y — the compromise is theory Z — this is theory Z"
- "when that policy proves not to work we shouldn't be surprised because neither person A or person B actually ever thought it was the best idea in the first place"
- "no one learns anything — person A goes back to saying I always told you X was correct and person B goes back to Y — so they haven't made any progress whatsoever"
- "they've shown that Z is wrong but no one ever thought Z was correct in the first place — so this is the poverty of compromise"

## Logic Chain

1. Two parties hold competing theories X and Y
2. Compromise = policy Z (between X and Y), neither party believes Z
3. Z fails → neither party learns anything: "I told you Z was wrong" → returns to prior position
4. No information is gained because no one believed Z would work → failure of Z was expected by everyone
5. Proper error correction requires: implement X → observe failure → update; or implement Y → observe success → update
6. Compromise skips this by implementing what nobody believed → produces no learning
7. In science: no scientist proposes theory Z as the average of competing theories; they test X and Y directly
