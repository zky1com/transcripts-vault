# Naval - Science is an error-correcting mechanism not a catalog of past regularities — it explains why events happen not just that they happened

## Position

The common misconception of science is that it is a sophisticated induction machine: collect observations, notice patterns, project patterns into the future. This is wrong. Science is not "the sun rose every day therefore it will rise tomorrow." Science asks: why does the sun appear to rise? What physical mechanisms produce this effect? Under what conditions would those mechanisms not produce it? A true scientific claim must be explanatory (it models underlying causes) and error-correcting (it can be refuted by observations that don't fit the model). "The sun always rises" is not a scientific claim — it is an inductive generalization that fails immediately when you go to Antarctica or the International Space Station, where sunrise patterns differ dramatically. The black swan argument applies: consistent past observation gives no logical guarantee of future continuance. Only explanatory theories that model the underlying mechanism can tell you when and why exceptions occur.

## Evidence Cited

- "science is not about cataloging a history of events that have occurred in the past and presuming they're going to occur again in the future science is an explanatory framework it's an error correcting mechanism"
- "all you need to do is to take a trip to antarctica and there for some months of the year the sun doesn't rise at all"
- "the black swan is an example...that repeatedly observing the same phenomena over and again should not make you confident that it will continue in the future"

## Logic Chain

1. Induction: observe pattern → project into future ("sun always rises")
2. Fails immediately at regime change: Antarctica (polar night), International Space Station (16 sunrises per day), cosmological timescales (sun will eventually expand)
3. Science: explain the mechanism (Earth rotates relative to sun; this rotation occurs under these conditions; exceptions arise when these conditions change)
4. Explanatory theories predict exceptions — induction cannot
5. Black swan: all European swans white → "all swans are white"; refuted by first Australian black swan
6. Science = explanatory theories + refutation by evidence; not induction + pattern projection
