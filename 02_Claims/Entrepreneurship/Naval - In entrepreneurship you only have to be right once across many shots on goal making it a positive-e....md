# Naval - In entrepreneurship you only have to be right once across many shots on goal making it a positive-e...

## Position

Entrepreneurship is a repeated-bet game where each shot on goal comes every 1-10 years depending on iteration speed. Because the upside of a single success is unbounded and you only need one hit across a career, the expected value of staying in the game is strongly positive even if most individual attempts fail. This asymmetry is fundamental to why high failure rates in entrepreneurship are not disqualifying â€” the one win is sufficient to justify the career.

## Evidence Cited

- "you just have to be right once"
- "you get many many shots on goal â€” every three to five years, maybe every ten at the slowest, or once every year at the fastest"
- Implicit contrast: salaried workers must be right every year; entrepreneurs only need one hit

## Logic Chain

1. Each startup attempt is an independent trial with a small probability of large upside
2. You can make multiple attempts over a career (at least 3-10 depending on pace)
3. Even a 10% per-attempt success rate across 5 attempts yields ~41% lifetime success probability
4. The upside of the one success is typically orders of magnitude larger than any individual salary
5. Therefore the expected value of entrepreneurship exceeds the expected value of salaried employment for people willing to tolerate variance
