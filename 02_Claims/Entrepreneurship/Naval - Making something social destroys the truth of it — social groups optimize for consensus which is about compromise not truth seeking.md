# Naval - Making something social destroys the truth of it — social groups optimize for consensus which is about compromise not truth seeking

## Position

Any truth-seeking enterprise that becomes primarily social will tend to drift away from truth and toward consensus. This is not a corruption of an otherwise healthy process — it is the predictable consequence of the structure of groups. Groups survive by getting along. Getting along requires consensus. Consensus is achieved by compromise. Compromise means discarding positions that others find unacceptable, even if those positions are correct. Truth-seeking and group-cohesion-maintenance are therefore structurally opposed: maximizing one tends to minimize the other. The more groupthink is visible in a domain — the more everyone agrees, the more dissenters are excluded, the more comfortable the conversation — the further that domain has drifted from reality. Naval's aphorism "the more groupthink you see involved, the further from the truth you actually are" is not cynicism but a structural observation.

## Logic Chain

1. Groups survive through cohesion; cohesion requires consensus
2. Consensus is achieved by compromise — discarding positions that create conflict
3. If a correct position creates conflict, it will be discarded in favor of cohesion
4. Therefore: group dynamics systematically favor cohesion over accuracy
5. More groupthink → stronger consensus → further from what is actually true
6. Corollary: the apparent harmony of a group's conclusions is evidence against their truth, not for it
