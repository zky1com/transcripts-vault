# Naval - Non-profits cannot fail because they have no objective feedback mechanism — they always declare vic...

## Position

Non-profits have no profit/loss signal â€” every outcome is evaluated socially. Social evaluation means: did the people involved feel good, congratulate each other, produce nice mission statements, and maintain relationships? Since the answer to all of those is always yes, every project is a success. This makes non-profits structurally incapable of error-correction. Without failure as a signal, they cannot learn what works. For-profits are forced to take feedback from reality (revenue/loss), which makes them both more honest and ironically more effective at their missions. Naval argues the best vehicle for world-changing is an ethical for-profit, not a non-profit.

## Evidence Cited

- "no matter what the foundation did they would declare victory... every project was victorious every project was a success... but nothing ever got done"
- "because there is no objective feedback because there is no loss it's all social profit they couldn't fail and because they couldn't fail they misdirected resources all day long"
- "for-profits have to take feedback from reality"
- "ironically for-profit entities are more sustainable than non-profit entities"

## Logic Chain

1. Error-correction requires a failure signal â€” some outcome that forces acknowledgment that an approach didn't work
2. Non-profits have no failure signal: the social feedback (recognition, relationships, dinners) is always positive
3. Without a failure signal, non-profits cannot identify which interventions work and which don't
4. Resources continue to flow to interventions that produce social rewards, regardless of impact
5. Therefore: non-profits are structurally optimized for social approval, not for achieving their stated missions
