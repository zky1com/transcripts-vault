# Naval - Good explanations are creative leaps that posit an underlying mechanism — they are not derived from...

## Position

Deutsch's definition of a good explanation is a creative act: you observe something happening in the world, and then you invent an explanation for *why* it happens â€” positing an underlying mechanism. This is categorically different from induction (observing past patterns and projecting them forward). The heliocentric explanation (earth rotates, creating apparent solar motion) is a creative leap that contradicts naive observation â€” the sun *appears* to move, but the explanation posits that we are the ones moving. Good explanations are also testable: they make predictions about what we would observe if they were true, allowing experiments to distinguish between competing explanations.

## Logic Chain

1. Naive observation of the sunset: the sun moves across the sky
2. An inductive approach would project this pattern: the sun will continue to move
3. A good explanation asks: *why* does the sun appear to move? What mechanism produces this observation?
4. The creative leap: perhaps we are moving, not the sun â€” the earth rotates
5. This explanation is testable and reveals a deeper truth that contradicts direct observation â€” which is what distinguishes genuine explanation from mere pattern description
