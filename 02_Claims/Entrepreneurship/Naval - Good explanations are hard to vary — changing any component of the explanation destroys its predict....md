# Naval - Good explanations are hard to vary — changing any component of the explanation destroys its predict...

## Position

Deutsch's second criterion for good explanations (beyond falsifiability): the explanation must be hard to vary. The contrast is between the Greek myth explanation for seasons (Persephone leaving Hades) and the axial tilt theory (Earth tilted at 23 degrees). The Persephone story is easy to vary â€” you could replace any character (Nike instead of Persephone, Zeus instead of Hades) and the seasonal predictions remain identical. No part of the explanation is load-bearing. By contrast, the axial tilt explanation is hard to vary: change the tilt angle, and the predicted sunrise positions and seasonal lengths at every latitude change. Every element has a precise physical reason for being what it is. Good explanations achieve a form where all the components constrain each other, leaving no free parameters.
