# Naval - True entrepreneurship eliminates the work-life distinction because the work is inseparable from who...

## Position

When you truly work for yourself, the work/life boundary collapses entirely. There are no weekends, vacations, or time off â€” but there is also no "work" in the sense of something to escape. You are the business, the product, the entity, and you care so deeply you cannot disengage. The curse and the reward are the same thing: you can't turn it off because it's genuinely yours.

## Evidence Cited

- "You won't have hobbies, you won't have weekends, and you won't have vacations, but you won't have work either"
- Paradox framing: sacrificing work-life balance to gain the elimination of work as a concept

## Logic Chain

1. Work-life balance requires a boundary between a thing you do and a thing you are
2. When work is self-expression, no such boundary exists
3. The entrepreneur is measured only on output and sets their own bar
4. This produces extreme exhilaration but also inability to disengage
5. The collapse of the boundary is simultaneously the curse and the reward
