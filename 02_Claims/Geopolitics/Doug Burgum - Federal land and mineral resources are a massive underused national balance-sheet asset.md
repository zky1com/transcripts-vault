# Doug Burgum - Federal land and mineral resources are a massive underused national balance-sheet asset

## Position
Doug Burgum argues that the federal government controls an enormous resource balance sheet and is generating far too little return from it for the American public.

## Evidence Cited
- He describes Interior as controlling hundreds of millions of surface and subsurface acres plus offshore resources.
- He says Interior would have the largest balance sheet in the world as a standalone company.
- He contrasts potential returns with the much smaller revenue currently produced.

## Logic Chain
1. Burgum frames public land and mineral rights as economic assets, not just stewardship obligations.
2. He argues those assets could produce materially more revenue through leasing and royalties.
3. That supports the claim that the national balance sheet is being underused.
