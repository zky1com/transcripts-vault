# Chamath Palihapitiya - Territorial disputes since World War 2 have consistently produced permanent stalemates making a clean Ukraine resolution historically unprecedented

## Position
A historical survey of territorial disputes since 1945 reveals that they almost universally resolve as permanent stalemates (DMZ arrangements) rather than clean resolutions. Korea (1953-present), Cyprus (1974-present), Kashmir (1947-present), Crimea (2014-present) all persist as frozen conflicts. If this historical pattern holds, Ukraine will likely settle into a similar frozen stalemate regardless of current diplomatic efforts.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Since 1945... how have other territorial disputes panned out? There are disputes that end in stalemates... there's one in Korea 1953 to now. There's one in Cyprus 1974 to now and there's one in Kashmir 1947 to today. And then Crimea 2014 to present."
- Transcript: "Unless President Trump figures out a way to do something that's frankly truly unprecedented, the historical pattern is that it's been nearly impossible for even the most powerful and influential person in the world to frankly draw deals in these kinds of situations."

## Logic Chain
1. Post-WWII territorial disputes predominantly produce frozen stalemates, not clean resolutions.
2. Korea, Cyprus, Kashmir, Crimea all remain unresolved after decades.
3. Resolving such disputes requires reversing military facts on the ground, which proved impossible in all comparable cases.
4. Ukraine fits this pattern: Russian forces control contested territories and a negotiated return is structurally difficult.
5. Without genuinely unprecedented diplomacy, Ukraine is likely to join the list of permanent frozen conflicts.
