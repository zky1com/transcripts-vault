# David Sacks - A comprehensive Ukraine peace deal requires no NATO membership and territorial concessions from Ukraine

## Position
The Trump-Putin Alaska summit established a framework for comprehensive (not just ceasefire) peace in Ukraine: no NATO membership for Ukraine, recognition of territorial concessions — and this framework has popular support even in Ukraine where support for continued fighting has dropped from 70% to 24%.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Number one: pursue a comprehensive peace deal, not a ceasefire. Number two: Ukraine not joining NATO. Number three: territorial concessions must be recognized."
- Transcript: "A Gallup poll shows Ukrainian support for fighting fell from 70% to 24%."
- Transcript: The Trump-Putin Alaska summit cited as the diplomatic venue establishing these three pillars.

## Logic Chain
1. A ceasefire without resolution leaves the conflict open to resumption.
2. Russia's core demands (no NATO expansion, recognition of territorial control) are prerequisites for Moscow accepting any deal.
3. Ukrainian popular support for continued fighting has fallen dramatically, reducing the political cost of territorial concessions.
4. Therefore a comprehensive deal on these three terms is achievable and represents the most durable path to peace.
