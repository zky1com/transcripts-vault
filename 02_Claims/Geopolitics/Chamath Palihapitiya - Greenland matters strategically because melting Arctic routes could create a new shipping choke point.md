# Chamath Palihapitiya - Greenland matters strategically because melting Arctic routes could create a new shipping choke point

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that Greenland matters strategically because melting Arctic routes could create a new shipping choke point.

## Evidence Cited
- He says melting Arctic ice could open a northern shipping passage for critical goods.
- He argues that influence over Greenland and adjacent routes could become as strategically important as the Panama Canal.
- He says the United States should not let China or Russia dominate that emerging corridor.

## Logic Chain
1. Shipping choke points create military and economic leverage.
2. Chamath says Arctic melt is making a new choke point more plausible and Greenland is central to it.
3. That supports his claim that Greenland has growing strategic importance.
