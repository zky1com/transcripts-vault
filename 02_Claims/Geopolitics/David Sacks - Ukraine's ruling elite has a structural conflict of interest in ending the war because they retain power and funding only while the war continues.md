# David Sacks - Ukraine's ruling elite has a structural conflict of interest in ending the war because they retain power and funding only while the war continues

## Position
Ukraine has canceled elections citing wartime necessity. The ruling elite therefore faces a structural incentive to prolong the war: peace would require elections, which Zelensky might lose given that polls show 76% of Ukrainians now want the war to end and are willing to make concessions. Meanwhile, billions in Western military and financial support continues flowing only as long as the conflict continues.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "The problem you have is it's not just Zilinsky, right? It's his ruling clique. They have canceled elections and they will remain in power as long as the war continues."
- Transcript: "There's a conflict of interest there and there's a lot of money flowing into Ukraine because of the war."
- Transcript: "There was a poll by Gallup... a year or two ago something like 70% of Ukrainians wanted to keep fighting the war. Now that number is down to 24%."

## Logic Chain
1. Ukraine has suspended elections citing wartime conditions.
2. Without elections, Zelensky's government cannot be democratically removed.
3. Peace would require elections, threatening the incumbent government's hold on power.
4. Western aid continues at scale only while the war continues, creating financial incentive to prolong it.
5. Therefore Zelensky's elite has structural reasons to reject peace even against the will of the Ukrainian population.
