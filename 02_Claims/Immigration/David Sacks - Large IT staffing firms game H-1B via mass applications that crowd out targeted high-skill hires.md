# David Sacks - Large IT staffing firms game H-1B via mass applications that crowd out targeted high-skill hires

## Position
Chamath, citing Howard Lutnik's explanation at a White House dinner, argues the H-1B lottery is structurally gamed by large IT outsourcing firms that file hundreds of thousands of applications for interchangeable employees, gaining a statistically overwhelming advantage over startups or companies filing for one or two specific high-skill roles. This batch-filing distorts the visa pool away from high-value targeted hires toward commodity IT labor.

## Logic Chain
1. H-1B lottery allocates visas across all applicants in a fixed pool regardless of company size.
2. Large IT staffing firms file for all offshore workers simultaneously, gaining lottery share proportional to headcount.
3. Smaller employers filing for one or two specific roles have near-zero probability of winning.
4. The outcome allocates visas to commodity IT labor rather than scarce high-skill targeted hires.
5. A mandatory application fee of $100K would price out low-wage H-1B batch filings and restore the high-skill intent.
