# Eric Schmitt - The key legal line is the difference between government persuasion and coercion when dealing with platforms

## Position
Eric Schmitt argues that the key legal line is the difference between government persuasion and coercion when dealing with platforms.

## Evidence Cited
- He says simply informing platforms differs from threatening liability or punishment.
- He describes officials using pressure tied to Section 230 and possible investigations.
- He says that move beyond persuasion formed the basis of the lawsuit.

## Logic Chain
1. Schmitt draws a legal distinction between mere communication and state-backed compulsion.
2. Once government leverage is used to force outcomes, the interaction becomes constitutionally suspect.
3. That supports the claim that the key line is persuasion versus coercion.
