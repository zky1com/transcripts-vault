# Andrew Schulz - Americans can handle hard truths better than spin and need much more brutal transparency from leaders

## Position
Andrew Schulz argues that Americans are proud enough to handle difficult realities if leaders state them plainly instead of managing perceptions with spin.

## Evidence Cited
- He says leaders need brutal transparency.
- He says Americans can get through whatever the truth is if it is said plainly.
- He frames honesty as the way to rebuild confidence.

## Logic Chain
1. Schulz believes public trust is damaged by message management and concealment.
2. He argues straightforward honesty is more stabilizing than polished narrative control.
3. That supports the claim that Americans need far more direct truth-telling from leaders.
