# Pete Buttigieg - Identity politics became too central to Democratic messaging and crowded out more universal language

## Position
Pete Buttigieg says identity-based framing became too central to how Democrats talked and that it displaced broader political language that could have reached more voters.

## Evidence Cited
- He says identity has become too central to how his party thinks.
- He acknowledges that this framing crowded out more universal language.
- He treats that messaging imbalance as a real political problem, not just a media caricature.

## Logic Chain
1. Buttigieg is making a direct critique of his own side's communicative habits.
2. He links identity-heavy framing with broader credibility and coalition problems.
3. That supports the claim that Democrats over-centered identity language.
