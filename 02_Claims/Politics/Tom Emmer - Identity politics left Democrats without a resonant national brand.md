# Tom Emmer - Identity politics left Democrats without a resonant national brand

## Position
Tom Emmer argues that identity politics left Democrats without a resonant national brand.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the party no longer knows who it is or what it stands for.
- He points to fights over issues like men competing in women's sports as examples of positions that do not resonate with voters.
- He says Democrats are now struggling to find a way out of a strategy that used to work for them.

## Logic Chain
1. A party brand weakens when its signature issues stop resonating with the public.
2. Emmer says identity politics has become a liability rather than an advantage for Democrats.
3. That supports his claim that Democrats currently lack a resonant national brand.
