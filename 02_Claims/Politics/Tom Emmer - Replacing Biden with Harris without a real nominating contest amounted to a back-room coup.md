# Tom Emmer - Replacing Biden with Harris without a real nominating contest amounted to a back-room coup

## Position
Tom Emmer argues that replacing Joe Biden with Kamala Harris without a real nominating contest amounted to a back-room coup.

## Evidence Cited
- He says Democrats removed Biden in a matter of hours or days instead of letting a normal delegate process unfold.
- He contrasts that move with the months of ballot-access and state-by-state campaigning usually required to win a nomination.
- He says the process avoided a genuine convention debate over candidates.

## Logic Chain
1. A legitimate nomination normally requires an open contest with delegates and public debate.
2. Emmer says Democrats skipped that process when they replaced Biden.
3. That is why he describes the handoff to Harris as a back-room coup.
