# Interview guest - Media distrust deepened when mainstream outlets omitted key context from politically charged stories

## Position
The interview guest says his skepticism hardened when mainstream outlets repeatedly omitted or buried context that changed the meaning of politically sensitive stories.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he started feeling uneasy after seeing Pirate Wires flag stories that mainstream outlets later acknowledged only much deeper in their coverage.
- He points to the Charlottesville coverage as the decisive example because he says the most repeated version omitted Trump's explicit denunciations of Nazis.
- He treats those omissions as evidence that mainstream media could not be trusted on core political narratives.

## Logic Chain
1. The guest describes a pattern of partial or delayed reporting rather than a one-off mistake.
2. He says those examples changed his confidence in the reliability of mainstream political coverage.
3. That supports the claim that omitted context drove his media distrust.
