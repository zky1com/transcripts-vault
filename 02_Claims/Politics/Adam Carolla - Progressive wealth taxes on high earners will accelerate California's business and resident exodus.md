# Adam Carolla - Progressive wealth taxes on high earners will accelerate California's business and resident exodus

## Position
With combined state and federal tax already at 53% for top earners, proposed additional wealth taxes will push job-creating individuals and companies out of California at an accelerating rate, deepening the fiscal crisis rather than solving it.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "People of California, the people in the highest tax brackets are already at about 50% with state — 53 with state and federal."
- Transcript: A 5% one-time wealth tax on net worth over $1B is circulating as a California ballot measure; an 8% federal oligarch tax on net worth over $50M is in Congress.
- Transcript: "Taking more than 50% of your money, I would argue is the ultimate government overreach."
- Transcript: Tesla, SpaceX, and Amazon, Microsoft, Costco are cited as already leaving or considering leaving Washington/California over payroll taxes.
- Transcript: "They never really kind of understand that... you're a big fan of telling them what a shitty school system there is. Yes, but you're making it worse. You're driving people out."

## Logic Chain
1. California's existing tax burden is among the highest in the nation at ~53% combined.
2. Proposed wealth taxes will make the marginal rate on wealth creation confiscatory.
3. High earners and business owners have mobility and will relocate to avoid confiscation.
4. Departing taxpayers reduce the tax base, requiring either spending cuts (politically difficult) or further tax increases.
5. Therefore wealth taxes will accelerate the fiscal death spiral rather than resolve it.
