# Francis Suarez - Lowering taxes and growing the economy gave Miami more capacity rather than less

## Position
Francis Suarez says lowering taxes did not starve Miami of capacity because stronger growth expanded the city's resources over time.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he lowered taxes to the lowest level in city history.
- He says city government still grew from about $500 million to roughly $1.5 billion during the following decade of prosperity.
- He frames that as proof that growth can expand fiscal capacity even while tax rates fall.

## Logic Chain
1. Suarez rejects the idea that lower taxes automatically reduce a city's ability to fund services.
2. He argues that pro-growth policy broadened the tax base and expanded total resources.
3. So the claim reflects his view that growth offset the lower tax burden.
