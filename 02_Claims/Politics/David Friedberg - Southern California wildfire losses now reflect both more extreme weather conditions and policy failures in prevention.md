# David Friedberg - Southern California wildfire losses now reflect both more extreme weather conditions and policy failures in prevention

## Position
David Friedberg argues that Southern California wildfire losses now reflect both more extreme weather conditions and policy failures in prevention.

## Evidence Cited
- He says more frequent extreme events are making rare disaster conditions less rare.
- He says zero rainfall and one-hundred-mile-per-hour winds created a fire-hurricane environment.
- He also says failures in brush clearing and tree removal were real policy mistakes.

## Logic Chain
1. A disaster can be worsened both by natural conditions and by human failures to prepare.
2. Friedberg says that combination is exactly what happened in Southern California.
3. That supports his claim that both extreme weather and prevention failures drove the losses.
