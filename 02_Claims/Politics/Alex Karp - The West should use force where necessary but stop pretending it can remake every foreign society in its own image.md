# Alex Karp - The West should use force where necessary but stop pretending it can remake every foreign society in its own image

## Position
Alex Karp argues that the West should use force when necessary but should stop trying to occupy and culturally remake foreign societies in its own image.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he is not a neocon and distinguishes necessary force from occupation and civilizational redesign.
- He says people elsewhere may have functioning ways of life that are different from Western liberal models.
- He treats regime-remaking projects as destabilizing and philosophically linked to other elite overreach.

## Logic Chain
1. Karp separates defense and coercive capacity from ideological nation-building.
2. He argues the West is strongest when it defends itself well, not when it tries to refound every society it touches.
3. That supports the claim that force may be necessary but remaking every foreign society is a mistake.
