# Alex Karp - Western decline begins when institutions stop rewarding merit and stop believing they deserve to win

## Position
Alex Karp argues that Western decline starts when institutions stop rewarding merit and lose confidence in their own right to win.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the protesters and elite institutions have absorbed anti-meritocratic ideas.
- He frames America's advantage as letting people express individual excellence and win without apology.
- He explicitly says people have a right to win and must fight to preserve that norm.

## Logic Chain
1. Karp links civilizational strength to meritocratic confidence rather than guilt or managed decline.
2. He says institutions decay when they moralize losing and delegitimize excellence.
3. That supports the claim that Western decline begins when institutions stop rewarding merit and cease believing they should win.
