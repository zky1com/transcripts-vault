# Adam Carolla - Safety-first governance culture systematically ignores second-order economic and social costs

## Position
Regulatory bodies and government leaders tasked with "safety" optimize only for that metric while ignoring downstream economic, social, and practical costs — producing policies that sound noble but cause substantial collateral damage.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Their job is not to think about the second and third order effects on the economy, on the affordability, on the ability to move quickly, on all the other factors."
- Transcript: The Prius analogy — adding NASCAR roll cage, fire suppression, ballistic fuel cell would make a car safer but price it at $150,000 so no one can buy it. "They hide behind this safety BS."
- Transcript: COVID school closures: "Shutting schools down for COVID may be the safest thing to do, but you damage kids. You cause a lot of collateral damage."
- Transcript: Autonomous vehicles — every million miles reduces fatalities 95%, but one crash shuts the whole program down despite massive net safety benefit.
- Transcript: "You want full safety. What did you do? You said shut the schools, shut the churches, shut the small businesses, mask up."

## Logic Chain
1. Government safety regulators are rewarded for reducing visible near-term risk and receive no accountability for collateral economic or social harm.
2. This incentive structure produces escalating regulation that maximizes narrow safety metrics at the expense of cost, speed, and societal function.
3. Therefore safety-only governance systematically produces worse aggregate outcomes than integrated multi-factor leadership.
