# Interview guest - Removing internal gatekeepers made his public politics more candid and authentic

## Position
The interview guest says that after losing an internal gatekeeper he became much more politically candid in public and found that candor more authentic.

## Evidence Cited
- He says his longtime chief of staff had been the main person stopping him from posting political thoughts.
- After that person left, he says he started tweeting what he actually felt, even when it was imperfect or emotional.
- He frames that transition as part of a broader shift toward authenticity in public life.

## Logic Chain
1. The guest links a more filtered public persona to internal political gatekeeping.
2. He says removing that filter changed both what he said and how authentic he felt being online.
3. That supports the claim that less mediation produced more candid political self-presentation.
