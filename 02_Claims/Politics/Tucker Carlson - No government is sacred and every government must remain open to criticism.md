# Tucker Carlson - No government is sacred and every government must remain open to criticism

## Position
Tucker Carlson argues that no government is sacred and every government must remain open to criticism because power always tends toward abuse.

## Evidence Cited
- He rejects the idea that any state should be insulated from criticism by moral blackmail or accusations about motive.
- He says his objections to governments derive from a broader principle, not from hostility to one country or people.
- He frames criticism of state conduct as a necessary part of limiting abuse.

## Logic Chain
1. Carlson treats state criticism as a structural requirement of free politics.
2. He argues that making governments morally untouchable only increases the chance of abuse.
3. That supports the claim that every government must remain open to criticism.
