# Pete Buttigieg - Government still has a legitimate role in solving trillion-dollar coordination problems the private sector will not solve alone

## Position
Pete Buttigieg argues that there are very large national problems where private incentives alone are insufficient and government has a legitimate coordinating role.

## Evidence Cited
- He says there are certain trillion-dollar ideas the private sector just won't do.
- He defends government action when private incentives fail to solve coordination problems.
- He presents public intervention as a practical tool rather than an ideological reflex.

## Logic Chain
1. Buttigieg is not defending government expansion in the abstract.
2. He specifically justifies state action where scale and coordination exceed what markets will spontaneously provide.
3. That supports the claim that he sees a real government role in major coordination problems.
