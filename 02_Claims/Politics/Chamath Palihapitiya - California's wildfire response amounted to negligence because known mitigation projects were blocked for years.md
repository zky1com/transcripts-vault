# Chamath Palihapitiya - California's wildfire response amounted to negligence because known mitigation projects were blocked for years

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that California's wildfire response amounted to negligence because known mitigation projects were blocked for years.

## Evidence Cited
- He says Southern California had already seen a closely comparable fire disaster in 2018.
- He points to insurers trying to exit fire coverage before the event as evidence that the risk was already widely knowable.
- He cites blocked or vetoed bills that would have made wildfire prevention and power-line mitigation easier.

## Logic Chain
1. Negligence becomes plausible when officials face known recurring risks and still block obvious mitigation.
2. Chamath says that is what happened in California before these fires.
3. That is why he frames the state's response as negligence rather than bad luck alone.
