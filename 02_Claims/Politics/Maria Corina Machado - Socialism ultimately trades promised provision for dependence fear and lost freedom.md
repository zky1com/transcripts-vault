# Maria Corina Machado - Socialism ultimately trades promised provision for dependence fear and lost freedom

## Position
Maria Corina Machado argues that socialism ultimately offers people provision in exchange for dependence on the state and the loss of freedom.

## Evidence Cited
- She says people are told they will receive support for free but actually surrender the freedom to choose for themselves.
- She describes that arrangement as a path into dependency and slavery rather than security.
- She uses Venezuela's collapse as a warning that whole societies can become equal in misery when the state dominates everything.

## Logic Chain
1. Machado treats the central trade in socialism as political dependence rather than economic generosity.
2. She argues that once the state controls everything, citizens lose autonomy before they realize the cost.
3. That supports the claim that socialism exchanges promised provision for lost freedom.
