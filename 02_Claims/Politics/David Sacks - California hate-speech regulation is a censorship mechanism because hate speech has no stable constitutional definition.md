# David Sacks - California hate-speech regulation is a censorship mechanism because hate speech has no stable constitutional definition

## Position
David Sacks argues that California hate-speech regulation is a censorship mechanism because hate speech has no stable constitutional definition.

## Evidence Cited
- He says hate speech is not a constitutional category with a recognized exception under the First Amendment.
- He says the proposed California approach turns hate speech into whatever people in power say it is.
- He says that makes social platforms vulnerable to fines for speech that is merely politically disfavored or offensive.

## Logic Chain
1. If the banned category is undefined, enforcement inevitably shifts to discretionary political judgment.
2. That discretionary enforcement creates a censorship tool rather than a neutral legal standard.
3. That supports the claim that the proposal is a censorship mechanism.
