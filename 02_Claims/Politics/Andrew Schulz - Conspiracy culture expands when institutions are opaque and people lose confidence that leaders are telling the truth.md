# Andrew Schulz - Conspiracy culture expands when institutions are opaque and people lose confidence that leaders are telling the truth

## Position
Andrew Schulz says conspiracy thinking grows when institutions lack transparency and people no longer trust the official version of events.

## Evidence Cited
- He says conspiracy obsession rises when there is not real transparency.
- He says people are looking for answers because nothing feels exactly as it seems.
- He links that to all-time-low confidence in institutions and information.

## Logic Chain
1. Schulz treats conspiracy behavior as partly a response to institutional credibility failure.
2. He argues opacity creates room for alternative explanations to flourish.
3. So the claim reflects his view that mistrust and opacity fuel conspiracy culture.
