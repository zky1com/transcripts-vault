# Pete Buttigieg - The debt path the United States is on is unsustainable even for politicians who still believe in an active government

## Position
Pete Buttigieg says the US debt trajectory is not sustainable and treats fiscal reality as a real constraint even though he still believes government has an important role to play.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the debt path the country is on is not sustainable.
- He pairs that claim with a defense of purposeful government action.
- He does not present fiscal concern as a purely right-wing talking point.

## Logic Chain
1. Buttigieg is trying to hold two ideas at once: active government and fiscal constraint.
2. That means he sees debt sustainability as a serious issue inside his own governing framework.
3. That supports the claim that he views the current debt path as unsustainable.
