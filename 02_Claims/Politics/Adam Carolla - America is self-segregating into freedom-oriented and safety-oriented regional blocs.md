# Adam Carolla - America is self-segregating into freedom-oriented and safety-oriented regional blocs

## Position
Americans are voluntarily relocating based on values alignment — freedom-oriented individuals moving to Florida, Texas, and Tennessee while safety-and-entitlement-oriented populations remain in California, Oregon, and Washington — and the latter bloc's governance model will collapse under its own weight.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "All the people that are tired of living in a nanny state... they're just going to self-segregate. They're going to move to Florida. They're going to move to Texas. They're going to move to Tennessee."
- Transcript: "Safe space places are going to fall apart and the octagons are going to thrive."
- Transcript: People he knows are now leaving California not because of job relocations but voluntarily: "I'm leaving. Where are you going? Florida, Nashville, Texas, Austin."
- Transcript: Orange County example — geographically adjacent to LA but "clean, safe, and normal" because of different political culture.
- Transcript: "Families will move to a place that has a good school if they have young kids. And that'll be that."

## Logic Chain
1. Policy divergence between states has made quality-of-life differences tangible and visible.
2. Mobile individuals and families are responding by relocating to better-governed regions.
3. The departing population concentrates wealth, entrepreneurship, and tax base in destination states.
4. Therefore the governance gap between regions will widen as the human capital differential compounds.
