# Eric Schmitt - Section 230 only makes sense if platforms act like open forums instead of secretly publishing and throttling disfavored views

## Position
Eric Schmitt argues that Section 230 only makes sense if platforms act like open forums instead of secretly publishing and throttling disfavored views.

## Evidence Cited
- He says Congress gave platforms Section 230 because they were treated as platforms rather than publishers.
- He argues that companies became publishers when they throttled ideas they disliked.
- He says the legal protection makes sense only if the platform role is actually honored.

## Logic Chain
1. Section 230 rests on a distinction between neutral hosting and editorial suppression.
2. If a platform secretly curates and throttles like a publisher, the normative case for the shield weakens.
3. That supports the claim that Section 230 only makes sense for genuine open forums.
