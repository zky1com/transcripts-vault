# Mark Cuban - Democrats keep losing practical arguments because they do not know how to sell

## Position
Mark Cuban says Democrats keep losing practical arguments because the party does not know how to sell tangible policy impacts in plain language.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he tells Democratic leadership they have to learn how to sell.
- He uses healthcare subsidy policy as an example of a real household issue that the party fails to explain concretely.
- He treats that inability to communicate practical stakes as a core political weakness.

## Logic Chain
1. Cuban believes policy substance is not enough if voters never hear the real-world consequence clearly.
2. He argues Democrats are especially weak at translating detail into persuasive, everyday economics.
3. That supports the claim that Democrats keep losing because they do not know how to sell.
