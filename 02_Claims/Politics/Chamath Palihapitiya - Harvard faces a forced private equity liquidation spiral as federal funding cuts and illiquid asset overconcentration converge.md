# Chamath Palihapitiya - Harvard faces a forced private equity liquidation spiral as federal funding cuts and illiquid asset overconcentration converge

## Position
Harvard's endowment is over-allocated to private equity (40% as of 2025, up from 20% in 2019) at the top of the market. With federal funding under threat and a projected $1B annual budget shortfall, Harvard will be forced to sell illiquid PE positions at severe discounts (20-40%) as smart money recognizes their financial desperation — creating a death spiral.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "They're going to get into a huge world of hurt. What they will have to do in order to finance their budget in probably 18 months is start to actively sell their private equity portfolio, which... almost doubled from 20 to 40%."
- Transcript: "Harvard was able to sell a billion dollars recently of private equity stuff... at a 7% discount. There is no smart money on the street that's going to look at any private equity portfolio from Harvard without asking for a 20 25 30 35 40% discount because your back will be totally against the wall."
- Transcript: "There's an excise tax by the way in the BBB that taxes foundation assets... upwards of 8% a year. For Harvard like $2.5 extra billion dollars a year."

## Logic Chain
1. Harvard allocated 40% of endowment to illiquid private equity at market peak.
2. Federal funding cuts create a projected $1B/year budget shortfall.
3. Without alternatives, Harvard must liquidate PE positions.
4. Buyers will demand 20-40% discounts knowing Harvard is a forced seller.
5. Discounted sales reduce endowment value, worsening the budget gap in a compounding spiral.
