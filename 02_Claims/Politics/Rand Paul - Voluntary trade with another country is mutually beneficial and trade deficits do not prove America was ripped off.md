# Rand Paul - Voluntary trade with another country is mutually beneficial and trade deficits do not prove America was ripped off

## Position
Rand Paul says voluntary trade is mutually beneficial and that a trade deficit does not prove the United States was ripped off.

## Evidence Cited
- He says every voluntary trade occurs only because both sides think they benefited.
- He uses the example of people voluntarily buying imported TVs because they value them more than the cash.
- He says drawing a circle around those purchases and calling it being ripped off is false accounting.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul grounds his argument in the logic of voluntary exchange.
2. He then applies that logic to cross-border trade and deficits.
3. Therefore the claim captures his argument that trade deficits are not proof of exploitation.
