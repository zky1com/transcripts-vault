# Rand Paul - Voluntary trade with another country is mutually beneficial and trade deficits do not prove America was ripped off

## Position
Rand Paul argues that voluntary trade with another country is mutually beneficial and trade deficits do not prove America was ripped off.

## Evidence Cited
- He says every voluntary trade happens because both sides think they gained.
- He uses Walmart and imported televisions to show that buyers willingly chose the exchange.
- He says circling all the purchases and calling the aggregate result a rip-off is false accounting.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul reasons from individual voluntary exchanges to the aggregate trade picture.
2. If each exchange is mutually beneficial on its own, a trade deficit alone cannot establish exploitation.
3. That supports the claim that deficits do not prove America was ripped off.
