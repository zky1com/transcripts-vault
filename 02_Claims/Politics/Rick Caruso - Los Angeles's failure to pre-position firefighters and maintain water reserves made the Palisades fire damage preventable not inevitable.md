# Rick Caruso - Los Angeles's failure to pre-position firefighters and maintain water reserves made the Palisades fire damage preventable not inevitable

## Position
The Palisades fire started in the same location as a fire 7 days prior that was never fully extinguished. Fire Department pre-deployment was not executed despite extreme wind warnings. The largest reservoir serving the area was not filled. When firefighters arrived, their hydrants ran dry — while Rick Caruso's Palisades Village, which had privately pre-positioned water trucks and firefighters, survived intact.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Where the fire started is the same exact place that there was a fire 7 days before. I think what we're going to see is the fire department did not put that original fire out. It smoldered and reignited."
- Transcript: "We had the largest reservoir not filled with water... The firefighters, their hoses have stopped. There is no more water in the fire hydrants."
- Transcript: "The fire department was never pre-deployed... We had our firefighters, our water, our retardant station there two days before the fire and we were ready to go... we saved the village, but we saved the whole block of properties."

## Logic Chain
1. The fire started in a location where a prior fire had occurred 7 days earlier.
2. No pre-deployment of public firefighting resources occurred despite documented extreme wind warnings.
3. The main water reservoir serving the area was not filled before the fires.
4. When hydrants ran dry, private resources (Caruso's pre-positioned trucks) filled the gap for properties they could reach.
5. Properties with private pre-positioned resources survived; those relying on public infrastructure largely did not.
