# Rand Paul - Social Security will not remain viable without raising the eligibility age to 70

## Position
Rand Paul says Social Security will not be sustainable unless the eligibility age is raised to 70.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he voted to raise the age of Social Security.
- He says it is not going to be around unless the age is raised to 70.
- He presents that as the only way the program sticks around.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul links the program's long-term viability to eligibility reform.
2. He frames higher retirement age as a necessary arithmetic adjustment.
3. Therefore the claim records his stated Social Security sustainability view.
