# Rand Paul - Social Security will not remain viable without raising the eligibility age to 70

## Position
Rand Paul argues that Social Security will not remain viable without raising the eligibility age to 70.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he voted to raise the Social Security age because otherwise it will not be around.
- He explicitly states that age 70 is the only way he sees the program sticking around.
- He frames the issue as arithmetic rather than ideology.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul treats the program as financially unsustainable under current demographics and promises.
2. If the math does not work at current eligibility levels, later eligibility is one of the few available levers.
3. That supports the claim that viability requires raising the age to 70.
