# Chamath Palihapitiya - Information warfare in closed societies is now as important as conventional pressure because connectivity can reveal or conceal the real mosaic

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that information warfare now matters as much as conventional pressure in closed societies because connectivity determines whether the real situation becomes visible.

## Evidence Cited
- He discusses Starlink and the attempt to get information out of Iran during unrest.
- He says packet loss, blocking, and shutdown tactics now shape what outside observers can actually know.
- He frames this as a preview of how future conflicts and internal crises will be fought.

## Logic Chain
1. Palihapitiya argues that the contest is no longer only over territory or force but also over what information can circulate.
2. If outside observers cannot assemble the full picture, both policy and public understanding become distorted.
3. That supports the claim that connectivity-driven information warfare is now central in closed societies.
