# David Sacks - Minneapolis became a test case for anti-ICE organized resistance rather than simple peaceful protest

## Position
David Sacks argues that Minneapolis became a test case for organized anti-ICE resistance rather than simple peaceful protest.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the local campaign involved encrypted chats, doxing, road blocking, hotel harassment, and alerting targets during operations.
- He says ICE was acting on named warrants for known criminal aliens.
- He says those tactics made already dangerous missions much more dangerous for federal agents.

## Logic Chain
1. Sacks distinguishes expressive protest from operational interference with arrests.
2. He presents the Minneapolis actions as coordinated obstruction of law enforcement rather than as ordinary demonstration.
3. That supports the claim that Minneapolis became a test case for organized anti-ICE resistance.
