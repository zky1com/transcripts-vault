# Eric Schmitt - The censorship record was a coordinated pattern rather than a collection of isolated mistakes

## Position
Eric Schmitt argues that the censorship record was a coordinated pattern rather than a collection of isolated mistakes.

## Evidence Cited
- He says discovery produced tens of thousands of documents showing agencies working with platforms.
- He names the White House, CDC, FBI, CISA, and other officials as part of the same enterprise.
- He says special portals and recurring communications showed a system, not one-off errors.

## Logic Chain
1. A cross-agency body of documents and repeated workflows indicates institutional coordination.
2. That evidence cuts against any story that the incidents were random or isolated.
3. That supports the claim that the censorship record was coordinated.
