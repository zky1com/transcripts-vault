# Ro Khanna - Offshoring and labor abuse hurt American workers more than blaming long-settled undocumented workers

## Position
Ro Khanna argues that offshoring and labor abuse hurt American workers more than blaming long-settled undocumented workers.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the hollowing out of towns and communities came from offshoring industry, not from one undocumented dental hygienist.
- He says both undocumented workers and H-1B workers can be abused through underpayment that depresses wages.
- He argues reform should target labor-market abuse and legal process rather than mass deportation rhetoric.

## Logic Chain
1. Worker harm can come from capital flight and exploitative labor arrangements rather than from immigrants as such.
2. Khanna says the real problems are offshoring and wage suppression through abuse of legal status.
3. That supports his claim that blaming long-settled undocumented workers misses the main causes of harm.
