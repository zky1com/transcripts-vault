# Adam Carolla - Big tech and AI have become the current political bogeyman for both parties

## Position
Politicians always need a villain to mobilize constituents; having cycled through Nazis, drug dealers, chemical companies, and cartels, big tech and AI are now the natural target because they are powerful, not fully understood by the public, and easy to characterize as threatening.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We always need a villain... it gets a little more complex... then Monsanto and these big chemical companies... We always sort of need somebody to protect my constituency from."
- Transcript: "Now that we've cycled through all that, I do think big tech is the current boogeyman."
- Transcript: "AI, that is the ultimate sort of pay no attention to the man behind the curtain... that's the evil wizard."
- Transcript: "Anything that America doesn't really fully understand makes it that much easier to pitch them and scare them."
- Transcript: Concerns about kids' screen time, pornography access, and AI harm cited as legitimate hooks politicians can exploit.

## Logic Chain
1. Political mobilization requires an identifiable threat that constituents fear.
2. Big tech and AI are now the largest, least-understood, most wealth-concentrated forces in public life.
3. Politicians on both left (anti-oligarch) and right (anti-censorship) have different but aligned motivations to target them.
4. Therefore tech and AI will face sustained bipartisan political pressure regardless of substantive policy merit.
