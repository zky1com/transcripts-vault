# Antonio Gracias - The surge in noncitizen Social Security numbers came mainly from asylum and notice-to-appear workflows that were expanded far beyond their original narrow use

## Position
Antonio Gracias argues that the sharp rise in Social Security numbers issued to noncitizens came mainly from asylum and notice-to-appear workflows that were expanded far beyond their intended use.

## Evidence Cited
- He says enumeration beyond entry had a normal baseline of roughly 300,000 to 400,000 people before ramping to more than 2 million.
- He says the vast majority of the growth was related to asylum programs and notices to appear, mostly at the border.
- He says a once-legitimate program had its aperture dramatically opened so many more people could come through.

## Logic Chain
1. Gracias ties the volume spike to specific immigration workflows rather than to normal visa processing.
2. He presents the change as a policy broadening of an existing channel, not a neutral administrative fluctuation.
3. That supports the claim that the surge came mainly from expanded asylum and notice-to-appear workflows.
