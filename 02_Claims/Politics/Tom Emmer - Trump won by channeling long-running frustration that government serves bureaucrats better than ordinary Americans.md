# Tom Emmer - Trump won by channeling long-running frustration that government serves bureaucrats better than ordinary Americans

## Position
Tom Emmer argues that Trump won by channeling long-running frustration that government serves bureaucrats better than ordinary Americans.

## Evidence Cited
- He says many Americans feel government has not listened to them for years.
- He says ordinary people believe bureaucrats and government insiders keep getting a better deal than Main Street.
- He credits Trump with understanding that energy and giving it political expression.

## Logic Chain
1. A durable political opening exists when voters feel ignored by governing institutions.
2. Emmer says Trump recognized and captured that frustration better than other Republicans had.
3. That is why he treats Trump's win as an expression of anti-bureaucratic populism.
