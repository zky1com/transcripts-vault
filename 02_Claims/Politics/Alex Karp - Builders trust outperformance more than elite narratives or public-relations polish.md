# Alex Karp - Builders trust outperformance more than elite narratives or public-relations polish

## Position
Alex Karp says builders ultimately trust outperformance against skepticism more than elite narratives or polished public relations.

## Evidence Cited
- He says highly talented builders discount what people say and judge accomplishment by outperforming expectations.
- He presents Palantir's credibility as rooted in results that beat the discount rate placed on the company.
- He contrasts that with the view that polished communications should determine legitimacy.

## Logic Chain
1. Karp treats performance as the durable source of trust among serious builders.
2. He says narrative management matters less than repeatedly beating skeptical expectations.
3. That supports the claim that builders trust outperformance more than elite storytelling.
