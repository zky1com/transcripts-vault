# Andrew Schulz - Democrats lose voters when they sound like elites who think they know better than ordinary people

## Position
Andrew Schulz argues that Democrats alienate voters when they speak with an Ivy League, expert-knows-best posture instead of meeting people where they are.

## Evidence Cited
- He says there is an Ivy League pretentiousness that tells people experts know what is best for them.
- He calls that tone condescending.
- He contrasts it with leaders who listen directly to what people say they want.

## Logic Chain
1. Schulz sees tone and posture as part of the substantive political problem.
2. He argues voters resent being talked down to more than Democrats appreciate.
3. So the claim captures his view that elite condescension is electorally damaging.
