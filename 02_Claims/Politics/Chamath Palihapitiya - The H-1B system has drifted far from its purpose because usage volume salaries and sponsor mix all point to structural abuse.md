# Chamath Palihapitiya - The H-1B system has drifted far from its purpose because usage volume salaries and sponsor mix all point to structural abuse

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that the H-1B system has drifted far from its original purpose because usage volume, salary levels, and sponsor mix all point to structural abuse.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the program was intended for about 85,000 visas but actual annual usage has at times been many multiples of that.
- He says the average pay is under roughly $120,000, which does not fit the story that these are mostly world-class elite specialists.
- He says a large share goes to foreign outsourcing firms rather than to American companies hiring rare talent.

## Logic Chain
1. Palihapitiya uses three separate indicators to test the official narrative of the program.
2. Each indicator points away from rare-talent recruitment and toward labor-cost arbitrage.
3. That supports the claim that the system has drifted into structural abuse.
