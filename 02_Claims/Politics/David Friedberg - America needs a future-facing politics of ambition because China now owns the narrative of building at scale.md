# David Friedberg - America needs a future-facing politics of ambition because China now owns the narrative of building at scale

## Position
David Friedberg argues that America needs a future-facing politics of ambition because China has taken over the narrative of building at scale.

## Evidence Cited
- He says America historically defined itself through ambition from westward expansion to the moon landing and the transistor revolution.
- He says China has built 30,000 miles of high-speed rail and is set to add more electricity production capacity than the US times two has today.
- He says American political debate is trapped in fixing the past instead of talking about where the country can lead the world next.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg contrasts an older American builder identity with current political backwardness.
2. He uses China as the live example of a nation projecting ambitious future-building at scale.
3. That supports the claim that America needs a future-facing politics of ambition.
