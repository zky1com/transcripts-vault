# Francis Suarez - Severe addiction is the core driver of visible street homelessness in big cities

## Position
Francis Suarez argues that severe mental illness and addiction are the main drivers of visible street homelessness in major cities.

## Evidence Cited
- He says more than 80 percent of Miami's homeless population appears to be dealing with mental illness or addiction.
- He agrees that meth and opioids are a major part of the street-homelessness problem.
- He says simply giving addicted people homes does not solve the underlying issue.

## Logic Chain
1. Suarez describes visible homelessness as tightly linked to untreated addiction and mental-health problems.
2. He argues that housing-only answers miss the main mechanism keeping people on the street.
3. That supports the claim that severe addiction is central to the problem.
