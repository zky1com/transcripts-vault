# Alex Karp - An orderly border and stronger public safety are pro-worker and therefore genuinely progressive

## Position
Alex Karp argues that border control and public order are pro-worker policies and therefore more genuinely progressive than open-border rhetoric.

## Evidence Cited
- He says progressives are harming poor people through permissive policies on drugs, crime, and borders.
- He argues that closed borders raise the value of domestic labor and preserve legal protections.
- He says public safety failures and fentanyl deaths fall disproportionately on poorer communities.

## Logic Chain
1. Karp ties progressive legitimacy to whether policy materially improves life for ordinary workers.
2. He argues that permissive disorder undermines that standard instead of meeting it.
3. That supports the claim that orderly borders and stronger public safety are genuinely pro-worker.
