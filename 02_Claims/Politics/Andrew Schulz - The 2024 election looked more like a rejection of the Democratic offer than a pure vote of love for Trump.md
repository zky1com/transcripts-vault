# Andrew Schulz - The 2024 election looked more like a rejection of the Democratic offer than a pure vote of love for Trump

## Position
Andrew Schulz says the 2024 outcome is better understood as voters rejecting Democrats rather than simply embracing Trump out of personal devotion.

## Evidence Cited
- He says people rejected the Democratic proposal and then chose the alternative.
- He argues that interpretation is harder for Democrats because it forces self-reflection.
- He contrasts that with the simpler story that voters just fell in love with Trump.

## Logic Chain
1. Schulz is distinguishing a protest or rejection vote from a purely affirmative mandate.
2. He says Democrats misunderstand the result if they frame it only as Trump charisma.
3. That supports the claim that the election was substantially a rejection of Democrats.
