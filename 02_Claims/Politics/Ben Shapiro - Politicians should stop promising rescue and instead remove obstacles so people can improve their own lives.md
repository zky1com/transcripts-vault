# Ben Shapiro - Politicians should stop promising rescue and instead remove obstacles so people can improve their own lives

## Position
Ben Shapiro argues that politicians should stop promising to rescue people and instead focus on removing obstacles so individuals can improve their own lives.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he would not promise to fix people's lives because he really cannot do that.
- He says the role of government is to get obstacles out of the way so people can fix their own lives and make them better.
- He says America should encourage people to strive, succeed, and try again if they fail.

## Logic Chain
1. Shapiro rejects paternal political promises as both dishonest and ineffective.
2. He reframes the state's role around enabling individual agency rather than substituting for it.
3. That supports the claim that politicians should remove obstacles instead of promising rescue.
