# Tucker Carlson - Punishing innocents is immoral whether it is done by America Israel or any other state

## Position
Tucker Carlson argues that punishing innocents is immoral no matter which government does it, and that principle should apply universally.

## Evidence Cited
- He says governments should punish the guilty, not children or other innocent people.
- He explicitly extends that principle to the United States, Israel, and any other state.
- He treats universal innocence-based moral limits as the basis of justice.

## Logic Chain
1. Carlson grounds his view in a cross-national moral rule rather than a country-specific grievance.
2. He argues that abandoning that rule excuses state violence against people who did nothing wrong.
3. That supports the claim that punishing innocents is immoral regardless of which state does it.
