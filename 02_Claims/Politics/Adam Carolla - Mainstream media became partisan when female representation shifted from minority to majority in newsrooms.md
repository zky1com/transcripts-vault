# Adam Carolla - Mainstream media became partisan when female representation shifted from minority to majority in newsrooms

## Position
As newsrooms shifted from roughly 12% female to 57% female, coverage became more emotionally driven and partisan rather than objective — because women are more likely to choose sides and advocate rather than report.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Newsrooms were — 15, 20 years ago — 12% female or the news outlets... had a minority of women. Now it's 57%."
- Transcript: "If you have now 57% women running the colleges or 57% of the women at the New York Times or the LA Times, well then they're going to sort of pick a side... what kind of articles are we going to print?"
- Transcript: Leslie Stahl cited as example: "That's a woman arguing with someone she hates" rather than a reporter asking for evidence about Hunter Biden's laptop.
- Transcript: The NYT op-ed example — women on staff pushed to fire the editor over Tom Cotton's op-ed about sending the National Guard to stop Antifa.
- Transcript: "They discredited themselves... they burned and that's what happened to them."

## Logic Chain
1. Newsrooms went from male-dominated to female-majority in a short period.
2. Carolla argues women are more likely to make emotional attachments to political sides.
3. Partisan attachment produces selective coverage and factual distortion.
4. Therefore the composition shift drove the loss of objective editorial standards in mainstream media.
