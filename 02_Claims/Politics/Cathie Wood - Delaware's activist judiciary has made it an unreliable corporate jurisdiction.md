# Cathie Wood - Delaware's activist judiciary has made it an unreliable corporate jurisdiction

## Position
Delaware courts have become activist and unpredictable — overriding shareholder-approved compensation packages and enabling minority shareholders to effectively veto majority decisions — making Delaware an unreliable home for corporate governance, which is why ARK itself is reincorporating elsewhere.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "We are moving out of Delaware." [ARK itself reincorporating]
- Transcript: "They're not predictable now and they're activist."
- Transcript: "What business do they have overriding the shareholders of Tesla when it comes to a pay package? All those shareholders who did that drive-by lawsuit twice."
- Transcript: A shareholder who owned just 10 shares and made a 20-bagger was allowed to sue twice to rescind shareholder-approved Musk compensation.
- Transcript: "It's in a bizarre way... It's like [someone] suing Uber."

## Logic Chain
1. Delaware's Court of Chancery has historically been the gold standard for predictable corporate law.
2. Recent rulings have allowed minority shareholders to override shareholder-ratified executive compensation packages.
3. This unpredictability creates legal risk for corporations incorporated in Delaware.
4. Therefore companies seeking governance stability are incentivized to reincorporate in more predictable jurisdictions.
