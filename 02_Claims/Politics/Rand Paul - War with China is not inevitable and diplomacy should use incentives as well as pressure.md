# Rand Paul - War with China is not inevitable and diplomacy should use incentives as well as pressure

## Position
Rand Paul argues that war with China is not inevitable and diplomacy should use incentives as well as pressure.

## Evidence Cited
- He recalls a Pacific commander telling him to make clear that war with China is not inevitable.
- He criticizes diplomats who lecture China publicly instead of offering transactional off-ramps.
- He says sanctions and pressure should be paired with rewards if behavior changes.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul rejects deterministic framing about superpower conflict.
2. If diplomacy offers only punishment and no incentive to change, it becomes less likely to alter behavior peacefully.
3. That supports the claim that diplomacy must use incentives as well as pressure.
