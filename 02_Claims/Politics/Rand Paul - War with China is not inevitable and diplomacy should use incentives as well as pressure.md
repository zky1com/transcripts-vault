# Rand Paul - War with China is not inevitable and diplomacy should use incentives as well as pressure

## Position
Rand Paul says war with China is not inevitable and argues that US diplomacy should offer incentives alongside threats and sanctions.

## Evidence Cited
- He quotes a Pacific military commander telling him that war with China is not inevitable.
- He says US diplomacy should not rely only on punishment and public scolding.
- He gives examples where he would pair demands with sanction relief or other incentives.

## Logic Chain
1. Paul rejects deterministic conflict framing with China.
2. He argues for a more transactional carrot-and-stick diplomacy.
3. Therefore the claim captures his non-inevitability and incentive-based diplomacy view.
