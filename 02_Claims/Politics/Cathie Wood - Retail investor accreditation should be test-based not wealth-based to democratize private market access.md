# Cathie Wood - Retail investor accreditation should be test-based not wealth-based to democratize private market access

## Position
Restricting private market investing to wealthy accredited investors is un-American and economically absurd — a knowledge test would better protect investors while enabling participation by people who understand and use the products they want to invest in.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "It is unamerican... You use chat GPT every day, but you can't buy OpenAI. Exactly. But you can buy a lottery ticket or you can bet on sports and it makes no sense."
- Transcript: "Why not just have a simple accreditation test? You understand diversification, you understand private versus public assets, how to read a balance sheet — wouldn't that just solve the problem?"
- Transcript: "What we're doing in the investment world right now would be the equivalent of saying you can't drive because you don't make enough money or you do not have enough net worth. Take a test."
- Transcript: Polarization of wealth tied to exclusion: "The people who don't [have equity exposure), they tend to trend towards socialism or handouts."
- Transcript: ARK has launched a venture fund specifically to address retail investors' desire to access private market innovation.

## Logic Chain
1. The current accreditation system restricts private market access to high-net-worth individuals regardless of financial sophistication.
2. This prevents ordinary people from investing in companies they use daily (OpenAI, Uber, Coinbase pre-IPO) while allowing them to gamble at casinos.
3. A knowledge-based test would protect genuinely unsophisticated investors while enabling sophisticated-but-not-wealthy investors to participate.
4. Therefore the reform that best balances protection and fairness is a test-based accreditation system.
