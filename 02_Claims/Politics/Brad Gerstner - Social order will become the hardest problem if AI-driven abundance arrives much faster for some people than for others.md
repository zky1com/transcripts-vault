# Brad Gerstner - Social order will become the hardest problem if AI-driven abundance arrives much faster for some people than for others

## Position
Brad Gerstner argues that social order will become the hardest problem if AI-driven abundance arrives much faster for some people than for others.

## Evidence Cited
- He says diffusion of innovation does not hit everyone at once.
- He says the current rate of change is creating highly asymmetric outcomes in who realizes the benefits first.
- He says once AI starts removing scarcity across domains, the biggest challenge that emerges is social order.

## Logic Chain
1. Gerstner treats unequal timing of benefits as socially destabilizing even if the technology is overall positive.
2. He frames social coordination, not technical capacity, as the hardest downstream constraint.
3. That supports the claim that social order becomes the hardest problem under uneven AI abundance.
