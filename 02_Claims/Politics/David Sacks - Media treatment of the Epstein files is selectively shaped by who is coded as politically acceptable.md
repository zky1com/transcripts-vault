# David Sacks - Media treatment of the Epstein files is selectively shaped by who is coded as politically acceptable

## Position
David Sacks argues that media treatment of the Epstein files is selective and depends heavily on who is coded as politically acceptable.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the New York Times gave Jason a major photo despite what he considered a minor tangential connection.
- He says right-coded figures like Peter Thiel, Elon, and Jason became the approved targets.
- He says people like Reed Hoffman and Bill Gates, who he described as having deeper relationships with Epstein, were largely spared in the article.

## Logic Chain
1. Sacks compares the relative depth of relationships with the relative prominence of coverage.
2. He concludes that editorial emphasis is being driven by political coding rather than by the strength of the underlying connection.
3. That supports the claim that Epstein-file coverage is selectively shaped by who is politically acceptable to target.
