# Antonio Gracias - Border identity controls were weak enough that many records lacked fingerprints and obvious placeholder birthdates

## Position
Antonio Gracias argues that border identity controls were weak enough that many records lacked fingerprints and contained obvious placeholder birthdates.

## Evidence Cited
- He says there was no real verification in the process he reviewed.
- He says 23 percent of the records they looked at did not have fingerprints.
- He says the most common birth date was January 1 and it was four times more likely than any other day, indicating defaults or fabricated entries.

## Logic Chain
1. Gracias points to missing biometrics and implausible birth-date concentration as direct signs of weak controls.
2. He treats those data defects as evidence the system accepted low-quality or unverified identity records.
3. That supports the claim that border identity controls were weak enough to allow missing fingerprints and placeholder birthdates.
