# Francis Suarez - Cities repel investment when they combine high taxes low safety and hostility to innovation

## Position
Francis Suarez says cities drive away companies and investment when they combine high taxes, weak safety, and a hostile posture toward innovation.

## Evidence Cited
- He summarizes his formula as keeping taxes low, keeping people safe, and leaning into innovation.
- He contrasts Miami's posture with places that rejected projects like Amazon HQ2 or signaled open hostility toward business leaders.
- He says those signals matter because they shape whether firms feel welcome enough to invest or relocate.

## Logic Chain
1. Suarez presents business attraction as a signaling problem as much as a cost problem.
2. He says anti-business symbolism compounds high-cost, low-safety conditions.
3. That supports the claim that cities repel investment when those conditions stack together.
