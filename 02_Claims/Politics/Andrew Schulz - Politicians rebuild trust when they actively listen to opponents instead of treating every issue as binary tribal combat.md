# Andrew Schulz - Politicians rebuild trust when they actively listen to opponents instead of treating every issue as binary tribal combat

## Position
Andrew Schulz says politicians can regain credibility when they actively listen and admit issues are often 60-40 or 55-45 questions rather than rigid one-or-zero party tests.

## Evidence Cited
- He praises active listening as a trust-building behavior that breaks down defensive walls.
- He says modern politics wrongly turns every issue into a binary one or zero.
- He argues many real disagreements are partial, not absolute.

## Logic Chain
1. Schulz connects listening behavior with lower audience defensiveness.
2. He says politics becomes distorted when parties force every issue into total alignment.
3. That supports the claim that trust grows when leaders engage complexity and listen.
