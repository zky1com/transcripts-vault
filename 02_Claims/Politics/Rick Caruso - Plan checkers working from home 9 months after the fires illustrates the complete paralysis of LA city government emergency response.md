# Rick Caruso - Plan checkers working from home 9 months after the fires illustrates the complete paralysis of LA city government emergency response

## Position
Nine months after the Palisades fire destroyed 7,000 homes, Los Angeles plan checkers — the city staff who must approve building permits before reconstruction can begin — are still working remotely from home rather than being stationed in the affected areas. This means permit processing continues at pre-disaster bureaucratic pace despite an emergency of unprecedented scale requiring immediate responses.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Since COVID we have still not, we being the city has still not called back workers to city hall. So plan checkers in LA city are working from home. You just lost 7,000 homes. You would think the first thing the mayor would do is say, 'You 10 plan checkers are now out in the Palisades in a construction trailer where the people need you.' They're still working out of a home."
- Transcript: "It's slower than it should be... slowing it down is an enormous amount of red tape."

## Logic Chain
1. LA city plan checkers must approve building permits before any reconstruction can begin.
2. 7,000 homes were destroyed requiring immediate permit processing at emergency scale.
3. Plan checkers continue working from home rather than being redeployed to affected areas 9 months later.
4. Remote work maintains the same bureaucratic pace as normal operations rather than emergency response pace.
5. The failure to redeploy plan checkers signals an absence of urgency in the mayor's office.
