# David Sacks - Greenland makes more strategic sense as part of the US security sphere than as a Danish possession

## Position
David Sacks argues that Greenland makes more strategic sense as part of the US security sphere than as a Danish possession.

## Evidence Cited
- He says new Arctic shipping lanes and national-security dynamics make Greenland more important.
- He notes that American policymakers have wanted Greenland before and that the idea is not new.
- He says its location and scale make it more naturally aligned with North American security interests.

## Logic Chain
1. Sacks treats Greenland as a strategic geography problem, not just a symbolic land deal.
2. He argues that Arctic change increases the US case for deeper control or alignment.
3. That supports the claim that Greenland makes more strategic sense in the US security sphere.
