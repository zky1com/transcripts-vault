# David Friedberg - Inflation and economic scarcity make regime instability in Iran increasingly inevitable

## Position
David Friedberg argues that extreme inflation and economic scarcity are making regime instability in Iran increasingly inevitable.

## Evidence Cited
- He points to years of very high inflation in Iran and low monthly incomes.
- He says the cost of basic needs eventually pushes people to stand up against an oppressive system.
- He frames sanctions pressure as part of what is breaking the regime's economic base.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg treats material scarcity as the condition that eventually overwhelms fear.
2. He argues that once basic life becomes unaffordable, civil stability becomes very hard to maintain.
3. That supports the claim that inflation and scarcity are making instability in Iran increasingly inevitable.
