# Francis Suarez - Miami pushed unsheltered homelessness to an 11-year low through functional-zero tactics

## Position
Francis Suarez says Miami drove unsheltered homelessness to an 11-year low by combining a stronger local economy with practical functional-zero tactics.

## Evidence Cited
- He says Miami's January census found 546 unsheltered homeless people, the city's lowest level in 11 years.
- He says the city is pursuing functional zero rather than accepting homelessness as permanent.
- He says Miami uses family reunification and rented housing with wraparound services to move faster than traditional construction pipelines.

## Logic Chain
1. Suarez presents homelessness reduction as an operational problem, not just a rhetorical one.
2. He names the concrete tactics Miami uses to move people indoors and reconnect them to support systems.
3. That supports the claim that Miami's lower homelessness came from functional-zero tactics.
