# Adam Carolla - Permitting and regulatory burden is the primary reason California fire victims cannot rebuild

## Position
California's permitting system, Coastal Commission rules, and regulatory layers are so cumbersome that virtually no homes were rebuilt a year after the Palisades fire — a predictable outcome Carolla stated on air 8 hours after the fire started.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "I guarantee you that. Good luck pulling a permit... it's been a year. There's nothing rebuilt in Malibu."
- Transcript: "I said right after the fire: 'Do not expect any rebuilding. You guys have no idea what the permitting process is.'"
- Transcript: Coastal Commission drove his friends (Allan Hamill) to abandon a Malibu rebuild and move to Palm Springs after years of regulatory obstruction.
- Transcript: As of November 2025, only one home has been rebuilt out of ~5,000 destroyed.
- Transcript: "It is not only really expensive but super time consuming and basically convinces you to scrub the project."

## Logic Chain
1. California's regulatory environment requires engineering sign-offs, plan checks, Coastal Commission approval, and city permits before rebuilding.
2. This process is so costly and time-consuming that many owners abandon projects entirely.
3. Therefore the regulatory burden — not logistics or funding — is the binding constraint on post-disaster recovery.
