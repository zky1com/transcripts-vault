# Maria Corina Machado - Venezuela can transition orderly because the public is broadly aligned on democratic restoration

## Position
Maria Corina Machado argues that Venezuela can have an orderly democratic transition because most citizens are aligned on ending the regime rather than fighting each other.

## Evidence Cited
- She rejects the idea that Maduro's fall would trigger civil war or deep social fragmentation.
- She says Venezuelans broadly want the same outcome and lack the religious, racial, or social fractures that would normally drive that scenario.
- She says years of resistance have prepared citizens to make the transition orderly.

## Logic Chain
1. Machado argues the regime's main remaining asset is coercion, not genuine mass support.
2. She says the opposition's organizing work has built enough civic alignment to support a controlled handoff.
3. That supports the claim that democratic restoration can be orderly rather than chaotic.
