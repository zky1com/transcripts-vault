# Maria Corina Machado - Natural-resource wealth does not produce prosperity without talent institutions and freedom

## Position
Maria Corina Machado argues that natural-resource abundance does not create prosperity unless a country also has talent, functioning institutions, and freedom.

## Evidence Cited
- She says Venezuela had extraordinary natural endowments yet still collapsed into mass poverty and exile.
- She explicitly says wealth requires talent, institutions, and freedom to turn resources into public well-being.
- She uses Venezuela as the example of how resource abundance alone cannot protect a country from state predation.

## Logic Chain
1. Machado rejects resource determinism as an explanation for national success.
2. She argues that institutional and civic conditions determine whether resource wealth becomes broad prosperity or concentrated plunder.
3. That supports the claim that freedom and institutions matter more than raw natural endowment alone.
