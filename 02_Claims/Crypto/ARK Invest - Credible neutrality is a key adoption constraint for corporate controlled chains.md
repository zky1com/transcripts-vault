# ARK Invest - Credible neutrality is a key adoption constraint for corporate controlled chains

## Position
The panel argues institutions may hesitate to build deeply on chains perceived as controlled by a direct commercial competitor.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript raises concern that corporate-run chains may be seen as insufficiently neutral by other institutions.
- Speakers discuss validator control and governance trust as adoption variables.
- Debate contrasts closed distribution advantages with longer-term openness requirements.

## Logic Chain
1. Competing institutions avoid dependency on infrastructure controlled by rivals.
2. Perceived governance bias can reduce willingness to commit strategic workloads.
3. Thus credible neutrality remains central for broader ecosystem adoption.
