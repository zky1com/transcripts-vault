# Vlad Tenev - Robinhood was the first platform to tokenize OpenAI and SpaceX equity making previously inaccessible private company shares available to retail investors in Europe

## Position
At an event in the south of France, Robinhood demonstrated tokenized versions of both OpenAI and SpaceX shares on blockchain rails, offering them to retail customers in Europe as part of a giveaway. Tenev claims Robinhood was the first to tokenize these specific private companies for retail access — creating a template for making illiquid private equity available globally.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "we were actually, I think, the first to tokenize OpenAI and SpaceX and we made that available to our retail customers in Europe in the form of a giveaway"
- Transcript mechanism: similar to stablecoin issuance — "you keep some dollars or treasuries in a bucket over here, you mint and burn tokens against that bucket back at one to one and the tokens can actually trade publicly on a variety of blockchains"
- Transcript: "I think the most powerful thing is taking inaccessible, illiquid assets and making them available"

## Logic Chain
1. Stablecoins work by holding a reserve asset and minting tokens at 1:1 on a blockchain.
2. The same mechanism can be applied to any asset — including private company equity held in a secure account.
3. Robinhood applied this to OpenAI and SpaceX shares, tokenizing the equity for retail distribution.
4. This demonstrates that tokenization removes the accredited investor and geography barriers for illiquid assets.
