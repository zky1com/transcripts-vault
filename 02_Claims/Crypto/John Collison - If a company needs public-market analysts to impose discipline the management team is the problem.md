# John Collison - If a company needs public-market analysts to impose discipline the management team is the problem

## Position
John Collison rejects the standard IPO-discipline argument and says a company that needs outside analysts for discipline has a management problem.

## Evidence Cited
- He calls the public-discipline argument "hogwash."
- He says if a company needs a young public-market analyst to force discipline, "something is horribly wrong."
- He says such a company needs new management.

## Logic Chain
1. John directly challenges the usual governance case for going public.
2. He ties the issue back to internal management quality.
3. Therefore the claim records his view that discipline should come from management, not public analysts.
