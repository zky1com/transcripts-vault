# Tom Lee - Equity wrappers around digital assets unlock institutional access that direct token mandates still block

## Position
Tom argues digital asset treasury equities exist because many institutions can buy corporate equities but cannot directly hold tokens or some fund wrappers.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript says many institutional mandates require corporate equity exposure rather than direct token ownership.
- Tom explains DAT structures bridge traditional portfolio constraints into crypto exposure.
- Discussion compares this to earlier periods where direct access routes were limited for major allocators.

## Logic Chain
1. Mandate constraints restrict direct token purchases for many institutions.
2. Equity wrappers fit existing compliance and portfolio infrastructure.
3. Therefore DAT equities become an access bridge for institutional demand.
