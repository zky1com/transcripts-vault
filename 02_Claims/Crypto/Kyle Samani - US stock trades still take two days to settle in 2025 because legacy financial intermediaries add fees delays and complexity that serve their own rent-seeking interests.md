# Kyle Samani - US stock trades still take two days to settle in 2025 because legacy financial intermediaries add fees delays and complexity that serve their own rent-seeking interests

## Position
The modern US stock market still operates on a T+2 settlement cycle in 2025, a direct product of layered intermediaries — exchanges, clearing houses, custodians — each adding fees and delays while resisting removal.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "It still takes 2 days to settle a stock trade in 2025"
- Transcript: "Investors have to go through exchanges. Exchanges go through clearing houses. Clearing houses go through custodians. Each of these players takes a cut. They add a fee. They add a delay."
- Transcript: "They create inertia in the status quo so that it's harder to remove them later"

## Logic Chain
1. The US financial system was built on intermediaries deputized to process regulatory compliance functions starting in the 1930s.
2. Each intermediary layer extracts rent (fees, delays) without corresponding value for end investors.
3. The result 90 years later is a system where a stock trade still takes 2 days to settle — slower than same-day crypto settlement.
4. This is not a technical constraint but an institutional/rent-seeking one.
