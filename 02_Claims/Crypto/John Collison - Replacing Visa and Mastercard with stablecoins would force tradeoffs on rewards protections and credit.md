# John Collison - Replacing Visa and Mastercard with stablecoins would force tradeoffs on rewards protections and credit

## Position
John Collison says any serious attempt to replace card networks with stablecoins would have to give something up in rewards, protections, or consumer credit.

## Evidence Cited
- He says most card interchange flows back into issuing banks and then to consumers.
- He asks whether a replacement would reduce rewards, consumer protections, or available credit.
- He concludes that the issue is a set of tradeoffs, not simple rent extraction.

## Logic Chain
1. John explains what current card economics fund.
2. He says removing that structure changes consumer outcomes.
3. Therefore the claim captures his view that stablecoin substitution involves tradeoffs, not free gains.
