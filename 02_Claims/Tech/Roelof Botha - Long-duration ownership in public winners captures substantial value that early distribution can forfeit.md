# 
Roelof Botha - Long-duration ownership in public winners captures substantial value that early distribution can forfeit

## Position
The interview argues that distributing shares too early after IPO often leaves significant value on the table versus disciplined long-duration ownership in true compounders.

## Evidence Cited
- Botha says many top companies compound for years after public listing.
- He reports Sequoia created billions in additional gains through post-IPO holding structures.
- Discussion cites multiple public-company examples of large secondary compounding phases.

## Logic Chain
1. Elite companies can continue compounding through later product cycles post-IPO.
2. Automatic early distribution can force selling before major value accrual.
3. Therefore selective long-duration hold structures can materially improve LP outcomes.
