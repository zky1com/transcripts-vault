# 
Roelof Botha - Most costly venture mistakes are often failures of imagination about market expansion

## Position
The interview frames major missed opportunities as underestimating how far early products can evolve into category-defining platforms.

## Evidence Cited
- Botha says his biggest investing mistakes came from not thinking big enough.
- He gives examples where early products looked limited but later became dominant franchises.
- He credits mentor influence on developing longer-horizon imaginative underwriting.

## Logic Chain
1. Early product shape often understates eventual platform potential.
2. Investors anchored to present form can miss nonlinear expansion paths.
3. Therefore imagination about future adoption vectors is critical in early-stage selection.
