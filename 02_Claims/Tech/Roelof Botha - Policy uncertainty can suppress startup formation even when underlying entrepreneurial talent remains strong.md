# 
Roelof Botha - Policy uncertainty can suppress startup formation even when underlying entrepreneurial talent remains strong

## Position
The interview argues unstable regulation can sharply reduce domestic startup creation while entrepreneurial activity migrates to friendlier jurisdictions.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript cites a large decline in annual company formation in China under uncertainty.
- Botha says entrepreneurial drive persists but founders relocate or build elsewhere.
- He warns US AI policy should avoid creating equivalent uncertainty shocks.

## Logic Chain
1. Founders require stable rule expectations to justify long-horizon risk taking.
2. When policy uncertainty rises, startup formation can shift geographically.
3. So regulatory instability can reduce local innovation density without eliminating entrepreneurship globally.
