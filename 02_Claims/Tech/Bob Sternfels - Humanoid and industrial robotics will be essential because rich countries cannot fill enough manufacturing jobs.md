# Bob Sternfels - Humanoid and industrial robotics will be essential because rich countries cannot fill enough manufacturing jobs

## Position
Bob Sternfels argues that robotics will become essential because advanced economies cannot fill enough manufacturing jobs to build resilient supply chains on their own.

## Evidence Cited
- He cites large manufacturers with tens of thousands of unfilled US jobs.
- He says demographics in places like Germany and Korea make the problem even worse.
- He frames robotics as the only realistic way to build resilient supply chains at scale.

## Logic Chain
1. Sternfels sees labor scarcity as a structural constraint, not a temporary mismatch.
2. He argues that robotics is therefore required to sustain competitive manufacturing capacity.
3. That supports the claim that robotics will be essential because rich countries cannot fill enough manufacturing jobs.
