# Dara Khosrowshahi - Long-term autonomous vehicle displacement of Uber drivers will be a major societal problem

## Position
In the near term (5-7 years), Uber's platform growth is fast enough that introducing Waymo vehicles in Austin has not reduced earnings for human drivers — Austin drivers earn as much or more than before. But Khosrowshahi acknowledges that 10-15 years out, AV-driven job displacement for drivers is 'a real issue' with no neat answer.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Austin drivers now are making as much or more money than they were before we introduced Whimo"
- Transcript: "for the next 5 to seven years we're going to have more human drivers and delivery people just because we're growing so quickly"
- Transcript: "10 to 15 years from now this is going to be a real issue. And Jason I don't have a neat answer for it"
- Transcript: China example — Wuhan limiting AV licenses due to civil unrest over displacement of male drivers

## Logic Chain
1. In Austin, Waymo introduction has not reduced driver earnings because overall ride demand growth exceeds AV supply.
2. This near-term buffer exists because ride-sharing is still only ~1-2% of total mobility trips globally.
3. However, as AV penetration rises over 10-15 years, driver job displacement becomes mathematically inevitable.
4. Uber's management acknowledges no solution exists beyond short-term adjustments like AI data labeling work.
