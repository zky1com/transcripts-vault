# John Martinis - Useful general-purpose quantum computing likely requires machines on the order of a million qubits

## Position
John Martinis says truly useful fault-tolerant quantum computing probably requires machines with roughly a million qubits because error correction and noise overhead dominate at practical scale.

## Evidence Cited
- He says current systems are around the hundred-qubit range.
- He explains that qubits are noisy and need error correction to refresh and stabilize computation.
- He says a million-qubit machine is a good round-number estimate for general-purpose usefulness.

## Logic Chain
1. Martinis distinguishes current experimental systems from fully useful error-corrected ones.
2. He attributes the gap mainly to noise and error-correction overhead.
3. That supports the claim that practical quantum computing likely needs around a million qubits.
