# David Sacks - The White House tech dinner showed Trump could convene rival tech leaders around a common pro-growth agenda

## Position
David Sacks argues that the White House tech dinner showed Trump could convene rival tech leaders around a common pro-growth agenda.

## Evidence Cited
- He says it was remarkable to get major competing companies into one room together.
- He says the group was largely aligned around a pro-innovation, pro-infrastructure, pro-export, and pro-worker agenda.
- He says the administration's AI and growth policies were the basis for much of the support in the room.

## Logic Chain
1. A president demonstrates convening power when rivals with distinct interests voluntarily show up and align around a shared agenda.
2. Sacks says that is what happened at the White House dinner.
3. That supports his claim that Trump had successfully convened tech leaders around a common pro-growth agenda.
