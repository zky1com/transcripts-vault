# Yan Stramic - America's infrastructure decline is entirely self-inflicted due to failure to build housing factories and energy

## Position
America's competitive decline relative to peers is 100% self-inflicted — a direct result of choosing not to build enough housing, office space, factories, freeways, trains, and energy infrastructure — proven by historical examples where America built at scale in years, not decades.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "All of it was entirely self-inflicted. This was a self goal. 100% of it. We failed to build enough housing, office space, factories, freeways, trains, and energy."
- Transcript: "The Golden Gate Bridge was built in four years. The 747 went from idea to passenger in three years."
- Transcript: California Forever's mission framed as restoring America's historical ability to build at speed.

## Logic Chain
1. Historical precedent proves America can build major infrastructure in years when it chooses to.
2. The current inability to build at scale reflects policy, regulatory, and institutional choices, not fundamental capacity constraints.
3. These choices have compounded over decades into a structural competitive disadvantage.
4. Therefore the solution is political and regulatory reform, not technological invention — the capability already exists.
