# Dara Khosrowshahi - Uber network density can raise AV revenue per car by reducing pickup times

## Position
Uber's core argument for why AV fleet operators should join its marketplace: a vehicle in Uber's dense network gets pickup requests from much closer proximity (3 minutes away) versus a standalone fleet (15 minutes away), dramatically increasing the ratio of revenue-generating miles to total miles driven and thus revenue per vehicle per day.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "instead of, you know, a pickup who's 15 minutes away for a 10-minute ride, you're going to get a pickup that's 3 minutes away for a 10-minute ride. So the utilization in terms of the revenue generating miles as a percentage of total miles driven is much much higher on our network"
- Transcript: "fleet player B who's working with us... will have many more miles that are creating revenue as a percentage of total miles driven and as a result each of their cars are going to get much more revenue per car per day"
- Transcript analogy: Every major food business (McDonald's) uses both direct channel and marketplace because marketplaces drive utilization

## Logic Chain
1. A vehicle's economic performance depends on the ratio of revenue-generating miles to total miles driven.
2. Higher ride request density (more nearby pickups) reduces deadhead miles and shortens wait times.
3. Uber's existing demand network provides that density advantage to partner AV fleets vs standalone operations.
4. Therefore AV fleet operators choosing Uber over going direct will generate more revenue per vehicle per day.
