# 
Roelof Botha - Venture fundraising persistence is sustained by delayed mark-to-market reality and J-curve narratives

## Position
The interview argues prolonged feedback loops and J-curve framing allow underperforming strategies to keep raising capital before true outcomes are visible.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript says managers can raise multiple funds before first-fund distributions are fully known.
- Botha notes investors often accept low interim multiples as temporary J-curve effects.
- He highlights long gestation periods as a structural reason capital discipline is delayed.

## Logic Chain
1. Long duration obscures true manager quality in early fundraising cycles.
2. Narrative explanations can postpone allocation correction by LPs.
3. Therefore capital can remain misallocated for multiple fund vintages.
