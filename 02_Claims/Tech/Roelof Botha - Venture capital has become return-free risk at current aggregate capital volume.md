# 
Roelof Botha - Venture capital has become return-free risk at current aggregate capital volume

## Position
The interview argues the venture industry now deploys more capital than plausible exit outcomes can support, compressing expected net returns for many LPs.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript estimates annual venture deployment around 150 to 200 billion dollars.
- Botha says required aggregate exit values to support target returns imply unrealistic yearly outcomes.
- He concludes the current setup often resembles return-free risk for allocators.

## Logic Chain
1. When deployed capital exceeds realistic exit capacity, median fund outcomes compress.
2. High dispersion means only a small subset of funds capture outlier gains.
3. Therefore broad allocator exposure to the asset class can underperform expectations.
