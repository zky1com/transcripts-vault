# Dara Khosrowshahi - LiDAR cost collapse makes autonomous-vehicle hardware economics viable at scale

## Position
LiDAR sensors — critical for AV perception — have undergone a 40–100x cost reduction from $20,000–$30,000 per unit ~5 years ago to $300–$500 today as solid-state designs replaced spinning mechanical units. This cost curve is cited as the enabling condition for commercially viable AV fleets.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "LAR was 20 30,000 bucks a pop like 5 6 years ago. Now solid state LAR is 300 to 500 bucks a pop"
- Transcript: "the cost of hardware is coming way down. It is going to need to continue to come down because these cars are very expensive"
- Transcript context: Khosrowshahi is explaining the economics that determine when third-party AV fleet owners can build profitable businesses on Uber's network

## Logic Chain
1. Mechanical spinning LiDAR was prohibitively expensive at $20K-$30K per unit.
2. Solid-state LiDAR achieves same function at $300-$500 per unit — a ~60x reduction.
3. This hardware cost reduction is the primary driver of AV commercial viability timelines.
4. Khosrowshahi frames continued cost reduction as necessary (not yet sufficient) condition for AV fleet economics.
