# Brad Gerstner - AI is forcing a repricing of software because substitution is reaching knowledge work rather than stopping at copilots

## Position
Brad Gerstner argues that AI is forcing a repricing of software because substitution is now reaching knowledge work instead of stopping at copilots.

## Evidence Cited
- He frames the software selloff as a direct response to AI tools entering white-collar workflows.
- He describes the shift as moving from simple assistance into actual substitution of tasks done by knowledge workers.
- He treats that transition as the reason old software valuations have to be reconsidered.

## Logic Chain
1. If AI tools begin replacing meaningful slices of knowledge work, then legacy software pricing and margin assumptions come under pressure.
2. Gerstner links that substitution dynamic directly to market repricing.
3. That supports the claim that AI is repricing software because it is reaching knowledge work.
