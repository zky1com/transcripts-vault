# Chamath Palihapitiya - AI competition is already disciplining Google strongly enough that breaking it up is less necessary

## Position
Chamath Palihapitiya argues that AI competition is already disciplining Google strongly enough that breaking it up is less necessary.

## Evidence Cited
- He says the judge was right to notice how quickly search competition changed after the case began.
- He points to user attention and revenue already shifting toward AI products outside Google.
- He says the free market is doing much of the corrective work that harsher antitrust remedies were supposed to do.

## Logic Chain
1. Structural remedies are less urgent when market competition is already breaking down a dominant position.
2. Chamath says AI has created exactly that kind of pressure on Google.
3. That supports his claim that a breakup is less necessary now than it once seemed.
