# John Martinis - Frontier science advances when researchers build tools and devices rather than only elegant theory

## Position
John Martinis says major scientific progress comes from building instruments and devices, not just generating ideas, and that this builder mindset shaped his own career in quantum physics.

## Evidence Cited
- He says his background involved building things and understanding how they worked.
- He says the field suited him because it combined great physics with building something real.
- He repeatedly describes his work in terms of instruments, fabrication, and experiments.

## Logic Chain
1. Martinis identifies experimental tool-building as central to scientific progress.
2. He treats hardware creation as inseparable from answering the physics questions.
3. That supports the claim that tools and devices are core to frontier science.
