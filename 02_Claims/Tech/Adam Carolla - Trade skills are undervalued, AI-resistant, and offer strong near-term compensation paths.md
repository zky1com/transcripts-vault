# Adam Carolla - Trade skills are undervalued, AI-resistant, and offer strong near-term compensation paths

## Position
Skilled tradespeople — electricians, plumbers, roofers, framers — are in extreme demand, earn $300+/day, and face no near-term displacement from AI, making trades a more defensible career choice than many white-collar paths currently vulnerable to automation.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "Malibu's burnt to the ground. Palisades are burnt to the ground. Do you know how many electricians and plumbers and framers and roofers and drywallers... we're going to need just here right now?"
- Transcript: "These guys ain't making 14 bucks an hour. They're making 300 bucks a day on the low end. There's good money and AI is not going to replace you in the near future."
- Transcript: Carolla has long advocated for restoring shop class: "I've always wanted kids... to get involved with the trades... 'What about shop class? Could someone save shop class?'"
- Transcript: "I've been a huge... let's bring shop back. We need it fan for a long time."

## Logic Chain
1. Physical skilled trades require dexterity, judgment, and on-site presence that current AI/robotics cannot replicate at scale.
2. Post-disaster rebuilding events (LA fires) create acute supply shortages in trades, driving compensation up.
3. Meanwhile AI is displacing white-collar analytical and creative roles more rapidly.
4. Therefore trades offer better near-term employment stability and compensation than many degree-track careers.
