# John Martinis - The United States should not assume it has a comfortable lead over China in superconducting quantum computing

## Position
John Martinis warns that Chinese researchers clearly understand the superconducting quantum-computing field and that the United States should not act as if its lead is secure.

## Evidence Cited
- He says when he reads the Chinese papers it is clear they know what they are doing.
- He notes that Chinese groups have produced work close to major US milestones soon after publication.
- He worries they may be holding results back until after Western publications appear.

## Logic Chain
1. Martinis is not dismissing Chinese work as derivative or unserious.
2. He treats China as a real peer competitor in the field.
3. That supports the claim that the US should not assume a comfortable lead.
