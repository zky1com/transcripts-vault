# David Friedberg - Power is the main bottleneck for AI, so the next wave of innovation will come from both escaping Earth constraints and slashing energy per token

## Position
David Friedberg argues that power is the main bottleneck for AI, so the next wave of innovation will come both from escaping Earth constraints and from slashing energy per token.

## Evidence Cited
- He says power is the requisite for scaling compute and therefore for scaling AI applications.
- He says one branch of innovation is to escape Earth-side constraints through space-based energy and data centers.
- He says another branch is 70x to 100x efficiency gains from chip and model architecture changes that reduce electricity per token.

## Logic Chain
1. Friedberg identifies energy, not demand, as the constraining variable for AI growth.
2. He then maps two solution paths: radical infrastructure expansion and radical efficiency improvement.
3. That supports the claim that the next wave comes from both escaping Earth constraints and reducing energy per token.
