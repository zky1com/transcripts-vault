# Brett Winton - Starship reliability is improving through iterative stress testing and rapid design feedback loops

## Position
The panel presents Starship as an iterative engineering program where repeated flight tests drive robustness gains.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript highlights successful test flight outcomes despite harsh thermal and control conditions.
- Speakers note deliberate stress experiments, including intentionally removed heat tiles, with continued survivability.
- Discussion contrasts this robustness with legacy aerospace systems where small failures often cause mission loss.

## Logic Chain
1. Fast test cycles reveal failure modes sooner and cheaper than slow certification-first cycles.
2. Rapid feedback enables continual hardening of weak components.
3. Thus iterative testing can accelerate reliability improvements.
