# Thomas Laffont - High costs and regulation are pushing film and software-adjacent production out of Los Angeles

## Position
Thomas Laffont says Los Angeles is losing production activity because costs, regulation, and weak local economics are pushing work to other regions.

## Evidence Cited
- He says LA is down materially on restaurant and film recovery versus other places.
- He points to entertainment as a local industry in secular decline and mentions filming being far below peak.
- The discussion explicitly contrasts LA with places offering better economics and policy support.

## Logic Chain
1. Thomas frames the issue as a structural competitiveness problem.
2. He ties it to costs, local policy, and industry decline.
3. Therefore the claim captures his view that production is being pushed out of LA.
