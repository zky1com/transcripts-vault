# Keller Rinaudo Cliffton - Autonomous logistics can save lives by extending reliable delivery into places that traditional systems fail to serve

## Position
Keller Rinaudo Cliffton argues that autonomous logistics matters because it extends fast reliable delivery into places where traditional systems fail, especially for medical care.

## Evidence Cited
- He says Rwanda pushed Zipline to start with blood delivery because delayed access was costing mothers and children their lives.
- He says millions of children still die for lack of access to basic medical products.
- He presents automated logistics as a way to reduce those failures while also expanding economic opportunity.

## Logic Chain
1. Rinaudo links logistics performance directly to health outcomes in underserved systems.
2. He argues that lower-cost autonomous delivery can expand access where existing networks are too slow or too weak.
3. That supports the claim that autonomous logistics can save lives by reaching places traditional systems do not serve well.
