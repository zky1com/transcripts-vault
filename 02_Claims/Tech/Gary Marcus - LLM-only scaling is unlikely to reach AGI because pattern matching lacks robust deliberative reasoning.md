# Gary Marcus - LLM-only scaling is unlikely to reach AGI because pattern matching lacks robust deliberative reasoning

## Position
Gary Marcus argues that scaling current LLM architectures does not solve core reasoning limitations needed for AGI.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: he distinguishes fast statistical processing from slower deliberative reasoning and says current neural systems are mostly the former.
- Transcript: he states large language models are not likely to reach AGI through scaling alone.

## Logic Chain
1. Marcus frames intelligence as requiring more than pattern recognition.
2. He says current LLM paradigm underweights reasoning structure.
3. The claim captures his anti-scaling-to-AGI thesis.
