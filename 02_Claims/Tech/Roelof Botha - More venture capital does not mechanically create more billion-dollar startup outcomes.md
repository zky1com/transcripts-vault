# 
Roelof Botha - More venture capital does not mechanically create more billion-dollar startup outcomes

## Position
The interview states that increasing fund capital supply does not proportionally increase the number of truly exceptional startup exits.

## Evidence Cited
- Botha says there are historically limited numbers of billion-dollar-plus real exits per decade.
- He argues great founders and ideas are not linearly produced by more investor capital.
- Discussion links excess capital to weaker underwriting discipline, not guaranteed innovation growth.

## Logic Chain
1. Exceptional founder-company combinations are scarce relative to available capital.
2. Capital abundance can inflate entry prices without increasing fundamental quality.
3. So excess capital lowers return efficiency rather than manufacturing elite outcomes.
