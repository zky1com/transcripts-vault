# Marty Makary - Medical dogma and paternalism are structural problems in healthcare, not just temporary pandemic distortions

## Position
Marty Makary argues that medical dogma and paternalism are structural problems in healthcare, not just temporary pandemic distortions.

## Evidence Cited
- He calls the sociology of medical dogma a longstanding feature of medicine.
- He says the system still suffers from paternalism and suppression of non-legacy ideas.
- He uses masking, mandates, and school closures as examples of a broader problem rather than a one-off anomaly.

## Logic Chain
1. Makary treats pandemic excesses as symptoms of a deeper institutional style.
2. If the same habits predate and outlast COVID, then the problem is structural rather than temporary.
3. That supports the claim that dogma and paternalism are structural problems.
