# David Friedberg - A VA database study of 215000 GLP-1 patients versus 1.2 million diabetic controls found a 30 percent reduction in cardiac arrest probability among GLP-1 users

## Position
A research team from St. Louis analyzed VA database records covering 1.2M diabetes patients without GLP-1, 215K GLP-1 users, and 600K on other diabetes drugs. The GLP-1 cohort showed a 30% reduction in cardiac arrest probability, along with reductions in hepatic failure, respiratory failure, and schizophrenia risk.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "this research team out of St Louis pulled all the data from the VA database and basically they looked at you know 1.2 million people with diabetes that didn't take anything compared to 215,000 that took the glp1 receptor agonists and another 600,000 people that took other drugs for diabetes"
- Transcript: "on cardiac arrest you see a 30% decrease in the probability of having cardiac arrest from the cohort that's on the gp1 versus not"
- Transcript: "you have a decrease in shock, a decrease in hepatic failure, respiratory failure, cardiac arrest... schizophrenia"
- Transcript: "the only thing that increased is like 8% or 10% increase in nausea and vomiting... muscular skeletal complications, GERD... sleep disturbance"

## Logic Chain
1. Large cohort study (1.2M+ patients) with proper control groups isolates GLP-1 specific effects.
2. VA data is especially high quality — comprehensive medical records across a large, tracked population.
3. The 30% cardiac arrest reduction is a hazard ratio reduction from the specific GLP-1 cohort vs non-GLP-1 diabetics.
4. Multiple serious conditions (liver failure, respiratory failure, schizophrenia) also declined, suggesting systemic effects.
