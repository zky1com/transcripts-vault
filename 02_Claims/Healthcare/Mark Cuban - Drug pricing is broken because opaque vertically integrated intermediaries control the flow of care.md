# Mark Cuban - Drug pricing is broken because opaque vertically integrated intermediaries control the flow of care

## Position
Mark Cuban says drug pricing is broken because vertically integrated insurers and pharmacy-benefit managers control access and pricing through opaque systems.

## Evidence Cited
- He says no one knows the real price of medications because the system was designed without transparency.
- He argues PBMs and insurers profit from formularies, tiers, and internal transfer arrangements that hide the real economics.
- He describes the resulting markups as wildly disconnected from manufacturing cost.

## Logic Chain
1. Cuban treats opacity and vertical integration as the mechanism that allows irrational pricing to persist.
2. He says patients and employers cannot discipline the market because the relevant data is hidden.
3. That supports the claim that opaque intermediaries are the core reason drug pricing is broken.
