# Dave Ricks - Biomedical innovation requires leaving enough economic upside to fund the next generation of drugs

## Position
Dave Ricks argues that even when a drug is socially valuable and under price pressure, the economics cannot be pushed so low that they destroy the incentive and capacity to fund the next wave of research.

## Evidence Cited
- He says if prices were crushed too far there would be no next generation of medicines in the category.
- He points to Lilly's large R&D spending as something that must be funded by revenue.
- He explicitly frames pricing as a balance between access and future innovation.

## Logic Chain
1. Ricks is not denying access concerns; he is arguing for an intertemporal tradeoff.
2. He says current profits finance future therapeutic breakthroughs.
3. That supports the claim that some economic upside must remain to sustain innovation.
