# Marty Makary - The United States is losing biotech competitiveness because slow trials contracting and reviews push early-stage work overseas

## Position
Marty Makary argues that the United States is losing biotech competitiveness because slow trials, contracting, and reviews push early-stage work overseas.

## Evidence Cited
- He says China and Australia are running phase-one processes in weeks while US systems take far longer.
- He blames fragmented hospital contracting, slow IRBs, and sluggish review timelines.
- He says the country was losing the race when he entered office because others were operating with less red tape.

## Logic Chain
1. If early-stage study setup is materially faster abroad, companies will shift work there.
2. That migration weakens domestic competitiveness and signals process failure at home.
3. That supports the claim that slow trials and reviews are pushing work overseas.
