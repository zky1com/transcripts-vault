# Chamath Palihapitiya - CT angiogram progression can be severe despite a zero calcium score

## Position
Chamath's cardiologist (Dr. Carlsburg) performed a CT angiogram with AI-assisted image analysis and found that over a 10-year period, Chamath had a 370% increase in arterial plaque material (total plaque volume) despite maintaining a zero calcium score. This demonstrates that zero calcium score does not rule out clinically relevant plaque progression.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "you have a zero calcium score and you still have a zero calcium score"
- Transcript: "We see that over the 10 years you had a 370% increase in that material, which means that you are on a pathway to atherosclerosis"
- Transcript: "you're one of the few peoples on the planet that have this kind of information"
- Transcript context: CT angiogram with contrast and AI model imaging revealed the plaque despite clean calcium score

## Logic Chain
1. Calcium score (CACS) is the standard non-invasive cardiovascular screening metric.
2. A zero calcium score is typically interpreted as very low near-term cardiovascular risk.
3. CT angiogram with contrast provides direct visualization of plaque, including non-calcified soft plaque invisible to calcium scoring.
4. A 370% increase in total plaque volume while maintaining zero calcium score = calcium score is an incomplete cardiovascular risk metric.
