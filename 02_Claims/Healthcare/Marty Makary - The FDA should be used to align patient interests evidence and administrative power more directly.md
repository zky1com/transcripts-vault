# Marty Makary - The FDA should be used to align patient interests evidence and administrative power more directly

## Position
Marty Makary argues that the FDA should be used to align patient interests, evidence, and administrative power more directly.

## Evidence Cited
- He says his bias comes from standing at the bedside with patients who have incurable conditions.
- He argues the agency should move at the speed patients demand rather than at government speed.
- He describes reforms aimed at faster pathways, clearer rejection letters, and less procedural drag.

## Logic Chain
1. Makary ties administrative process to real patient costs from delay.
2. If evidence supports action and the patient need is urgent, administrative power should shorten rather than prolong access.
3. That supports the claim that the FDA should align patient interests, evidence, and power more directly.
