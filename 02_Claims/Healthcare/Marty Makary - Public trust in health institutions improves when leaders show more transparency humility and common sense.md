# Marty Makary - Public trust in health institutions improves when leaders show more transparency humility and common sense

## Position
Marty Makary says health institutions regain trust when leaders communicate with more transparency, humility, and common sense instead of leaning on closed authority and rigid certainty.

## Evidence Cited
- He blends transparency common sense and scientific humility in describing the role.
- He says the conversation is strongest on incentives and public trust.
- He contrasts that with more dogmatic institutional behavior.

## Logic Chain
1. Makary treats trust as downstream of posture and institutional style, not just technical competence.
2. He identifies humility and transparency as core trust-building behaviors.
3. That supports the claim that public trust improves with those traits.
