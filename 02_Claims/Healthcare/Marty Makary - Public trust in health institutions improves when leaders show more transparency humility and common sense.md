# Marty Makary - Public trust in health institutions improves when leaders show more transparency humility and common sense

## Position
Marty Makary argues that public trust in health institutions improves when leaders show more transparency, humility, and common sense.

## Evidence Cited
- He says one goal of his office is rebuilding public trust in health institutions.
- He links trust losses to absolutism and the suppression of dissent during COVID.
- He says reforms like public rejection letters and common-sense decision making increase accountability.

## Logic Chain
1. Makary presents trust as an output of behavior rather than branding.
2. When institutions are more transparent and less paternalistic, the public has more reason to believe them.
3. That supports the claim that trust improves with transparency, humility, and common sense.
