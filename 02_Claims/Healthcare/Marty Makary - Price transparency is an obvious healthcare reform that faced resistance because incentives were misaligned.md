# Marty Makary - Price transparency is an obvious healthcare reform that faced resistance because incentives were misaligned

## Position
Marty Makary argues that price transparency is an obvious healthcare reform that faced resistance because incentives were misaligned.

## Evidence Cited
- He says he led a national effort to push hospital price transparency.
- He describes the idea as simple common sense that still met institutional resistance.
- He presents the eventual executive order as proof that the obstacle was not concept difficulty but incentive misalignment.

## Logic Chain
1. A reform that is easy to explain yet hard to adopt often signals incumbent incentives protecting opacity.
2. Makary uses the slow path to obvious transparency as evidence of that misalignment.
3. That supports the claim that price transparency faced resistance because incentives were misaligned.
