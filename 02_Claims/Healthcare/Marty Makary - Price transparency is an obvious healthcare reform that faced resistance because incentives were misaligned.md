# Marty Makary - Price transparency is an obvious healthcare reform that faced resistance because incentives were misaligned

## Position
Marty Makary says price transparency is one of the clearest examples of a commonsense healthcare reform that faced irrational resistance because institutional incentives were badly aligned.

## Evidence Cited
- He presents price transparency as one of his clearest reform examples.
- He says it should have been obvious but still faced resistance.
- He ties that resistance to deeper incentive problems in the system.

## Logic Chain
1. Makary treats transparency as low-hanging reform rather than radical redesign.
2. He says the resistance reveals how distorted the incentives are.
3. That supports the claim that price transparency faced resistance because of misaligned incentives.
