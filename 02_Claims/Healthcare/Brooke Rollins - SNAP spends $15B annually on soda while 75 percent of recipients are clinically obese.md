# Brooke Rollins - SNAP spends $15B annually on soda while 75 percent of recipients are clinically obese

## Position
The SNAP program is a $123B/year program where 13% of Americans are enrolled; roughly $15B goes to soda purchases annually while 75% of recipients are clinically obese and 74% of American teenagers would fail military readiness tests — demonstrating a fundamental misalignment between program spending and health outcomes.

## Evidence Cited
- Transcript: "13% of Americans are on SNAP at $123 billion a year. Roughly $15 billion a year is spent on soda."
- Transcript: "75% of the SNAP population are clinically obese."
- Transcript: "74% of our teenagers would not pass the military readiness test."
- Transcript: Rollins presenting this data as justification for reforming what SNAP benefits can purchase.

## Logic Chain
1. SNAP's stated purpose is nutritional assistance, not caloric assistance.
2. Allowing $15B in soda purchases actively funds obesity while the majority of recipients are already clinically obese.
3. Military readiness data connects nutritional dysfunction to national security capability gaps.
4. Therefore SNAP purchasing rules require reform to align spending with health outcomes.
