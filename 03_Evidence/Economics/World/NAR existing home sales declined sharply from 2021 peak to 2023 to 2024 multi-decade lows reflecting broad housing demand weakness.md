# NAR existing home sales declined sharply from 2021 peak to 2023 to 2024 multi-decade lows reflecting broad housing demand weakness

## Core Evidence
NAR data shows existing home sales fell from approximately 6.1 million in 2021 to 4.09 million in 2023 and further to 4.06 million in 2024 — the lowest annual level since 1995. This sector-wide decline in existing-home activity affected multiple housing-linked businesses simultaneously, supporting the claim that weak earnings from individual builders or product suppliers reflected a broad structural demand constraint rather than company-specific execution failures.

## Verification Notes
- NAR annual data confirmed on 2026-02-26; the 2021-to-2024 decline trajectory is documented in NAR research and widely cited in financial media.

## Limits
- Existing home sales data is for the overall market; individual company performance depends on geographic concentration, product mix, and margin structure as well.
