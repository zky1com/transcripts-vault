# NY Fed data shows auto loan delinquency transition rates remained elevated throughout 2024 as consumers faced competing payment obligations

## Core Evidence
The NY Fed Household Debt and Credit Reports for 2024 document that auto loan delinquency transition rates (the share of balances moving into delinquency) remained at elevated levels throughout the year, with approximately 7.9% of auto loan balances transitioning into delinquency in Q1 2024. The reports note that stress was particularly pronounced among younger borrowers. Total auto loan balances were approximately $1.63 trillion in Q2 2024. The data corroborates that auto-loan consumer stress is a genuine, measurable phenomenon — consistent with the premise that lenders face real credit pressure even if modification and extension practices delay formal loss recognition.

## External Source

## Verification Notes
- NY Fed 2024 Household Debt and Credit Reports confirmed on 2026-02-26; auto loan data, delinquency transition rates, and borrower demographic stress are documented in the quarterly reports.

## Limits
- The NY Fed data covers delinquency transitions; it does not directly measure the rate of loan modifications or extension behavior, which would require OCC servicer data or individual lender disclosures.
