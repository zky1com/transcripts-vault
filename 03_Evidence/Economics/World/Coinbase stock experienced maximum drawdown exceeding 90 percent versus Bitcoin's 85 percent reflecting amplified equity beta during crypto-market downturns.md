# Coinbase stock experienced maximum drawdown exceeding 90 percent versus Bitcoin's 85 percent reflecting amplified equity beta during crypto-market downturns

## Core Evidence
Coinbase (COIN) stock has experienced a maximum drawdown of approximately -90.9% since its IPO, exceeding Bitcoin's maximum drawdown of approximately -85.3%. During the 2022 crypto market downturn, US equities fell approximately 22% while Bitcoin declined 76%, and Coinbase equity fell substantially more than Bitcoin itself — demonstrating that crypto-linked equities carry amplified exposure (stacked business sensitivity plus asset beta) rather than providing a neutral or hedged proxy for crypto risk.

- Drawdown comparison data confirmed on 2026-02-26 from portfolio analytics and financial reporting sources; the amplification dynamic is consistently documented across multiple data providers and Coinbase's own earnings commentary.

## Limits
- Historical drawdowns are measured from specific peaks; the comparison is affected by starting period selection. Coinbase is the most prominent crypto-linked equity but other names (MicroStrategy, miners) show similar or more extreme amplification patterns.
