# AIG received 182 billion in US government support in 2008 from Fed and TARP after governance failures

## Core Evidence
American International Group (AIG) received approximately $182 billion in total US government financial support during the 2008 financial crisis: approximately $112 billion committed by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York through extraordinary credit facilities and asset purchase vehicles (Maiden Lane II and III), plus approximately $70 billion committed through TARP. The Federal Reserve and Treasury ultimately realized a combined positive return of $22.7 billion on this investment. Prior to the crisis, AIG had already exhibited significant governance failures: in 2006, AIG paid $1.64 billion to settle Securities and Exchange Commission and Department of Justice charges related to accounting fraud and financial statement manipulation under then-CEO Hank Greenberg. ## Limits
- The $182B represents total commitments, not all of which was drawn simultaneously; AIG repaid the government in full with positive returns. The 2006 accounting fraud settlement predated the credit-default-swap losses that drove the 2008 crisis, so the governance signal was partially distinct from the ultimate failure mechanism.
