# EIA data shows US data center electricity consumption rose from 58 TWh in 2014 to 183 TWh in 2024 as AI-driven load accelerated

## Core Evidence
EIA data shows US data center electricity consumption rose from 58 TWh in 2014 to 176 TWh in 2023 and approximately 183 TWh in 2024. Data centers consumed about 4.4% of total US electricity in 2023 and are projected to consume 6.7–12% by 2028. Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta collectively spent over $200 billion on capital expenditures in 2024 — a 62% year-over-year increase from 2023. This data directly supports the claim that AI buildout has moved well beyond a software narrative into measurable physical infrastructure demand, particularly in power capacity.

## Limits
- Data center electricity consumption is not solely AI; it includes non-AI cloud computing workloads. However, AI inference and training are the primary drivers of the recent acceleration in growth rate.
