# CMS transitioned Medicare Advantage risk adjustment to HCC Model V28 in 2024 reducing allowable diagnosis codes from 9797 to 7770 and projecting lower risk scores

## Core Evidence
CMS introduced HCC Model V28 for Medicare Advantage risk adjustment with a phased transition: 67% V28 for 2024 dates of service, 100% V28 for 2025. The model reduced the number of allowable ICD-10-CM codes from 9,797 to 7,770 — removing approximately 2,294 diagnosis codes from the risk scoring framework. CMS projected that CY2024 MA risk scores would decrease by -3.12%, translating to approximately $11 billion in net savings to the Medicare Trust Fund. This directly supports the claim that reimbursement coding rule tightening (removing diagnosis code pathways) can structurally reduce revenue capture for Medicare Advantage plans that had relied on expansive coding practices.

## Limits
- The -3.12% risk score reduction is CMS's projected aggregate impact; individual Medicare Advantage plan impacts vary by coding intensity and patient population mix.
