# US District Court ruled in August 2024 that Google unlawfully monopolizes search through default distribution agreements including Apple paying Google 18 billion dollars in 2021

## Core Evidence
On August 5, 2024, US District Judge Amit Mehta ruled that Google unlawfully monopolizes markets for general search services and search text advertising through exclusive default distribution contracts with browser developers, device manufacturers, and wireless carriers. Google paid $26.3 billion in 2021 to secure default search placement, with Apple receiving $18 billion of that. The court found no meaningful competitive alternative to these agreements, as Apple testified no price Microsoft could offer would cause it to switch defaults to Bing. The remedies phase (finalized December 2025) restricted Google from exclusive default deals and imposed 12-month limits on default agreements. This directly supports the claim that default-distribution agreements can become a material legal-risk channel under antitrust scrutiny.
