# One Big Beautiful Bill Act July 2025 restores 100 percent bonus depreciation reversing TCJA phase-down

## Core Evidence
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA), enacted on July 4, 2025, permanently restores 100% first-year bonus depreciation (additional first-year depreciation deduction) for most qualifying business property acquired after January 19, 2025. Under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017, 100% bonus depreciation was available through 2022 and then phased down: 80% in 2023, 60% in 2024, and 40% in 2025, before the OBBBA reversed the phase-down. The OBBBA also raises the Section 179 annual expense deduction from $1 million to $2.5 million (with a phaseout threshold of $4 million), and introduces new 100% full expensing for "qualified production property" (manufacturing facilities constructed between January 2025 and January 2029). These changes directly lower the effective project costs for capital investment, implementing the policy mechanism the claim identifies as driving delayed but accelerating corporate adoption.

## Limits
- The 100% deduction is permanent for most property but the new "qualified production property" full expensing is temporary (construction must begin before 2029 and be placed in service before 2031). The effective project cost reduction applies to businesses with taxable income to absorb the deduction.
