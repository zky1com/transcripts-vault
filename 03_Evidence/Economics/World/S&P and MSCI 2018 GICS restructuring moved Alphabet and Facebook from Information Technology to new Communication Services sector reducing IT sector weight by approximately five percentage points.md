# S&P and MSCI 2018 GICS restructuring moved Alphabet and Facebook from Information Technology to new Communication Services sector reducing IT sector weight by approximately five percentage points

## Core Evidence
On September 28, 2018, S&P Dow Jones Indices and MSCI implemented a major restructuring of the Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS). The old Telecom Services sector was renamed Communication Services and expanded to absorb Alphabet (Google) and Facebook (now Meta) from Information Technology, as well as Netflix and 13 additional media/entertainment companies from Consumer Discretionary. The reclassification caused the Information Technology sector's weight in the S&P 500 to fall from approximately 26% to 21%, and Consumer Discretionary to fall from approximately 13% to 10%, while Communication Services grew from approximately 2% to approximately 12% — becoming the fourth-largest S&P 500 sector. The concentration of the largest companies in the index was not dispersed; their labels simply changed.

## Verification Notes
- S&P/MSCI official GICS documentation and Wikipedia confirmed on 2026-02-26; the sector weight changes and company movements are documented in multiple industry sources and described as the largest single GICS restructuring since the classification system's creation in 1999.

## Limits
- Sector weights at the time of reclassification varied by index and weighting methodology; figures cited reflect approximate S&P 500 market-cap weights. Concentration dynamics shifted somewhat further after 2018 as these companies continued to grow.
