# FDIC Quarterly Banking Profile shows industry net interest margin rising to 3.39 percent in Q4 2025 while credit quality metrics remained broadly stable

## Core Evidence
The FDIC Quarterly Banking Profile for Q4 2025 reports that the banking industry's net interest margin (NIM) rose to 3.39 percent — the highest level since 2019 — while the number of problem banks decreased to 57 (1.3% of total banks), within the historical non-crisis range of 1–2%. Full-year 2025 banking net income was $295.6 billion, up 10.2% from 2024. This data supports the claim that when NIMs improve and broad credit quality remains stable, the macro backdrop typically reflects genuine intermediation resilience rather than speculative stress.

## Limits
- Aggregate industry data can obscure pockets of localized stress; certain consumer credit categories (auto, credit cards) showed elevated delinquency rates even as the headline NIM and earnings metrics were improving.
