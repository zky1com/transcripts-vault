# Behavioral-finance evidence supports that salient purchase-and-sale prices can bias perceived housing investment outcomes when ongoing ownership costs are ignored

## Core Evidence
Behavioral-finance findings on salience show decision makers can overweight attention-grabbing values and underweight less salient costs. In housing, focusing on buy/sell prices while ignoring taxes, maintenance, and financing costs can overstate perceived investment returns.
