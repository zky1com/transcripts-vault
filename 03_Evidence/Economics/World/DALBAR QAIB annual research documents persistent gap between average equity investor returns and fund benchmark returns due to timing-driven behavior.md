# DALBAR QAIB annual research documents persistent gap between average equity investor returns and fund benchmark returns due to timing-driven behavior

## Core Evidence
DALBAR's annual Quantitative Analysis of Investor Behavior (QAIB), published since 1994, consistently documents that the average equity fund investor earns substantially less than the benchmark index — primarily due to behavioral errors including poor timing, panic selling, and performance chasing. In 2024, the average equity investor earned 16.54% versus the S&P 500's 25.05%, an 848 basis point gap. The report concludes that investment results are more dependent on investor behavior than on fund performance.

## Limits
- DALBAR's methodology has been critiqued for using average cash flow timing rather than dollar-weighted returns per investor; the magnitude of the gap may be overstated. The directional finding (behavioral gap exists) is widely corroborated by Morningstar "Mind the Gap" research as well.
