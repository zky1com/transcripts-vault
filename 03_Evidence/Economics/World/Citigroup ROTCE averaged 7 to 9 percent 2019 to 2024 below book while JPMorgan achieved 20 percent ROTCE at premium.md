# Citigroup ROTCE averaged 7 to 9 percent 2019 to 2024 below book while JPMorgan achieved 20 percent ROTCE at premium

## Core Evidence
Citigroup's return on tangible common equity (ROTCE) averaged approximately 7-9% from 2019 through 2024, consistently trailing JPMorgan Chase's approximately 18-22% ROTCE over the same period. This profitability divergence was reflected in sustained valuation discounts: Citigroup traded at approximately 0.7-1.0x tangible book value â€” often at or below book â€” while JPMorgan traded at approximately 2.0-2.5x tangible book value. The gap was not explained by cyclical factors alone; it reflected structural differences in business mix, efficiency ratios, and capital utilization. In 2023, Citigroup CEO Jane Fraser launched a comprehensive multi-year "transformation" program targeting ROTCE improvement toward 11-12%, explicitly acknowledging the structural profitability shortfall as the primary driver of the persistent valuation discount. This case provides the primary empirical anchor for the claim's framework: banks with structurally weak ROTCE face valuation traps that quarterly earnings beats cannot escape without durable profitability regime change.

## Limits
- The ROTCE differential reflects business mix differences as well as execution; Citigroup's transformation program aims to improve ROTCE to 11-12% by 2026, narrowing but not closing the gap. Both banks' ROTCE figures are sensitive to regulatory capital requirements and interest rate levels.
