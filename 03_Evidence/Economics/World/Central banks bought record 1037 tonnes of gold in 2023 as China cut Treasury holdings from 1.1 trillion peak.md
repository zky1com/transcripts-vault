# Central banks bought record 1037 tonnes of gold in 2023 as China cut Treasury holdings from 1.1 trillion peak

## Core Evidence
World Gold Council data shows global central banks purchased approximately 1,037 tonnes of gold in 2023 â€” the second consecutive record year and the highest annual volume since at least the 1950s. China's People's Bank of China added approximately 225 tonnes in 2023, raising gold's share of Chinese official reserves to approximately 4.3%. Separately, China's holdings of US Treasury securities declined from approximately $1.07 trillion (2021 peak) to approximately $800 billion by late 2024, a ~$270 billion reduction documented in US Treasury TIC (Treasury International Capital) data. Russia had already completed a near-total exit from USD reserves by 2022, accumulating approximately 2,332 tonnes of gold (~25% of total reserves). The World Gold Council's Central Bank Gold Reserve Survey identified "geopolitical risks," "dollar weaponization concerns," and "desire for assets free from sanctions risk" as the top motives cited by central bank reserve managers for increasing gold allocations. This systematic, policy-driven de-dollarization of reserve portfolios â€” replacing USD-denominated assets with gold â€” provides direct empirical evidence for the politically-motivated allocation shift the claim identifies.

## Limits
- Central bank gold buying motives include both geopolitical diversification and macroeconomic factors (inflation hedging, real rate concerns); attribution to solely political motivation is based on survey self-reporting. China's US Treasury reduction also reflects active portfolio rebalancing and is not necessarily a hostile political act. Gold's share of global reserves remains below 15% on average, limiting the dollar replacement narrative.
