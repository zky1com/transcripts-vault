# US Department of Defense FY2025 budget totaled 852 billion dollars a 3.3 percent increase over FY2024 reflecting sustained defense spending growth and aerospace-defense sector recovery

## Core Evidence
The FY2025 US Department of Defense appropriations bill provided $852.2 billion in total defense funding, a $27.2 billion (3.3%) increase over FY2024 enacted levels. The full-year continuing resolution (P.L. 119-4) provided $831.3 billion in discretionary base budget authority for DoD programs. Additional mandatory defense funding of $156.2 billion was appropriated for shipbuilding, air and missile defenses, munitions, and supply chains. ## Limits
- The defense budget figure represents total government defense appropriations; not all flows directly to industrial/aerospace suppliers. Commercial aerospace recovery is separately driven by post-COVID air travel demand rather than US defense spending. The claim's "aerospace/defense recovery" encompasses both defense procurement and commercial aerospace.
