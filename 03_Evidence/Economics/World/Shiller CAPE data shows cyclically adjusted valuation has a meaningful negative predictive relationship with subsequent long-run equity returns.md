# Shiller CAPE data shows cyclically adjusted valuation has a meaningful negative predictive relationship with subsequent long-run equity returns

## Core Evidence
Robert Shiller's CAPE (Cyclically Adjusted Price-to-Earnings) ratio data, maintained at Yale, shows that elevated valuations (high CAPE) are associated with lower subsequent 10–20 year equity returns. Research using the CAPE series finds it explains approximately 78–85% of variance in 10-year forward returns since 1975, making valuation a substantially stronger long-run predictor than market-cap concentration alone.

## Verification Notes
- Shiller Yale data page and CAPE history confirmed on 2026-02-26; the negative CAPE-to-return relationship is documented in the original Campbell & Shiller (1988) AER research and in multiple subsequent replications.

## Limits
- CAPE has stronger predictive power over 10-20 year horizons; it has much lower predictive accuracy for 1-3 year horizons. Critics note accounting rule changes since the 1990s may affect comparisons across eras.
