# US data centers consumed 200 TWh in 2023 with AI workloads projected to double demand by 2026

## Core Evidence
US data centers consumed approximately 176-200 terawatt-hours (TWh) of electricity in 2023, representing approximately 4% of total US electricity generation according to Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's Data Center Energy Usage Report. AI-intensive workloads are projected to drive data center electricity demand to 6-9% of total US consumption by 2026-2030 (LBNL, Goldman Sachs, Grid Strategies LLC scenarios). PJM Interconnection — the grid operator for the Virginia data center corridor, the highest-density AI infrastructure market globally — reported interconnection queue backlogs of 5-7 years for new utility connections as of 2024. In response, hyperscalers and data center operators (Amazon Web Services, Google, Microsoft, CoreWeave) increasingly contract for on-site natural gas turbines, diesel generation, and nuclear power purchase agreements to secure capacity independent of the transmission grid. These on-site generation investments represent substantial additional capex above GPU and real estate costs typically cited in simple EBITDA analyses, directly supporting the claim's argument that AI infrastructure operators carry complex cost structures requiring analysis beyond peer EBITDA multiple comparisons.

## Limits
- Electricity consumption projections vary significantly across methodologies and AI efficiency assumptions. The grid interconnection backlog reflects both data center and renewable energy projects. Operators in markets with available grid capacity face less severe constraints than the Northern Virginia cluster.
