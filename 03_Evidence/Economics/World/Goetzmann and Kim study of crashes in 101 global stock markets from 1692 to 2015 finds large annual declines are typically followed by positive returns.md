# Goetzmann and Kim study of crashes in 101 global stock markets from 1692 to 2015 finds large annual declines are typically followed by positive returns

## Core Evidence
Goetzmann and Kim (2018) study 101 global stock markets from 1692 to 2015, identifying 1,032 events where a market declined more than 50% over a 12-month period. Their key finding is that extremely large annual stock market declines are typically followed by positive returns — a pattern that holds across markets and time periods and does not appear driven by survivorship bias or macroeconomic shocks.

## Verification Notes
- NBER working paper (no. 23830) and SSRN listing confirmed on 2026-02-26; published in European Financial Management vol. 24, issue 2, 2018.

## Limits
- The finding applies specifically to crashes exceeding 50% annual decline; the pattern does not hold for smaller declines.
