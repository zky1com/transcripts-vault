# Federal Reserve Survey 2022 top 10 percent own 93 percent of stocks and homeowner renter net worth gap expanded 70 percent

## Core Evidence
The Federal Reserve's Distributional Financial Accounts (DFA) show that the top 10% of US households by wealth own approximately 93% of all directly held corporate equities and mutual fund shares as of Q4 2022. The Federal Reserve's triennial Survey of Consumer Finances (SCF) 2022 documented a 37% increase in median family net worth between 2019 and 2022, but with extreme distributional divergence: median homeowner net worth reached approximately $396,200 compared to approximately $10,400 for median renters â€” a 38:1 ratio. During 2020-2022, the S&P 500 rose approximately 100% from the March 2020 trough to the January 2022 peak, and the S&P/Case-Shiller national home price index rose approximately 43% nationally between January 2020 and June 2022. This asset price surge accrued almost entirely to the asset-owning segment of the population, creating the K-shaped wealth bifurcation Bianco describes: whether an individual is an "asset owner" â€” primarily determined by stock and real estate ownership â€” is now more economically decisive than income level, and short-term market rallies reinforce rather than resolve that structural divide.

## Limits
- The 93% stock ownership concentration figure reflects direct holdings; when including employer retirement plans (401k), the distribution is somewhat less concentrated. The homeowner/renter net worth gap includes primary residence equity, which is illiquid and non-income-generating in normal conditions. The wealth gains of 2020-2022 were partially reversed by 2022 asset price corrections.
