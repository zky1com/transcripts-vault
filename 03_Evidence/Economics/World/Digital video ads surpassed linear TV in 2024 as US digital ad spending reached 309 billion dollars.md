# Digital video ads surpassed linear TV in 2024 as US digital ad spending reached 309 billion dollars

## Core Evidence
In 2024, digital video advertising (including Connected TV and streaming) surpassed linear TV advertising for the first time in the US, representing a structural inflection in the advertising market. US digital advertising spending reached $309.3 billion in 2024, a 15.1% year-over-year increase, while linear TV ad spend was $60.56 billion and is projected to decline to $56.83 billion by 2027 per eMarketer forecasts. Connected TV alone is expected to reach $33.35 billion in 2025, with continued double-digit annual growth. The structural advantage of targeted digital and CTV formats — superior measurement, audience targeting, and ROI attribution — is driving permanent ad-dollar migration away from linear broadcast models. This is the demand-side shift that undermines the earnings case for legacy linear media assets within conglomerate structures, directly supporting the claim that industrial logic weaknesses in media conglomerates compound rather than offset each other.

## Limits
- "Linear TV" and "digital video" category definitions vary by source; live sports rights remain concentrated on linear for now. The aggregate ad revenue shift is clear, but specific legacy media companies retain content advantages that delay full earnings pressure.
