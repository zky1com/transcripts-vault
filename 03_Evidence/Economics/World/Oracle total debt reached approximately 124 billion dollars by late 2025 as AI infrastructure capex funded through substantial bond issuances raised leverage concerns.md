# Oracle total debt reached approximately 124 billion dollars by late 2025 as AI infrastructure capex funded through substantial bond issuances raised leverage concerns

## Core Evidence
Oracle's total debt reached approximately $124 billion by late 2025, a 39% increase from the prior year, with a debt-to-EBITDA ratio above 4x. Oracle raised $18 billion in a single bond transaction in September 2025 and issued an additional $25 billion in February 2026. FY2026 capex guidance was raised to $50 billion, primarily for AI data centers. The debt-to-equity ratio expanded sharply, prompting credit agency scrutiny and secondary-market bond pricing inconsistent with the official investment-grade ratings. This supports the claim that large AI capex programs funded with incremental debt can create meaningful valuation overhang even when the strategic direction remains thematically attractive.
