# S&P 500 historical annual returns data shows approximately one-quarter of calendar years since 1927 produced negative returns

## Core Evidence
S&P 500 historical annual return data shows that approximately 25–27% of calendar years since 1927 produced negative returns. From 1928 to 2021, the S&P 500 had 25 negative years out of roughly 94, consistent with the roughly one-in-four frequency cited in long-run equity crash analyses.

## External Source
- https://www.macrotrends.net/2526/sp-500-historical-annual-returns

## Verification Notes
- MacroTrends dataset manually checked on 2026-02-26; annual return series supports ~25% negative-year frequency.

## Limits
- The exact percentage varies depending on index used (S&P 500 vs. broader CRSP total market), time period selected, and whether total return or price return is measured.
