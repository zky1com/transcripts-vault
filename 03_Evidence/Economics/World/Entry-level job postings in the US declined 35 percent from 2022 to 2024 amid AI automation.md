# Entry-level job postings in the US declined 35 percent from 2022 to 2024 amid AI automation

## Core Evidence
LinkedIn's 2024 Workforce Intelligence data documented that approximately 35% of all entry-level job postings required 3 or more years of work experience, reflecting the phenomenon of "experience inflation" as AI tools have shifted employer preferences toward experienced workers who can supervise automated systems. Indeed's hiring platform data showed entry-level professional services and finance job postings declined approximately 35% between 2022 and 2024, concentrated in roles involving content creation, data analysis, research, and report generation — tasks where generative AI has demonstrated high substitutability. McKinsey Global Institute research (2024) projected that generative AI could automate 60-70% of tasks currently performed by entry-level knowledge workers. These converging trends document the mechanism the claim identifies: if companies eliminate entry-level positions to capture AI efficiency gains, the cohort of professionals who would become senior leaders in 5-10 years through organizational experience accumulation will be smaller, creating a structural future leadership pipeline shortage that static efficiency models do not capture.

## Limits
- The "experience inflation" in job postings reflects multiple causes including post-COVID labor market tightening and not solely AI automation; precise attribution to AI is difficult to isolate. LinkedIn and Indeed data covers digital job postings and may not represent all hiring channels. McKinsey's 60-70% automation estimate is a modeling projection subject to significant uncertainty.
