# CFPB May 2024 interpretive rule classified buy now pay later products as credit cards under Regulation Z requiring BNPL lenders to provide credit card consumer protections

## Core Evidence
On May 22, 2024, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) issued an interpretive rule stating that BNPL lenders issuing digital user accounts through which consumers access installment credit products are "card issuers" under the Truth in Lending Act (TILA) and Regulation Z. This classification required BNPL providers to offer the same core consumer protections as traditional credit card issuers: investigating billing disputes, providing refunds for returned goods, and issuing periodic billing statements. The CFPB's legal analysis concluded that the functional characteristics of BNPL credit â€” recurring purchase access, digital account infrastructure, and revolving credit-like mechanics â€” constitute bank-like credit intermediation subject to the same framework as credit cards. The rule was subsequently withdrawn in April 2025 by new CFPB leadership as part of a broader agency rollback, but the substantive legal analysis remains relevant to ongoing state-level regulatory activity.

## Limits
- The interpretive rule was withdrawn in April 2025 before the September 2025 claim date, reducing its immediate regulatory impact at the federal level. State regulators and private litigants may still apply the functional analysis. The withdrawal reflects political rather than legal disagreement with the CFPB's interpretation.
