# FCIC Final Report documents sharp deterioration in subprime mortgage underwriting standards between 2004 and 2007 with widespread no-doc and low-doc lending

## Core Evidence
The Financial Crisis Inquiry Commission (FCIC) Final Report (2011) documents that subprime mortgage underwriting standards deteriorated significantly between 2004 and 2007. Subprime originations reached approximately $600 billion in 2006, representing 23.5% of all mortgage originations. The report describes widespread no-doc (stated income) lending, minimal income verification, and volume-driven origination incentives that prioritized fee generation over loan quality — consistent with Eisman's description of a visible underwriting breakdown identifiable before losses fully surfaced.

## Limits
- The FCIC report is a government-commissioned post-mortem written in 2011; the data points on no-doc prevalence and origination volume reflect the Commission's synthesis of evidence rather than a single primary data series.
