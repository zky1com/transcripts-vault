# BVP Cloud Index fell 67 percent from 2021 peak as SaaS multiples compressed from 20x to 5-6x NTM

## Core Evidence
The Bessemer Venture Partners Nasdaq Emerging Cloud Index (EMCLOUD) â€” a benchmark index of publicly traded cloud software companies â€” declined approximately 67% from its November 2021 peak to its June 2023 trough. Median enterprise value-to-next-twelve-months revenue (EV/NTM revenue) multiples for public SaaS companies compressed from approximately 20-30x in late 2021 to approximately 5-6x by mid-2023, according to Meritech Capital's SaaS valuation benchmarks. The compression reflected both interest-rate-driven duration punishment on long-cash-flow assets and growing market recognition that AI coding tools (GitHub Copilot, Cursor) could significantly lower the cost of building software alternatives. GitHub's 2023 research quantified developer productivity gains of 40-55% using AI code completion tools, implying meaningful reduction in software development cost barriers. Together, these dynamics illustrate the claim's "simultaneously transformative and valuation-disruptive" framework: AI tools both accelerate innovation (transformative) and challenge incumbent SaaS pricing power by lowering entry barriers for competitors (disruptive to existing equity multiples).

## Limits
- The 67% EMCLOUD decline reflected primarily interest rate normalization (Fed funds rose from 0.25% to 5.25-5.5% over 2022-2023) rather than AI competitive threats alone; AI disruption risk is a forward-looking concern rather than a documented historical cause of 2022-2023 declines. The SaaS multiple compression reversed partially in 2024 as rate cut expectations emerged.
