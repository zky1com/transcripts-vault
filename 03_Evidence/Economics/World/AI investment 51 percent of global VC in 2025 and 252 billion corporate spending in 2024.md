# AI investment 51 percent of global VC in 2025 and 252 billion corporate spending in 2024

## Core Evidence
According to Stanford HAI's AI Index Report 2025, AI captured 51% of global venture capital deal value in 2025, up from 37% in 2024, demonstrating an accelerating concentration of private investment flows into AI-linked companies. Corporate AI investment reached $252.3 billion in 2024, a 44.5% year-over-year increase. The four major hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) committed approximately $381 billion in combined 2025 capital expenditure, predominantly AI infrastructure. In contrast, broader private fixed investment growth outside AI-linked categories was flat to slightly negative in real terms through 2025, and US manufacturing PMI remained below 50 (indicating contraction) for extended periods. This documented bifurcation â€” with AI-linked capex accelerating sharply while non-AI investment stagnated â€” provides direct empirical support for the claim that the economy can appear bifurcated based on whether measurement captures AI-concentrated or broader economic activity.

## Limits
- The 51% VC deal-value share reflects concentration in a high-growth sector, which is partially normal for venture during technology cycles; whether "ex-AI" weakness constitutes economic stagnation vs. healthy rebalancing is debated. PMI readings are manufacturing-specific and may understate services-sector health. The bifurcation framing is descriptively accurate but does not itself indicate whether the AI investment boom is sustainable or whether broader activity will recover.
