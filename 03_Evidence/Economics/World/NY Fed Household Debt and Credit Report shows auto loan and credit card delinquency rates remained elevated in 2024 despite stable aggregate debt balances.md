# NY Fed Household Debt and Credit Report shows auto loan and credit card delinquency rates remained elevated in 2024 despite stable aggregate debt balances

## Core Evidence
The NY Fed Household Debt and Credit Report for 2024 shows total household debt rose steadily throughout the year, reaching $18.04 trillion by Q4 2024. However, delinquency transition rates for auto loans and credit cards remained persistently elevated: approximately 7.9% of auto balances and 8.9% of credit card balances transitioned into delinquency in Q1 2024, with elevated rates maintained through year-end. This pattern — stable aggregate balances alongside rising delinquency transition rates — supports the claim that headline credit data can appear broadly stable while consumer balance sheet stress builds underneath.

## Verification Notes
- NY Fed Q1–Q4 2024 Household Debt and Credit Reports confirmed on 2026-02-26; delinquency transition rate data is from NY Fed Consumer Credit Panel/Equifax.

## Limits
- Aggregate delinquency rates are higher than during the 2015-2019 period but below 2008-2010 crisis levels; the stress is concentrated in auto and revolving credit rather than mortgage credit.
