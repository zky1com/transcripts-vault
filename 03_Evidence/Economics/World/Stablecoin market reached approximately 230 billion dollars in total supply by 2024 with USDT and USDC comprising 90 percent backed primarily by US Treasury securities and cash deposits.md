# Stablecoin market reached approximately 230 billion dollars in total supply by 2024 with USDT and USDC comprising 90 percent backed primarily by US Treasury securities and cash deposits

## Core Evidence
Total stablecoin market capitalization reached approximately $230-309 billion by 2024-2025. Tether (USDT) at approximately $150 billion and Circle (USDC) at approximately $60 billion together account for approximately 90% of total stablecoin supply. Reserve structures differ materially: USDC is backed entirely by US Treasury securities ($42.3B) and cash deposits ($12.2B). USDT's backing is approximately 81.5% repo and US Treasuries, with approximately 18% in other assets including corporate bonds, secured loans, and other crypto assets. The dependence on short-term Treasury reserves means stablecoin issuers are structurally linked to US short-rate policy and Treasury market functioning — the "reserve structure" mechanic the claim identifies as essential to proper stablecoin analysis. Trading volume of USDT and USDC reached $23 trillion in 2024.

## Limits
- Reserve composition changes month-to-month; USDT has faced historical scrutiny over reserve transparency and the 18% non-Treasury component adds credit/liquidity risk not present in USDC. Stablecoin market cap is dominated by crypto-native use cases rather than traditional consumer payments.
