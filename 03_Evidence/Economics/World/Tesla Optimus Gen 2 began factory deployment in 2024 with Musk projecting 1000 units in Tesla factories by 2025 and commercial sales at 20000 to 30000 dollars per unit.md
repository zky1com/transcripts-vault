# Tesla Optimus Gen 2 began factory deployment in 2024 with Musk projecting 1000 units in Tesla factories by 2025 and commercial sales at 20000 to 30000 dollars per unit

## Core Evidence
Tesla demonstrated Optimus Gen 2 in October 2023, showing the robot walking autonomously at 0.9 m/s, performing dexterous hand tasks (sorting objects, handling eggs without breaking them), and operating with on-board neural network processing derived from Tesla's Full Self-Driving stack — the same LLM-adjacent reasoning layer Calacanis identifies as the critical cognitive enabler that prior generations of robots lacked. By mid-2024, Tesla had begun deploying Optimus robots in its Fremont and other factories for unsupervised real-world manufacturing assistance tasks. Elon Musk publicly projected more than 1,000 Optimus units operating in Tesla facilities by end of 2025, with commercial sales at an estimated $20,000–$30,000 per unit. Goldman Sachs projected the humanoid robot market to reach $38 billion by 2035; Morgan Stanley projected $100B+ at full production scale. The broader competitive landscape includes Figure 01/02 (backed by Microsoft and OpenAI), Boston Dynamics Electric Atlas, Agility Robotics Digit (deployed in Amazon warehouses), and Unitree H1/G1 — all launched post-2022, confirming the market's acceleration driven by LLM integration that Calacanis's claim describes.

## Limits
- Commercial sales timeline and pricing remain projections subject to engineering and regulatory uncertainty. The $38B Goldman Sachs figure is a baseline scenario with wide variance. The claim that Optimus will be "the most transformative product in human history" is a qualitative prediction that the world evidence supports structurally but cannot confirm — scale projections require humanoid robots to achieve reliable autonomous operation in unstructured environments, which remains an engineering challenge as of early 2026.
