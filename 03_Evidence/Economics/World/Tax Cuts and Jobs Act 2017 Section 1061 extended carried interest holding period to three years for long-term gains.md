# Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017 Section 1061 extended carried interest holding period to three years for long-term gains

## Core Evidence
The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017 introduced IRC Section 1061, which extended the holding period required for fund managers to qualify their carried interest for long-term capital gains tax treatment from 1 year to 3 years. Under the standard private equity fee structure (typically "2 and 20" — a 2% management fee plus 20% carried interest on profits above a hurdle rate), fund managers receive approximately 20% of fund profits with limited direct capital at risk. This 20% carried interest — when held for the required 3-year period — is taxed at the preferential 20% long-term capital gains rate rather than ordinary income rates of up to 37%. Limited partners (LPs), by contrast, receive net-of-carry returns that are mechanically reduced by the manager's share before allocation. The result is an asymmetric structure where managers capture a fixed percentage of gross upside while investors absorb 100% of the downside and all costs.

## Limits
- The TCJA Section 1061 change was the most recent legislative adjustment; proposals to tax carried interest as ordinary income have repeatedly failed. Fee structures vary across fund types (growth equity, VC, credit); some funds charge less than 20% carry or higher hurdle rates, moderating the asymmetry.
