## Summary

Naval argues that wealth-building requires valuing your time far above what the market currently pays you. Setting an absurdly high internal hourly rate forces you to stop sweating small tasks, reject low-value friction, and organize life around compounding rather than busyness or penny-pinching.

## Key Insights

- No one will reliably value your time more than you do yourself.
- Wealth creation and obsessive optimization of tiny expenses pull in opposite directions.
- A high internal hourly rate helps you filter errands, hassles, and obligations that consume scarce cognitive energy.
- Outsourcing is rational even before you are rich if it protects the hours that matter most.
- Aspirational self-pricing works partly as identity training: you start acting like your future leverage already matters.

## Key Quotes

> Set an aspirational hourly rate and stick to it.

> No one is going to value you more than you value yourself.

> If you can outsource something for less than your hourly rate, outsource it.
