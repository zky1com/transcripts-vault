## Summary

Naval introduces Brett Hall through the Royal Society motto `nullius in verba`, using it as a statement of epistemic independence. The point is that difficult ideas from Deutsch and Popper are worth engaging only if you test them against your own understanding rather than accepting them on authority.

## Key Insights

- Deep books are often hard because they are written for peers in a specialized conversation, not for broad accessibility.
- Real understanding requires verification or refutation for yourself rather than passive admiration.
- The motto `nullius in verba` captures the discipline of taking no authority at face value.
- Naval's attraction to Deutsch is tied to the possibility of understanding reality as a unified project.
- Brett Hall is introduced as a guide who helped Naval clarify the theory-of-knowledge aspects of Deutsch's work.

## Key Quotes

> Nullius in verba.

> Take no one's word for it.

> Figure it out yourself. That's the only way you know anything.
