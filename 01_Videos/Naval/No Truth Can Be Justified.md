## Summary

Naval rejects the old "justified true belief" model of knowledge and replaces it with a Popperian one. We never prove a theory finally true; we keep conjectures that survive criticism and refutation better than their rivals, which is what allows knowledge to stay corrigible and progress to continue.

## Key Insights

- There is no general method for showing that a piece of knowledge is conclusively true.
- Knowledge grows by error correction, not by accumulating certainty.
- A conjecture is not arbitrary if it has survived repeated serious attempts to refute it.
- Bayesianism is criticized here as a modern continuation of the justification mindset.
- A fallibilist view of knowledge makes endless future improvement possible.

## Key Quotes

> No truth can be justified.

> All we have are guesses about reality.

> When people are unable to show that it's false, then we accept it as a piece of knowledge.
