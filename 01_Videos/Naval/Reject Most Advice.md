## Summary

Naval argues that advice is useful mainly as raw material, not as a template to copy literally. Since other people's success often depends on context you cannot reproduce, the real skill is absorbing enough viewpoints to build your own internal model and discard whatever does not fit your situation.

## Key Insights

- People who succeeded quickly often overfit their advice to the exact sequence that happened to work for them.
- Listening broadly is useful, but unfiltered obedience to advice is destructive.
- Good founders keep their own model of reality and are willing to throw away received wisdom.
- Maxims are best understood as memory hooks for experience you have already earned yourself.
- Without firsthand context, even sharp advice degrades into inspirational wallpaper.

## Key Quotes

> Reject most advice.

> They're just reading you their winning lottery ticket numbers.

> The best founders I know, they listen and read to everyone, but then they ignore everybody and they make up their own mind.
