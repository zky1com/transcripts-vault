## Summary

Naval generalizes Popperian knowledge creation beyond science into innovation, evolution, and technology. Progress comes from generating bold explanatory guesses, subjecting them to reality, and removing the ones that fail rather than trying to infer the future mechanically from past regularities.

## Key Insights

- Complex systems improve through variation and selection rather than passive trend extrapolation.
- Induction is unreliable because past regularity can hide abrupt regime change.
- Creativity is required to produce explanations that go beyond the data already visible.
- The turkey example shows how confidence built on repeated observation can collapse suddenly.
- Everyday reasoning improves when people treat their best ideas as hypotheses to test rather than truths to defend.

## Key Quotes

> They make bold guesses and then they weed out the things that didn't work.

> The theories have to be guessed.

> Your best theories are going to be creative guesses, not simple extrapolations.
