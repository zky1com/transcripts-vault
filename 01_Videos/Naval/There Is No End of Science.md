## Summary

Naval presents scientific progress as open-ended rather than terminal. Even our best theories remain corrigible, and that is a strength, because the absence of final certainty is exactly what leaves room for discovery, improvement, and an indefinitely continuing frontier of knowledge.

## Key Insights

- A failed test does not automatically kill the only theory available if there is no workable alternative.
- Eddington's eclipse test mattered because it discriminated between Newton and Einstein, not because it crowned relativity as eternal truth.
- Scientific optimism comes from the fact that ideas can keep being improved rather than sealed shut.
- Fallibility is not a defect of science but the condition that makes progress possible.
- The phrase beginning of infinity captures an endless process of better explanations rather than a finished map of reality.

## Key Quotes

> There is no end of science.

> We never have the final word, and that's a good thing.

> We are at the beginning of infinity.
