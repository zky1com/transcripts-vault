## Summary

Naval argues that the most important modern leverage comes from products that replicate at near-zero cost, especially code and media. Unlike labor and capital, these forms can scale permissionlessly, letting one person's work reach millions without requiring a proportional increase in people or money.

## Key Insights

- Code and media let individuals multiply their effort without directly adding more labor.
- The newest fortunes are often built from permissionless leverage rather than permissioned leverage.
- Software already functions like an army of robots executing instructions at scale.
- Tech startups become explosive when they combine small elite teams with capital and product leverage.
- Learning to code matters because it is a way of commanding increasingly large systems of automation.

## Key Quotes

> Product and media are new leverage.

> All the new generation fortunes are made through code or media.

> Coding is such a great superpower because now you can speak the language of the robot armies.
