## Summary

Naval explains that leverage is what allows fortunes to scale beyond direct effort, but not all leverage is equal. Labor and capital are the older forms: labor is intuitive but messy and politically fraught, while capital scales far better but is harder to access without specific knowledge, reputation, and accountability.

## Key Insights

- Human beings intuitively overvalue labor leverage because it is the form most visible to our social instincts.
- Managing people is noisy, conflict-prone, and difficult to scale cleanly.
- Capital can scale with much less friction than labor once trust and competence are established.
- The ability to attract capital usually comes after proving judgment and credibility in a domain.
- Naval treats both labor and capital as older leverage classes that newer permissionless forms may outperform.

## Key Quotes

> Labor is the oldest form of leverage.

> I would argue that this is the worst form of leverage that you could possibly use.

> Capital is a powerful form of leverage.
