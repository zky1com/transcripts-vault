## Summary

Naval says that once leverage is available, judgment becomes the decisive variable. In a world where code, capital, media, and organizations can amplify a person's decisions enormously, the long-run winners are the people who can repeatedly make clear, reality-based calls under uncertainty and with accountability.

## Key Insights

- Leverage multiplies decisions, so errors and insights both scale much more than effort does.
- Demonstrated judgment attracts capital and trust because others want exposure to high-quality decision-making.
- Experience matters because pure intellect without skin in the game often produces overconfident abstraction.
- Judgment improves through many fast, honest iterations rather than passive time served.
- Emotional volatility is usually an enemy of judgment because it obscures what is actually happening.

## Key Quotes

> In an age of infinite leverage, judgment becomes the most important skill.

> Judgment is knowing the long-term consequences of your actions.

> Intellect without any experience is often worse than useless.
