## Summary

Naval argues that hard work matters, but only after you have chosen the right market and the right people. In startup terms, effort is the third leg of the stool behind what you work on and who you work with, and it works best in intense bursts driven by genuine inspiration rather than performative grind.

## Key Insights

- Hard work is necessary for ambitious wealth-building, but it cannot compensate for working on the wrong thing.
- Product-market-founder fit matters because a business works better when the founder is naturally suited to the problem.
- Talent density compounds, which is why choosing colleagues is nearly as important as choosing the market.
- Knowledge work runs best in sprint-and-rest cycles, not fake 120-hour marathons.
- Inspiration decays quickly, so action should follow quickly when clarity appears.

## Key Quotes

> Work as hard as you can.

> They're like three legs of a stool.

> Inspiration is perishable.
