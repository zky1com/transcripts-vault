## Summary

Naval says partner selection is one of the highest-leverage decisions in life and business, and he reduces the screen to three essentials: intelligence, energy, and integrity. Intelligence and drive matter, but integrity is the hard floor, because a smart, energetic person without ethics becomes a dangerous liability rather than an asset.

## Key Insights

- "Smart" is domain-specific, so you need intelligence aligned to the actual work being done.
- High energy is critical because smart but inert people do not create much.
- Integrity dominates because the combination of talent and dishonesty is eventually destructive.
- Character is best read from signals in behavior, especially when people think nobody important is watching.
- Intrinsic motivation cannot be permanently installed from the outside, so partners need to already care about the mission.

## Key Quotes

> Pick people to work with who have high intelligence, high energy, and high integrity.

> The signals are what they do, not what they say.

> Self-esteem is the reputation that you have with yourself.
