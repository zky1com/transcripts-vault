## Summary

This conversation focuses on how Ethereum governance changed as the project matured. Vitalik describes protocol politics as real politics under different incentives, reflects on the DAO fork as his least proud governance episode, and explains how Ethereum has become more bureaucratic, more decentralized in decision-making, and therefore more resilient but slower to move.

## Key Insights

- Protocol governance eventually develops the same tribalism, fear, and factionalism seen in ordinary politics.
- The DAO fork was justified by Vitalik as a pragmatic exception, but he remains critical of how the social process handled dissent.
- Ethereum's culture hardened over time from founder-led improvisation into a more veto-heavy, process-heavy system.
- Fear, more than greed, is presented here as the force that drives communities toward their worst political behavior.
- Vitalik's longer-term interest appears to be using his influence and capital to push infrastructure, privacy, security, and decentralized social systems beyond Ethereum itself.

## Key Quotes

> Protocol politics is less different from regular politics than you might think.

> People are at their morally worst not out of greed but out of fear.

> My influence at Ethereum keeps decreasing every six months.
