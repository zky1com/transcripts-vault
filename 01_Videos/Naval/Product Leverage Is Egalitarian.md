## Summary

Naval argues that product leverage, especially through code and media, is unusually egalitarian on the output side. Rich and poor users often consume the same software, entertainment, and mainstream manufactured goods, which means the best versions are frequently designed for large markets rather than reserved only for elites.

## Key Insights

- Labor-heavy services remain unequal because more money can buy more personalized human attention.
- Code and media outputs are often identical for billionaires and ordinary users.
- Large-scale products improve because broad usage supports the biggest budgets and best refinement loops.
- Signaling goods are different because exclusivity, not utility, is their core function.
- Product-driven wealth creation has more positive externalities than labor- or capital-heavy forms of leverage.

## Key Quotes

> Product leverage is egalitarian.

> Jeff Bezos doesn't get to watch better movies and TV than we do.

> The best products tend to be at the center, at the sweet spot, the middle class rather than being targeted at the upper class.
