## Summary

Naval uses the boiling point of water to show why prediction without explanation is fragile. Data trends alone cannot tell you when a system will change regime; only an explanatory model of what is happening underneath can produce reliable understanding and better forecasts.

## Key Insights

- Induction can fit observed trends while still missing the mechanism that will break the pattern.
- The boiling-water example shows how a clean extrapolation can fail exactly at the point where causal structure changes.
- Scientific explanation works by describing unseen causes, not just visible sequences.
- Prediction becomes trustworthy only after explanation clarifies what constraints govern the system.
- Creativity is indispensable because the right explanation has to be invented, not extracted mechanically from data.

## Key Quotes

> The correct answer can only come from creativity.

> Science is not about predicting where the trend starts and where the trend goes.

> Only once we have the explanation can we in fact make the prediction.
