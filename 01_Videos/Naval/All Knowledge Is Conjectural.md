## Summary

The transcript in this note appears corrupted, but the title points to Naval's recurring Popper-inspired view that knowledge is provisional rather than final. The core idea is that understanding advances through conjecture, criticism, and revision rather than absolute certainty.

## Key Insights

- Knowledge should be treated as tentative and revisable, not permanently settled.
- Progress comes from proposing explanations and then testing or criticizing them.
- Certainty is usually less useful than having better error-correcting methods.
- Intellectual humility is a strength because it keeps learning open-ended.

## Key Quotes

> All knowledge is conjectural.

> We improve knowledge by correcting errors, not by achieving final certainty.
