## Summary

Naval’s point is not that money buys happiness, but that a lack of material comfort creates unnecessary obstacles to peace. In modern life, basic wealth and health remove many of the common causes of suffering, which makes the inner work of becoming peaceful much easier than trying to renounce everything first.

## Key Insights

- Wealth is most useful for removing recurring stressors rather than producing permanent joy.
- Denying yourself what you strongly want can become its own form of bondage.
- Material success can be a faster route out of certain distractions than trying to transcend them through denial.
- Physical health sits beneath nearly every other aspiration because illness collapses attention onto survival.
- Naval distinguishes between the pursuit of peace and the practical conditions that make that pursuit less punishing.

## Key Quotes

> You're not going to buy your way to happiness, but you will buy your way out of common causes of unhappiness.

> Happiness without material comfort is playing on hard mode.

> A sick man only wants one thing. A healthy man wants 10,000 things.
