## Summary

Naval presents David Deutsch's worldview as radical rational optimism. The core claim is that reality is comprehensible, problems are soluble, and the growth of knowledge has no fixed endpoint, which means human progress can continue indefinitely as long as we keep generating and correcting explanations.

## Key Insights

- The book is framed as a worldview centered on good explanations rather than authority or convention.
- Human beings matter because they are universal explainers capable of understanding whatever is knowable.
- Progress is open-ended because every theory can be improved and no final resting point is available.
- Material wealth is treated as the set of physical transformations made possible by knowledge.
- Optimism here is not naive cheerfulness but confidence in problem-solving and criticism.

## Key Quotes

> We're at the beginning of an infinity of knowledge.

> Reality is comprehensible.

> Problems are soluble.
