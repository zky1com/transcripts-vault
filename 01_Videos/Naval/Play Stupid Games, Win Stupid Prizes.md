## Summary

Naval warns that competition can trap founders into fighting over the wrong objective entirely. When rivalry becomes the focus, people often miss adjacent opportunities that were truer to customer needs and more valuable than winning the original contested space.

## Key Insights

- Competition can create tunnel vision that hides better nearby moves.
- Authentic founders are less likely to be trapped by rivalry because they stay attached to the real mission.
- Some markets are winner-take-all, but even then the exact battlefield is often more flexible than it first appears.
- Strategic pivots are harder to see when ego is invested in beating a specific opponent.
- Naval's early review-site example shows how booby prizes can absorb years of effort while the real opportunity sits adjacent.

## Key Quotes

> Play stupid games, win stupid prizes.

> Sometimes you need to take that one step, and you're not going to be able to take it if you're busy fighting over a booby prize.

> We should have been looking at what the consumer really wanted.
