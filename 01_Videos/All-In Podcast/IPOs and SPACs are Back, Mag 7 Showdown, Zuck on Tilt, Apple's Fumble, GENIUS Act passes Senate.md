## Summary

This episode captures the sense that both capital markets and big-tech competition are broadening again. IPOs and SPACs returning, Mag 7 dispersion, and the GENIUS Act all suggest a world in which the old mega-cap consensus is fragmenting and more of the action is shifting back into structure, product, and policy details.

## Key Insights

- The AI talent market has become so competitive that compensation itself is now a strategic weapon.
- Product distribution and installed surfaces may matter as much as underlying model quality in determining winners.
- Crypto and stablecoin legislation are moving from speculative side-show to first-order market variable.
