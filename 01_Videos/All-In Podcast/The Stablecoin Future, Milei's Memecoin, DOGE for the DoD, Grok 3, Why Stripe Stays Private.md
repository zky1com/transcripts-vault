## Summary

This episode is a dense market-and-policy mix centered on what becomes infrastructure and what stays speculation. Stablecoins, private-company strategy, defense efficiency, and frontier model competition are all treated as tests of where real utility is emerging versus where narrative is running ahead of substance.

## Key Insights

- Stablecoins are discussed as payment infrastructure rather than just another crypto trade.
- The Milei memecoin segment serves as a contrast case for how quickly speculation can outrun credibility.
- Stripe staying private is framed as a strategic choice enabled by strong internal compounding and reduced need for public-market signaling.
- The Grok and DOGE segments continue the show's habit of linking technical capability to distribution and political execution.
