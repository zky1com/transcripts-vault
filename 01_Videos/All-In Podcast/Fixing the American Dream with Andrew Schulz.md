## Summary

Andrew Schulz helps the hosts translate economic frustration into cultural language ordinary people actually use. Housing, wages, crypto, and class mobility are all discussed through the lens of whether the American Dream still feels reachable to people who are not already inside the winner's circle.

## Key Insights

- Affordability is framed as a lived experience problem, not a spreadsheet problem.
- Crypto adoption is partly explained as an attempt to find an alternative wealth ladder when traditional ladders feel blocked.
- Comedy conversation works here as a sentiment detector for public trust and frustration.

## Key Quotes

> The American Dream has to feel reachable again.

> People move to crypto when the normal ladder feels closed.

> You cannot slogan your way out of affordability.
